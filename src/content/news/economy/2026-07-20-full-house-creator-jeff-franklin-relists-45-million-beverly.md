## Jeff Franklin Relists Beverly Hills Mansion Tied to Manson Murders

Jeff Franklin, the creator of the iconic television series "Full House," has put his Beverly Hills mansion back on the market for $45 million. This luxurious property, however, carries a dark historical significance, as it was built on the site of the infamous Charles Manson murders that took place in 1969.

### A Notable Property with a Troubling Past

The mansion, located in the exclusive neighborhood of Beverly Hills, was constructed in 1990. It occupies a site that was previously home to the residence of actress Sharon Tate, who was tragically murdered by followers of Charles Manson. The house was demolished in 1994, and Franklin's property was built in its place, but the shadow of its past continues to linger.

Despite its luxurious amenities and prime location, the mansion's history has made it a subject of intrigue and controversy. Franklin initially purchased the property in 2018 for approximately $4.2 million, and after extensive renovations, he has now decided to relist it at a significantly higher price.

### Features of the Mansion

The mansion boasts a range of high-end features that cater to luxury living. Spanning over 10,000 square feet, it includes seven bedrooms, ten bathrooms, and a host of amenities such as a home theater, gym, and an expansive outdoor area with a pool. The property is designed to offer both comfort and opulence, making it an attractive option for potential buyers looking for a prestigious residence in one of the most sought-after areas in Los Angeles.

### Market Dynamics and Challenges

The real estate market in Beverly Hills has seen fluctuations in recent years, with luxury properties often facing unique challenges. While the allure of celebrity ownership can attract buyers, the historical significance of Franklin's mansion may deter some. The connection to the Manson murders adds a layer of complexity to the sale, as potential buyers weigh the property's luxurious offerings against its grim past.

Real estate experts suggest that the current market dynamics may favor sellers, but properties with controversial histories can be harder to move. Franklin's decision to relist the mansion at a premium price indicates his confidence in the property's appeal, despite its history.

### Conclusion

As Jeff Franklin seeks a buyer for his Beverly Hills mansion, the intersection of luxury real estate and historical significance remains a focal point. The mansion not only represents a substantial financial investment but also serves as a reminder of a tragic chapter in Hollywood's history. Whether the property can find a buyer willing to overlook its past remains to be seen, but its unique story continues to attract attention in the competitive Los Angeles real estate market.
