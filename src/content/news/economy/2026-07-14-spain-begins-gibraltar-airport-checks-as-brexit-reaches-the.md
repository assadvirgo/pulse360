## Spain Implements Airport Checks in Gibraltar as Brexit Transitions

As the implications of Brexit continue to unfold, Spain has initiated airport checks at Gibraltar International Airport, marking a significant shift in the status of the British Overseas Territory. Starting Wednesday, Gibraltar will effectively become part of the Schengen zone, a move that introduces new regulations and procedures for travelers between Spain and Gibraltar.

## Background on Brexit and Gibraltar

The United Kingdom's departure from the European Union in January 2020 set off a series of negotiations and adjustments that have impacted various territories, including Gibraltar. This small, strategically located peninsula at the southern tip of Spain has long been a point of contention between the UK and Spain. With Brexit, the dynamics of border control and immigration have become increasingly complex.

Gibraltar has historically enjoyed a unique status, allowing for relatively free movement between the territory and Spain. However, the transition to the Schengen zone means that travelers will now face more stringent checks, aligning with the EU's border management protocols.

## The New Entry System

The new entry system, which will be implemented at Gibraltar airport, is part of Spain's broader efforts to manage its borders post-Brexit. Under the Schengen Agreement, member states have agreed to abolish passport and other types of border control at their mutual borders, but external borders are subject to stricter checks. As a result, passengers traveling from Gibraltar to Spain will now need to comply with these regulations.

Officials in Spain have indicated that the new checks are necessary to ensure security and proper immigration control. This development has raised concerns among residents and businesses in Gibraltar, who fear that the increased scrutiny could hinder the free flow of people and goods that has characterized the region for decades.

## Reactions from Gibraltar

The government of Gibraltar has expressed its apprehension regarding the new measures. Chief Minister Fabian Picardo has emphasized the importance of maintaining a close relationship with Spain while also safeguarding the interests of Gibraltar's residents. He has called for dialogue with Spanish authorities to address potential issues arising from the new entry system.

Local businesses, particularly those reliant on cross-border trade and tourism, are also voicing concerns. The fear is that longer wait times and more complex procedures could deter visitors and impact economic activity in the territory.

## Looking Ahead

As the new entry system takes effect, it remains to be seen how both Spain and Gibraltar will navigate this transition. The introduction of airport checks is a pivotal moment in the ongoing evolution of Gibraltar's relationship with the EU and the UK post-Brexit.

Both parties will likely need to engage in continued discussions to find a balance that ensures security while facilitating the movement of people and goods. The situation is fluid, and stakeholders in Gibraltar are closely monitoring developments as they unfold.

In summary, the implementation of airport checks at Gibraltar International Airport signifies a new chapter in the region's history, one that will require careful management and cooperation between Spain and Gibraltar to ensure a smooth transition in the wake of Brexit.
