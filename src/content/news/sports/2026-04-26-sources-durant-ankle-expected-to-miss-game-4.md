## Kevin Durant Expected to Miss Game 4 Due to Ankle Injury

In a significant development for the Houston Rockets, star forward Kevin Durant is anticipated to miss Game 4 of the Western Conference quarterfinal series against the Los Angeles Lakers. This news comes after Durant underwent further testing, which revealed a bone bruise in his sprained left ankle.

### Injury Details

Durant's injury occurred during the third game of the series, raising concerns among fans and analysts alike about the impact it could have on the Rockets' playoff aspirations. The team has been heavily reliant on Durant's scoring ability and experience, making his absence particularly concerning as they face a formidable opponent in the Lakers.

Sources close to the situation have indicated that Durant's recovery will be closely monitored, but the decision to hold him out of Game 4 reflects a cautious approach to ensure he does not exacerbate the injury. The Rockets are currently evaluating their options and strategies in light of Durant's expected absence.

### Implications for the Rockets

The Rockets find themselves in a challenging position as they navigate the playoffs without one of their key players. Durant has been instrumental in the team's success throughout the season, and his scoring prowess, defensive skills, and leadership on the court will be sorely missed. 

In his absence, the team will need to rely on other players to step up and fill the void. This could provide an opportunity for younger players to gain valuable playoff experience, but it also places additional pressure on the remaining starters to elevate their performance.

### Looking Ahead

As the Rockets prepare for Game 4, the coaching staff will need to devise a game plan that leverages the strengths of the available roster. The matchup against the Lakers is crucial, and with the series tied, every game becomes increasingly important.
