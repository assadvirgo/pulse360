## Olise Shines with Hat-Trick in France's Victory Over Northern Ireland

In a compelling display of skill and determination, Bayern Munich's Michael Olise scored a hat-trick, leading France to a 3-1 victory over Northern Ireland in their final warm-up match ahead of the World Cup. The match took place at Stade Pierre-Mauroy in Lille, where Olise's performance not only secured the win but also showcased his potential as a key player for the national team.

From the outset, France demonstrated their dominance on the field. The team, under the guidance of head coach Didier Deschamps, was eager to finalize their preparations for the upcoming World Cup. With a mix of experienced players and emerging talent, France aimed to refine their strategies and build momentum ahead of the tournament.

Olise, who has been making waves in the Bundesliga, quickly established himself as the standout player of the match. His first goal came in the 15th minute, where he capitalized on a defensive error by Northern Ireland, slotting the ball past the goalkeeper with precision. This early goal set the tone for the remainder of the match and energized the French squad.

### Olise's Hat-Trick

The 21-year-old forward continued to impress, doubling the lead just before halftime. His second goal highlighted his technical ability and composure in front of goal, as he navigated through the defense to find the back of the net. By the time he completed his hat-trick in the 70th minute, it was clear that Olise was in exceptional form. His third goal was a powerful strike from outside the box, leaving the Northern Irish goalkeeper with little chance to respond.

Northern Ireland managed to pull one back in the 82nd minute through a well-placed header, but it was too little, too late. France's defense held firm, ensuring that Olise's remarkable performance would be the defining feature of the match.
