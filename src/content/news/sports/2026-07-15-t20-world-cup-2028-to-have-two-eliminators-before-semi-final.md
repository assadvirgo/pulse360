## T20 World Cup 2028 to Feature Two 'Eliminators' Prior to Semi-Finals

The International Cricket Council (ICC) has announced significant changes to the format of the T20 World Cup 2028, set to take place in the United States and the West Indies. In a move aimed at enhancing the competitive nature of the tournament, the ICC will introduce two 'eliminators' before the semi-finals, alongside a revised group structure.

### Revised Group Structure

One of the most notable changes in the tournament format is the shift from four groups of five teams to five groups of four teams. This adjustment is expected to increase the intensity of the group stage, as each match will carry more weight, with fewer teams competing in each group. The top two teams from each group will progress to the next stage, along with the four best third-placed teams, leading to a total of 20 teams advancing to the eliminator rounds.

### Introduction of Eliminators

The introduction of two eliminators marks a significant shift in how the tournament will unfold. These matches will serve as a knockout stage, providing teams that finish third in their respective groups an additional opportunity to qualify for the semi-finals. This format aims to keep fans engaged and maintain a high level of competition throughout the tournament, as teams will need to perform consistently well to secure their place in the later stages.

### Impact on Teams and Fans

The ICC's decision to implement these changes comes in response to feedback from players, teams, and fans who have expressed a desire for a more dynamic and unpredictable tournament structure. By allowing more teams a chance to compete for the semi-finals, the ICC hopes to create an environment where every match is crucial, enhancing the overall excitement of the tournament.

### Looking Ahead

As the cricketing world prepares for the T20 World Cup 2028, anticipation is building not only for the new format but also for the venues and the potential for cricket to expand its reach in North America. The tournament is expected to draw significant attention, with the ICC aiming to attract new fans and participants to the sport in a region where cricket is still developing.

In conclusion, the T20 World Cup 2028 promises to be an innovative and thrilling event, with its new format designed to elevate the level of competition and engagement. As teams gear up for this prestigious tournament, the cricketing community will be watching closely to see how these changes impact the dynamics of the game on the global stage.
