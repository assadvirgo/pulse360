## Mystics Triumph in Historic Four-Overtime Thriller Against Fire

In an extraordinary display of endurance and skill, the Washington Mystics outlasted the Portland Fire in a gripping four-overtime match on Sunday, securing a 124-123 victory. This nail-biting encounter not only showcased the talents of both teams but also tied the record for the longest game in WNBA history.

### A Career-Defining Performance

Sonia Citron emerged as the standout player of the match, scoring a career-high 32 points. Her performance was pivotal, particularly in the closing moments of the game. With just 21.4 seconds remaining, Citron hit a crucial basket that allowed the Mystics to take the lead for good. Her ability to perform under pressure was instrumental in the team's success, as she consistently found ways to score and support her teammates throughout the extended match.

### A Battle of Resilience

The game was a test of resilience for both teams, as they exchanged leads multiple times over the course of the four overtimes. The intensity of the match was palpable, with each possession becoming increasingly critical. Fans were treated to a showcase of high-stakes basketball, where both teams demonstrated their determination and skill.

The Fire, despite the loss, put up a formidable fight. Their players rallied to match the Mystics' intensity, making the game a memorable spectacle. The back-and-forth nature of the contest kept spectators on the edge of their seats, highlighting the competitive spirit that defines the WNBA.

### Historical Significance

This match not only serves as a testament to the capabilities of the players involved but also marks a significant moment in WNBA history. Tying the record for the longest game, this four-overtime showdown adds to the league's legacy of thrilling and competitive basketball. The previous record was set during a game in 2020, and the Mystics and Fire have now etched their names alongside that historic encounter.
