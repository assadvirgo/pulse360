## Balogun Scores and Sees Red as USA Advances Past Bosnia-Herzegovina

In a pivotal match held in Santa Clara, California, the United States men's national soccer team secured a decisive victory against Bosnia-Herzegovina, winning 2-0 and advancing to the last 16 of the World Cup. Folarin Balogun played a significant role in the match, scoring the opening goal before receiving a red card later in the game.

### A Strong Start for the U.S. Team

The U.S. team entered the match with high expectations, aiming to solidify their position in the tournament. From the outset, they displayed a commanding presence on the field, demonstrating both skill and determination. Balogun, who has been a standout player throughout the tournament, opened the scoring in the 35th minute, capitalizing on a defensive error by the Bosnian side. His well-placed shot found the back of the net, igniting cheers from the home crowd.

The first half saw the U.S. maintain control, with several opportunities to extend their lead. However, Bosnia-Herzegovina's defense managed to hold firm for the remainder of the half, leaving the score at 1-0 as the teams headed into the locker rooms.

### Second Half Tensions

The second half began with Bosnia-Herzegovina attempting to mount a comeback. Their efforts were met with a resolute U.S. defense, which effectively neutralized their attacks. In the 60th minute, the U.S. doubled their lead when Weston McKennie scored with a powerful header from a corner kick, further solidifying their dominance in the match.

However, the game took a turn for the worse for Balogun in the 75th minute. After a controversial challenge that resulted in a foul, the referee issued a second yellow card, leading to his dismissal from the match. The decision sparked discussions among fans and analysts alike, as many questioned the severity of the second booking.

Despite being reduced to ten men, the U.S. team managed to maintain their composure and control the game until the final whistle. The victory not only secures their place in the knockout stages but also boosts team morale as they prepare for tougher challenges ahead.
