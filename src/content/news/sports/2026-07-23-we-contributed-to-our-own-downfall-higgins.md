## Coleraine Manager Reflects on Heavy Defeat in UEFA Conference League

In a candid assessment following a challenging match, Coleraine manager Ruaidhrí Higgins expressed regret over his team's performance in their recent UEFA Conference League encounter. The team suffered a significant 5-0 defeat against HJK Helsinki in the first leg of the second qualifying round, prompting Higgins to acknowledge the role his players played in the outcome.

### Acknowledging Mistakes

After the match, Higgins stated, "We contributed to our own downfall," highlighting the critical errors that led to the lopsided scoreline. The manager's comments reflect a sense of accountability and a desire for improvement as the team prepares for the return leg of the tie. The defeat marks a challenging moment for Coleraine, who had hoped to advance further in European competition.

### Tactical Analysis

Higgins pointed to specific areas of concern that contributed to the defeat, including defensive lapses and missed opportunities in attack. The manager noted that the team struggled to maintain possession and failed to capitalize on key moments, allowing HJK to dominate the match. "We need to learn from this experience and ensure we are better prepared for the next game," he added, emphasizing the need for tactical adjustments.

### Looking Ahead

As Coleraine gears up for the return leg, Higgins remains optimistic about the possibility of a turnaround. "We have to regroup and focus on what we can control," he stated. The manager is likely to implement changes in strategy and personnel in hopes of reversing the outcome in front of their home supporters.

The upcoming match will be crucial not only for the team's aspirations in the tournament but also for morale as they aim to bounce back from this setback. Higgins' leadership will be pivotal in guiding the players through this challenging period, fostering resilience and determination.

### Conclusion

The defeat against HJK serves as a stark reminder of the challenges facing teams in European competitions. As Coleraine reflects on their performance, the focus will shift to learning from mistakes and preparing for the next opportunity to compete. With the right adjustments and a renewed sense of purpose, the team hopes to turn the tide in the second leg of the qualifying round.
