## The FTD Brothers: Racing Against Time to Combat Dementia

In a poignant and inspiring endeavor, brothers Jordan and Cian Adams, known as the "FTD Brothers," are gearing up for a remarkable challenge that combines athleticism with a deeply personal mission. Following the 2026 London Marathon, where Jordan plans to run while carrying a 25kg fridge, the duo will undertake a grueling sprint across all 32 counties of Ireland in just 32 days. This ambitious undertaking is not only a test of endurance but also a heartfelt tribute to their late mother, Geraldine, who succumbed to a rare form of dementia at the age of 52.

### A Personal Journey

The Adams brothers are motivated by their own family history with dementia, particularly Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD), a condition that has significantly impacted their lives. Both Jordan and Cian have tested positive for the same genetic mutation associated with FTD, which raises their risk of developing symptoms as they age. This shared genetic fate has propelled them to take action, not only for their own futures but also in the hope of raising awareness and funds for dementia research.

### The Challenge Ahead

The upcoming marathon and subsequent county sprint represent a monumental physical challenge. Jordan's unique decision to run with a fridge, a symbol of the burdens that come with caring for someone with dementia, aims to draw attention to the struggles faced by families dealing with the condition. The brothers plan to cover approximately 1,500 kilometers across Ireland, a feat that requires not only physical stamina but also meticulous planning and support from their community.

### Raising Awareness and Funds

Through their efforts, the FTD Brothers hope to raise significant funds for dementia research, specifically targeting advancements in understanding and treating FTD. Their campaign emphasizes the importance of early detection and the need for increased funding for research initiatives. The brothers have expressed their desire to inspire others facing similar challenges, highlighting that while the journey may be fraught with difficulties, there is hope in community support and scientific progress.

### The Broader Impact of Dementia

Dementia, particularly forms like FTD, affects millions worldwide, creating profound challenges for both patients and their families. The Adams brothers’ initiative shines a light on the urgent need for research and resources dedicated to understanding these conditions. By sharing their story and undertaking this remarkable challenge, they aim to foster a broader conversation about dementia, its impact, and the importance of finding effective treatments.

### Conclusion

As Jordan and Cian Adams prepare for their ambitious journey across Ireland, they embody resilience and hope in the face of personal adversity. Their commitment to raising awareness and funds for dementia research reflects a growing recognition of the need for action against this debilitating disease. The FTD Brothers not only honor their mother’s memory but also inspire a movement towards understanding and combating dementia, reminding us all of the power of determination and community in the face of daunting challenges.
