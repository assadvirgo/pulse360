## Sam Monaghan to Start for Ireland in Six Nations Finale Against Scotland

In a strategic move ahead of the concluding match of the Six Nations Championship, Ireland has announced that second row Sam Monaghan will make her first start of the tournament. This adjustment marks the only change to Ireland's starting XV as they prepare to face Scotland at the Aviva Stadium on Sunday.

### Monaghan's Inclusion

Monaghan's selection comes as a significant development for the Irish team, reflecting the coaching staff's confidence in her abilities. The 24-year-old player has been a notable presence in the squad, and her inclusion in the starting lineup is expected to bolster Ireland's performance in the crucial matchup against Scotland.

The decision to start Monaghan indicates a tactical shift aimed at enhancing the team's physical presence in the second row. Her experience and skill set will be vital as Ireland seeks to secure a strong finish in the tournament.

### Context of the Match

The upcoming game against Scotland is pivotal for Ireland, as it represents their final opportunity to make an impact in the Six Nations Championship. With the stakes high, the team is focused on delivering a strong performance to conclude the tournament on a positive note.

Ireland has had a challenging campaign, and the match against Scotland will be an opportunity to demonstrate growth and resilience. The team will be looking to build on their previous performances and capitalize on their home advantage at the Aviva Stadium.

### The Significance of the Six Nations Championship

The Six Nations Championship is one of the premier international rugby competitions, featuring teams from England, France, Ireland, Italy, Scotland, and Wales. Each year, the tournament garners significant attention from fans and media alike, showcasing some of the best talent in women's rugby.

As Ireland prepares for this important clash, the focus will not only be on the result but also on the development of the players and the overall growth of women's rugby in the country. The inclusion of players like Monaghan in starting roles is indicative of the evolving landscape of the sport, where young talent is increasingly given the opportunity to shine on the international stage.

### Conclusion

As the match day approaches, all eyes will be on the Aviva Stadium to see how Sam Monaghan and the rest of the Irish team perform against Scotland. With a blend of experience and youthful energy, Ireland aims to finish the Six Nations Championship with a strong showing, setting the tone for future competitions and the continued growth of women's rugby in Ireland.
