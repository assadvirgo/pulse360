## Champions League Knockout Rounds: Key Information and Dates

The UEFA Champions League, one of the most prestigious tournaments in club football, is set to enter its knockout stages. As teams from across Europe vie for the coveted trophy, fans and analysts alike are eager to understand the format, key dates, and potential matchups that lie ahead.

### Overview of the Knockout Stages

The knockout rounds of the Champions League follow the group stage, where teams compete in a round-robin format. The top two teams from each of the eight groups advance to the Round of 16. This stage is characterized by two-legged ties, meaning each matchup consists of home and away games, with the aggregate score determining which team progresses to the quarter-finals.

### Key Dates to Remember

The knockout phase is scheduled to unfold as follows:

- **Round of 16 Draw**: The draw for the Round of 16 will take place on December 18, 2023. This event will determine the matchups for the first knockout round.
  
- **Round of 16 Matches**: The first legs will be held on February 13-14 and February 20-21, 2024, while the second legs are scheduled for March 5-6 and March 12-13, 2024.

- **Quarter-Finals and Semi-Finals**: The quarter-final draw will occur on March 15, 2024, with the quarter-finals set for April 9-10 and April 16-17. The semi-finals will follow on April 30 and May 1, with the second legs on May 7-8.

- **Final**: The climax of the tournament will take place on June 1, 2024, at Wembley Stadium in London, where the two finalists will compete for the title.

### Format and Regulations

The knockout rounds are governed by specific regulations that enhance the competitive nature of the tournament. In the event of a tie on aggregate score after both legs, the away goals rule is no longer in effect. Instead, matches will proceed to extra time and, if necessary, a penalty shootout to determine the winner.
