## Ben Stokes: A Look Back at England's 2019 World Cup Triumph

As Ben Stokes announces his retirement from Test cricket, the cricketing world reflects on his remarkable contributions to the sport, particularly his pivotal role in England's historic victory at the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup. This tournament marked a significant milestone for England, as it was their first win in the 50-over format, a feat that has since become a cornerstone of the nation's cricketing legacy.

The 2019 World Cup, hosted by England and Wales, was a tournament filled with high expectations and intense competition. England entered the tournament as one of the favorites, buoyed by a strong squad and a dynamic playing style. Throughout the group stages, the team demonstrated resilience and skill, ultimately finishing in the top four and securing a place in the knockout rounds.

Stokes, who had already established himself as a key player for England, rose to prominence during the knockout phase. His performances were characterized by a blend of aggression and composure, qualities that would become crucial in the final match.

### The Final Showdown

The World Cup final, held at Lord's on July 14, 2019, was a historic encounter against New Zealand. The match was a nail-biter, with both teams displaying exceptional cricketing prowess. England, chasing a target of 242 runs, found themselves in a precarious position at various stages of the innings. It was in this moment of crisis that Stokes showcased his extraordinary talent.

Stokes played a match-winning innings, scoring 84 runs off 98 balls. His ability to remain calm under pressure was evident as he guided England through the latter stages of the chase. In a dramatic turn of events, the match ended in a tie, leading to a Super Over — a first in World Cup history. Stokes' contributions were instrumental not only in the regular innings but also in the Super Over, where England ultimately triumphed based on the boundary count rule.
