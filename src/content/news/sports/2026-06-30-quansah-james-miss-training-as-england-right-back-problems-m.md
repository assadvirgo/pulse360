## Quansah and James Miss Training as England Faces Right-Back Dilemma

As England prepares for their crucial World Cup last-32 match against the Democratic Republic of Congo on Wednesday, concerns are mounting regarding the team's right-back position. Both Jarell Quansah and Reece James were absent from training sessions, raising questions about their availability for the upcoming match.

### Injury Concerns

Jarell Quansah, who has been a standout performer for England in the tournament thus far, is reportedly dealing with a minor injury that has kept him off the training pitch. His absence is particularly troubling for the England coaching staff, as Quansah has shown significant promise in his role, contributing not only defensively but also in attack.

Reece James, a seasoned player with vast international experience, is also facing fitness issues. The Chelsea defender has had a history of injuries that have sidelined him in critical moments, and his current situation is no different. His ability to provide both defensive solidity and offensive support from the right flank makes his potential absence a significant blow to the team's tactical setup.

### Impact on Team Strategy

With both players missing training, England's coaching staff will need to consider alternative options for the right-back position. The team has depth in this area, but the loss of two key players could force a reshuffle that may impact overall team cohesion. 

Coaches will likely look to other squad members who can step into the right-back role. Players such as Kyle Walker or Kieran Trippier could be called upon to fill the void, but both have their own strengths and weaknesses that may alter England's game plan against DR Congo. 

### Upcoming Match Against DR Congo

The match against DR Congo is pivotal for England, as they aim to advance further in the tournament. The team has shown flashes of brilliance in their previous matches, but consistency has been a challenge. The right-back position is crucial, as it not only involves defensive responsibilities but also contributes significantly to the team's attacking play.

Head coach Gareth Southgate will need to make strategic decisions in light of these injuries. The absence of Quansah and James could lead to a more conservative approach, focusing on solidifying the defense while relying on the midfield and forwards to create scoring opportunities.

### Conclusion

As the World Cup progresses, the pressure on England to perform intensifies. The missing presence of Jarell Quansah and Reece James in training is a cause for concern, but it also presents an opportunity for other players to step up and showcase their abilities on the world stage. As the team prepares for their critical match against DR Congo, all eyes will be on the coaching staff's decisions and the players who will take the field in their stead.
