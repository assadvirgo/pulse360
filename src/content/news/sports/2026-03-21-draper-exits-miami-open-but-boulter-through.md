## Draper Exits Miami Open While Boulter Advances

The Miami Open has seen a mix of outcomes for British tennis players, as Jack Draper faced a disappointing exit in the second round, while Katie Boulter advanced to the next stage due to her opponent's injury.

### Draper Knocked Out by Opelka

Jack Draper, the British number two, was eliminated from the tournament after a challenging match against American player Reilly Opelka. Draper, who had shown promise in earlier rounds, struggled to find his rhythm against the towering Opelka, who is known for his powerful serve and aggressive play style. The match concluded with a decisive scoreline that reflected Opelka's dominance on the court.

Draper's exit marks a setback in his campaign at the Miami Open, where he had hoped to build on his recent performances. The young Brit has been making strides in his career, but this loss serves as a reminder of the competitive nature of the ATP Tour, particularly in high-stakes tournaments like the Miami Open.

### Boulter Advances Amidst Injury Withdrawal

In contrast to Draper’s fate, Katie Boulter has successfully progressed to the next round of the Miami Open. Boulter faced Danish player Clara Tauson, who was forced to retire from the match due to an injury. The circumstances surrounding Tauson’s withdrawal were unfortunate, as both players were poised to deliver an exciting contest.

Boulter's advancement is a positive development for British tennis, especially as she continues to gain experience in high-pressure situations. Her ability to capitalize on the opportunity presented by Tauson's retirement underscores her resilience and determination to succeed in the tournament.
