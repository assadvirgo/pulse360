## Jess Warner-Judd Sees London Marathon 2026 as a 'Second Chance'

In a recent interview with BBC Sport, four-time British champion Jess Warner-Judd expressed her optimism regarding the upcoming London Marathon in 2026, referring to the event as a "second chance" in her running career. This sentiment comes in the wake of a significant health challenge she faced during the 2024 European Championship, where she suffered a seizure while competing in the 10,000 meter final.

### A Challenging Journey

Warner-Judd's journey has been marked by both triumph and adversity. The seizure during the championship race was a pivotal moment for the athlete, leading to a diagnosis of epilepsy. This condition not only posed immediate risks to her health but also raised questions about her future in competitive athletics. 

In her statements, Warner-Judd emphasized the emotional and physical toll that the incident took on her. "It was a frightening experience," she remarked, reflecting on the moments leading up to the seizure and the subsequent uncertainty regarding her athletic career. The diagnosis required her to reassess her training and competition plans, as she navigated the complexities of managing her health while remaining committed to her sport.

### Looking Ahead to 2026

Despite the challenges, Warner-Judd remains resolute in her pursuit of excellence. The London Marathon, scheduled for 2026, represents not only a significant event in the running calendar but also a personal milestone for the athlete. "I see it as an opportunity to prove to myself that I can overcome this," she stated, highlighting her determination to return to competitive form.

The London Marathon is renowned for its vibrant atmosphere and the support it garners from spectators and fellow athletes alike. Warner-Judd's participation is anticipated to inspire many, particularly those who may be facing their own health challenges. Her journey serves as a reminder of the resilience required in sports and life, illustrating how athletes can triumph over adversity.

### Community Support and Awareness

Warner-Judd's situation has also shed light on the importance of awareness surrounding epilepsy and the challenges that athletes with health conditions face. The support from the running community, as well as initiatives aimed at raising awareness about epilepsy, have been crucial in her recovery process. She has expressed gratitude for the encouragement she has received from fans and fellow athletes, which has played a significant role in her mental and emotional well-being.

As the marathon approaches, Warner-Judd is focused on her training regimen, working closely with her medical team to ensure that she is in optimal condition. Her journey toward the 2026 London Marathon is not merely about competition; it is also about personal growth and the pursuit of a dream that she refuses to relinquish.

### Conclusion

Jess Warner-Judd's story is one of resilience and hope. As she prepares for the London Marathon in 2026, her experience serves as an inspiration to many, illustrating the power of determination in the face of adversity. With her sights set on the future, Warner-Judd is poised to make a significant impact, both on the track and in the lives of those who follow her journey.
