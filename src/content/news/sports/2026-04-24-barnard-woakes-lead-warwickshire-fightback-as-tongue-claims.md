In a gripping encounter at Edgbaston, Warwickshire's cricket team showcased remarkable resilience as they fought back against Nottinghamshire, thanks to the stellar performances of skipper Will Rhodes and Chris Woakes. The match, which saw Nottinghamshire initially gain the upper hand, turned in Warwickshire's favor as they rallied to post a competitive total.

The day began with Nottinghamshire's bowlers making early inroads, putting Warwickshire on the back foot. The visitors capitalized on their momentum, claiming crucial wickets that threatened to derail Warwickshire's innings. However, the team's captain, Will Rhodes, emerged as a beacon of hope amidst the turmoil. His steadfast performance at the crease was pivotal in stabilizing the innings.

### Rhodes' Unbeaten Century

Rhodes not only steadied the ship but also propelled Warwickshire into a stronger position by scoring an unbeaten century. His innings was marked by patience and precision, as he navigated through the challenging conditions posed by Nottinghamshire's bowling attack. Rhodes' century not only boosted his team's morale but also provided a solid foundation for the middle order to build upon.

### Woakes' Support and Tongue's Five-Wicket Haul

In conjunction with Rhodes, Chris Woakes played a crucial role in the fightback.
