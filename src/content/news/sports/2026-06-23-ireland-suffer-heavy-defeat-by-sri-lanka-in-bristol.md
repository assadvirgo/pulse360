## Sri Lanka Secures Dominant Victory Over Ireland in World Cup Clash

In a decisive encounter at the ICC Women's Cricket World Cup, Sri Lanka delivered a commanding performance against Ireland, winning by nine wickets in Bristol. This victory not only bolstered Sri Lanka's chances of advancing to the semi-finals but also highlighted the team's growing confidence as the tournament progresses.

### Chamari Athapaththu Shines

The standout performer for Sri Lanka was Chamari Athapaththu, whose remarkable innings of 106 not out was pivotal in steering her team to victory. Athapaththu's century was characterized by a mix of aggressive stroke play and calculated shot selection, allowing her to dominate the Irish bowling attack. Her performance was instrumental in chasing down Ireland's target, showcasing her ability to perform under pressure.

### Ireland's Struggles with the Bat

Ireland, batting first, faced significant challenges throughout their innings. The team struggled to build partnerships and was ultimately restricted to a total that fell short of competitive standards. Despite some individual contributions, the batting lineup failed to capitalize on early opportunities, resulting in a total that proved insufficient against a determined Sri Lankan side.

Sri Lanka's bowlers executed their plans effectively, applying consistent pressure on the Irish batswomen.
