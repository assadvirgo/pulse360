## King and Campbell Steady West Indies in Pursuit of 302

In a match that appears to be heading towards a draw, West Indies openers Brandon King and Jeremy Campbell have approached their innings with patience as they chase a target of 302 runs set by Sri Lanka. The ongoing Test match has showcased a resilient batting display from the West Indies, who have navigated the challenges posed by Sri Lanka's seamers, including notable performances from Prabath Jayasuriya.

### A Steady Start

As the West Indies began their second innings, the openers were tasked with a formidable challenge. The pitch conditions, which had previously offered assistance to bowlers, seemed to settle, allowing King and Campbell to find their rhythm. Their partnership has been marked by careful shot selection and a focus on building a solid foundation, crucial for their ambitious chase.

### Sri Lanka's Bowling Efforts

Despite the West Indies' steady progress, Sri Lanka's bowlers, particularly the seamers, were unable to exert significant pressure. Jayasuriya, who has been a key figure in the Sri Lankan bowling attack, struggled to find the breakthrough that his team desperately needed. His inability to dismiss the openers early on has allowed King and Campbell to settle in, further complicating Sri Lanka's efforts to force a result.

### The Road Ahead

As the match progresses, the West Indies find themselves in a favorable position, with both openers looking increasingly comfortable at the crease. The target of 302 runs, while challenging, seems achievable if the pair can maintain their composure and continue to build their partnership. The focus now shifts to how long they can sustain their innings against a Sri Lankan attack that will be eager to make inroads.

### Implications for Both Teams

For the West Indies, a successful chase would mark a significant achievement and bolster their confidence in the longer format of the game. Conversely, for Sri Lanka, the inability to secure a win in this match could raise questions about their bowling effectiveness and strategies in high-pressure situations.

As the game heads towards its conclusion, the cricketing world watches closely, anticipating whether the West Indies can complete their chase or if Sri Lanka will find a way to salvage a draw from what has been a tightly contested encounter.
