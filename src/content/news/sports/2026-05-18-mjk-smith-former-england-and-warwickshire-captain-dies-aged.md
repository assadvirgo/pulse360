## MJK Smith, Former England and Warwickshire Captain, Passes Away at 92

MJK Smith, the former captain of the England cricket team and Warwickshire, has passed away at the age of 92. His death marks the end of an era for English cricket, as Smith was a significant figure in the sport, both on and off the field.

### A Dual Sportsman

Born in 1931, Smith's athletic prowess was not limited to cricket. He also made his mark in rugby union, earning an England cap as a fly-half. This dual talent in two major sports is a testament to his exceptional skills and versatility as an athlete. Smith's ability to excel in both cricket and rugby is a rare achievement, highlighting his commitment and dedication to sports.

### Cricket Career Highlights

Smith's cricketing career was distinguished, with a total of 50 Test matches played for England. He captained the national team in 25 of those matches, showcasing his leadership qualities and strategic acumen. Under his captaincy, England experienced notable successes, and he was respected not only for his skills as a batter but also for his tactical insights.

His tenure with Warwickshire was equally impressive. Smith was a key player for the county, contributing to their successes during a transformative period in English cricket. His performances helped elevate the status of Warwickshire within the domestic circuit, and he remains a celebrated figure in the club's history.
