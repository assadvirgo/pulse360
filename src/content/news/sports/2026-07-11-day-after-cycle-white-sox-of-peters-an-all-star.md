## Tristan Peters Named All-Star Following Historic Cycle

In a remarkable turn of events, Chicago White Sox outfielder Tristan Peters has been named to the American League All-Star team as an injury replacement just one day after achieving the rare feat of hitting for the cycle. This recognition comes as a significant milestone in Peters' burgeoning career.

### A Historic Achievement

On Friday, Peters showcased his exceptional talent by hitting for the cycle, a rare accomplishment in Major League Baseball that involves a player hitting a single, double, triple, and home run in the same game. This extraordinary performance not only captivated fans but also highlighted Peters' versatility and skill as a hitter. 

Peters' cycle was a testament to his ability to contribute offensively in multiple ways, demonstrating both speed and power. His performance on the field has drawn attention from fans and analysts alike, solidifying his place as a key player for the White Sox.

### All-Star Selection

The following day, the White Sox announced that Peters would be joining the American League All-Star team as an injury replacement. This selection underscores the impact of his recent performance and the potential he has shown throughout the season. The All-Star Game, set to take place in the coming weeks, will provide Peters with a platform to showcase his talents on a national stage alongside some of the league's best players.

Peters expressed his excitement about the selection, stating that it is an honor to be recognized among the elite in baseball. "It's a dream come true," he remarked, reflecting on the significance of being named an All-Star. "I just want to go out there and represent the White Sox and my teammates."
