## Key Questions for College Football Teams After Spring Ball

As college football programs across the United States wrap up their spring practices, several prominent teams, including Alabama, Georgia, BYU, and Oregon, find themselves facing critical questions that will shape their upcoming seasons. With the College Football Playoff (CFP) landscape becoming increasingly competitive, resolving these uncertainties is essential for teams aspiring to contend for a national title.

### Alabama: Quarterback Conundrum

Alabama, a perennial powerhouse, enters the summer with a significant question mark at the quarterback position. With the departure of last season's starter, the Crimson Tide must determine whether to rely on a seasoned backup or trust a promising newcomer. The decision could have far-reaching implications for the team's offensive strategy and overall performance in the upcoming season.

### Georgia: Defensive Depth Concerns

As the reigning national champions, Georgia's primary challenge lies in maintaining its defensive dominance. With several key players leaving for the NFL, the Bulldogs must assess their depth and identify emerging stars capable of stepping into pivotal roles. The coaching staff will need to evaluate whether the current roster can uphold the high standards that have defined Georgia's defense in recent years.

### BYU: Transitioning to Big 12 Competition

BYU faces a unique challenge as it transitions into the Big 12 Conference. The Cougars must adapt to a new level of competition while integrating new recruits into their system. Questions surrounding the team's ability to compete against established programs will be critical as they prepare for a more rigorous schedule. The upcoming months will be crucial for BYU to develop chemistry and identify its strengths.

### Oregon: Offensive Identity

Oregon's spring practices have raised questions about the team's offensive identity moving forward. With a new offensive coordinator at the helm, the Ducks must define their style of play and ensure that players are on board with the new system. Establishing a clear offensive strategy will be vital for Oregon as it aims to reclaim its status as a contender in the Pac-12.

### Other Notable Teams

In addition to these programs, several other teams are grappling with their own sets of questions. For instance, Michigan must address its quarterback situation following the departure of its starter, while Texas A&M seeks to find consistency after a tumultuous previous season. Each of these teams is at a crossroads, where the decisions made in the coming weeks could significantly impact their trajectories.

### Conclusion

As summer approaches, the uncertainty surrounding these college football programs underscores the importance of effective leadership and strategic planning. Coaches and players alike must work diligently to address these questions and solidify their positions ahead of the fall season. With the stakes higher than ever in college football, the answers to these pressing issues will be closely watched by fans and analysts alike. The upcoming months will be pivotal for these teams as they strive to position themselves for success in the 2023 season.
