## Late Goal Secures Vital Win for Leeds United Over Brighton

In a dramatic conclusion to their Premier League clash, Leeds United secured a crucial 1-0 victory against Brighton & Hove Albion, thanks to a last-minute goal from Dominic Calvert-Lewin. The match, held at Elland Road, marked a significant moment for both teams as they approach the final week of the season.

### A Tense Encounter

The match unfolded with both teams exhibiting a strong defensive posture, making it difficult for either side to create clear goal-scoring opportunities. Leeds, under the management of Fabian Hurzeler, aimed to maintain their position in the upper half of the table, while Brighton sought to bolster their hopes of qualifying for European competition next season.

As the clock ticked down, it appeared that the match would end in a stalemate. However, in the 96th minute, Calvert-Lewin found the back of the net, sending the home crowd into a frenzy. The goal not only secured three points for Leeds but also dealt a significant blow to Brighton's aspirations for a Europa League spot.

### Implications for Brighton

With this defeat, Brighton's chances of securing a place in European competition have been severely impacted. The team had been in contention for a Europa League position, but the loss leaves them with limited opportunities as they head into the final matches of the season. The pressure will now mount on Brighton to secure positive results in their remaining fixtures to keep their European hopes alive.

### Leeds United's Position

For Leeds, the victory is a testament to their resilience and determination as they strive to finish the season strongly. The win keeps them in 7th place in the Premier League standings, a position that could potentially lead to European qualification depending on the outcomes of the final matches across the league. Hurzeler's side will be looking to build on this momentum as they prepare for their concluding fixtures.

### Looking Ahead

As the Premier League season draws to a close, both teams face critical challenges. Leeds United will aim to solidify their standing and possibly secure a European berth, while Brighton must regroup quickly to salvage their aspirations for next season's European competitions. The final week promises to be an exciting conclusion to the season, with both teams having much at stake.

In summary, Leeds United's late winner not only highlights their competitive spirit but also significantly alters the landscape of the Premier League's European qualification race. As both teams prepare for their next matches, the implications of this encounter will resonate throughout the league.
