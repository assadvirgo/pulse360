## Family of Miller Gardner Files Wrongful Death Suit Against Costa Rican Resort

The family of Miller Gardner, the 14-year-old son of former New York Yankees outfielder Brett Gardner, has initiated a wrongful death lawsuit against a Costa Rican resort following the tragic death of their son due to carbon monoxide poisoning. The lawsuit, filed in a New York court, alleges negligence on the part of the resort, claiming that inadequate safety measures contributed to the incident.

### Background of the Incident

Miller Gardner was discovered unresponsive in his hotel room at the resort in Costa Rica last year. Subsequent investigations revealed that the cause of death was carbon monoxide poisoning, a silent yet deadly hazard often associated with faulty heating systems or poor ventilation. The family contends that the resort failed to provide a safe environment for its guests and neglected to ensure that proper safety protocols were in place.

### Allegations of Negligence

In the lawsuit, the Gardner family asserts that the resort did not adequately maintain its facilities, which they believe directly led to the tragic outcome. They argue that the resort should have been aware of the risks associated with carbon monoxide exposure and should have taken measures to prevent such a tragedy. The legal filing seeks damages for emotional distress, loss of companionship, and other related expenses incurred as a result of their son’s untimely death.

### Response from the Resort

As of now, the resort has not publicly commented on the lawsuit. Legal experts suggest that the resort may face significant scrutiny as the case unfolds. The allegations of negligence could lead to broader discussions about safety regulations in hospitality settings, particularly in tourist destinations where foreign visitors may be unaware of potential risks.

### Broader Implications

This case highlights the ongoing concerns regarding safety standards in resorts and hotels, especially in regions that attract international tourists. Carbon monoxide poisoning, while rare, can occur in any environment where gas appliances are used, and the implications of such incidents can be devastating for families. The Gardner family's lawsuit may prompt a reevaluation of safety protocols within the hospitality industry, as well as increased awareness among travelers regarding the potential hazards of carbon monoxide.

### Conclusion

The tragic death of Miller Gardner has not only affected his family but has also raised critical questions about the responsibilities of resorts in ensuring guest safety. As the wrongful death lawsuit progresses, it will likely draw attention to the need for stringent safety measures in the hospitality sector, potentially influencing future regulations and practices. The Gardner family's pursuit of justice serves as a poignant reminder of the importance of vigilance in maintaining safe environments for all guests.
