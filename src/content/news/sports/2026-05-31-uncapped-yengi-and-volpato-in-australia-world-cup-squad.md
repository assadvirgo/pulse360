## Uncapped Yengi and Volpato Selected for Australia’s World Cup Squad

In an exciting announcement for Australian football, head coach Graham Arnold has unveiled a 26-man squad for the upcoming FIFA World Cup, featuring two notable uncapped players: Tete Yengi and Cristian Volpato. This selection marks a significant moment for both players, who have the opportunity to represent Australia on the world stage.

### Tete Yengi: Rising Star from Livingston

Tete Yengi, currently playing for Livingston in the Scottish Premiership, has emerged as a promising talent in Australian football. The 23-year-old striker has garnered attention for his performances in the league, showcasing his ability to score and assist in crucial matches. His inclusion in the World Cup squad reflects the coaching staff's confidence in his potential to contribute to the national team’s success.

Yengi's journey to the national team has been marked by hard work and determination. Having previously played for the Australian Under-23 team, he has demonstrated his capabilities at various levels of competition. His selection is seen as a reward for his consistent performances and a testament to the depth of talent in Australian football.

### Cristian Volpato: A Dual Heritage Talent

Cristian Volpato, a former Italy Under-21 winger, is another exciting addition to the squad. The 19-year-old, who has been making waves in Serie A with AS Roma, has opted to represent Australia, a decision that has been welcomed by fans and analysts alike. Volpato's technical skills, pace, and creativity on the ball make him a valuable asset for the Australian side.

Volpato's background is particularly interesting, as he was born in Australia to Italian parents, allowing him to hold dual citizenship. This unique heritage has provided him with the opportunity to choose between two footballing nations, and his decision to don the green and gold jersey is seen as a significant boost for the Socceroos.

### A Young and Dynamic Squad

The inclusion of Yengi and Volpato is part of a broader strategy by Arnold to infuse the squad with youthful energy and talent. The 26-man roster also features a mix of experienced players who have previously represented Australia in major tournaments, ensuring a balance between youth and experience.

As Australia prepares for the World Cup, the coaching staff is focused on creating a competitive environment that fosters growth and development among the players. The presence of uncapped players like Yengi and Volpato signifies a commitment to building a team that can compete at the highest level while also looking towards the future.
