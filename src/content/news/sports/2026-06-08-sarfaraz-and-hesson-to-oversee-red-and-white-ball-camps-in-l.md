## Sarfaraz and Hesson to Lead Red and White-Ball Camps in Lahore

In a strategic move to enhance the performance of the national cricket team, Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) has appointed Sarfaraz Ahmed and Mike Hesson to oversee two separate training camps in Lahore. These camps are designed to prepare players for upcoming international fixtures, focusing on both red-ball and white-ball formats.

### Overview of the Camps

The red-ball camp, which commenced recently, features a squad of 22 players, including the national team's captain, Babar Azam. This camp is set to run until July 10, providing players with intensive training and practice sessions aimed at improving their skills in the longer format of the game. The emphasis will be on developing techniques, fitness, and mental resilience, which are crucial for success in Test cricket.

Conversely, the white-ball camp will host a larger group of 27 players and is scheduled to continue until September 18. This camp will focus on One Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 matches, which are increasingly popular among fans and crucial for maintaining competitive rankings. The white-ball format requires a different skill set, and the coaching staff will tailor their approach accordingly.

### Leadership and Coaching

Sarfaraz Ahmed, a veteran wicketkeeper-batsman with extensive experience in international cricket, is expected to bring valuable insights and leadership to the red-ball camp. His familiarity with the pressures of Test cricket will be instrumental in guiding younger players through the nuances of the format.

Mike Hesson, who has previously served as the head coach of the New Zealand national team, brings a wealth of experience in white-ball cricket. His expertise will be vital in preparing the players for the fast-paced nature of ODIs and T20s, as well as for the upcoming international series.

### Objectives and Expectations

The primary objective of these camps is to ensure that players are well-prepared for their respective formats, particularly with international series on the horizon. The PCB aims to foster a competitive environment that encourages skill development and teamwork among players. 

Both Sarfaraz and Hesson will focus on individual player assessments, identifying areas for improvement, and implementing strategies to enhance overall team performance. The PCB has expressed confidence that these camps will not only strengthen the squad but also contribute to the long-term success of Pakistan cricket.

### Conclusion

As the red and white-ball camps unfold in Lahore, the PCB remains committed to investing in the development of its players. With experienced coaches at the helm, the national team is poised to refine its skills and strategies, aiming for success in upcoming international competitions. The outcomes of these training sessions will be closely monitored, as they could play a significant role in shaping the future of Pakistan cricket.
