## Final NBA MVP and Rookie of the Year Straw Poll Results

As the NBA season approaches its climax, the race for the Most Valuable Player (MVP) and Rookie of the Year (ROY) awards is heating up. In a recent straw poll conducted by ESPN's Tim Bontemps, 100 NBA insiders weighed in on their predictions for these prestigious accolades. The results provide a snapshot of the current landscape as players make their final pushes for recognition.

### MVP Race: A Tight Contest

The MVP race this season has been characterized by intense competition, with several standout performances influencing voter opinions. Leading the charge is Shai Gilgeous-Alexander (SGA) of the Oklahoma City Thunder, who has been a dominant force on the court. His ability to score, facilitate, and lead his team has garnered significant attention, positioning him as a frontrunner for the award.

However, Victor Wembanyama, the highly touted rookie from the San Antonio Spurs, has been making a late-season surge that could potentially alter the voting landscape. Wembanyama's unique skill set and impact on games have sparked discussions among voters about whether he could unseat SGA in the final stretch of the season. His performances have not only showcased his talent but have also contributed to the Spurs' competitiveness in the league.

### Insights from the Poll

The straw poll results indicate that while SGA remains the favorite among voters, Wembanyama's recent performances have made a significant impression. Analysts suggest that if Wembanyama continues to excel in the final games of the season, he could sway undecided voters who are looking for a fresh narrative in the MVP conversation.

Other notable candidates in the MVP discussion include established stars such as Nikola Jokic and Giannis Antetokounmpo, who have consistently delivered exceptional performances throughout the season. Their experience and track records make them perennial contenders, but the current focus remains on the dynamic between SGA and Wembanyama.

### Rookie of the Year Contenders

In the race for Rookie of the Year, Wembanyama is also a leading candidate, with his exceptional skills and ability to impact games at a high level. The straw poll reflects a strong consensus among insiders that he is the frontrunner for this award as well. His combination of height, agility, and basketball IQ has set him apart from other rookies, making him a standout in a talented class.

Other rookies, such as Scoot Henderson and Amen Thompson, have also made significant contributions to their teams, but Wembanyama's unique talent has overshadowed their efforts. As the season concludes, the focus will be on whether Wembanyama can maintain his momentum and secure both the MVP and ROY titles.

### Conclusion

As the NBA regular season nears its end, the anticipation surrounding the MVP and Rookie of the Year awards continues to build. With Shai Gilgeous-Alexander holding a slight edge, Victor Wembanyama’s late push adds an intriguing layer to the narrative. Voter sentiment may shift in the coming weeks, making the final outcomes of these awards one of the most closely watched storylines in the league. As fans and analysts alike await the final results, the excitement of the NBA season remains palpable.
