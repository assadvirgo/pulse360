## Un-Nooh Secures Stunning Victory with Maximum Break in World Open Final

In a remarkable display of skill and composure, Thepchaiya Un-Nooh achieved a maximum break of 147 during the World Open final, leading to a surprising 10-7 victory over the renowned Ronnie O'Sullivan in Yushan, China. This match not only showcased Un-Nooh's talent but also marked a significant moment in the world of professional snooker.

### A Historic Performance

The final, held on a prestigious stage, saw Un-Nooh delivering an extraordinary performance that captivated fans and experts alike. The Thai player, known for his fluid playing style, demonstrated exceptional precision and focus throughout the match. His maximum break of 147, a feat that requires a player to pot all 15 reds with 15 blacks followed by the yellow, green, brown, blue, pink, and black balls, is the highest possible score in a single frame of snooker. This achievement solidified his status as a formidable competitor in the sport.

### O'Sullivan's Challenge

Ronnie O'Sullivan, a six-time world champion and one of the most celebrated figures in snooker history, entered the final as a favorite. His experience and tactical prowess have often placed him at the forefront of the sport. However, Un-Nooh's relentless determination and skillful play proved to be a formidable challenge. Despite O'Sullivan's efforts to regain control, Un-Nooh maintained his composure, ultimately securing the victory.
