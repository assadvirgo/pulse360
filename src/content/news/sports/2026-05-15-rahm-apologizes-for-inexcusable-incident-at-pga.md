## Jon Rahm Issues Apology Following Incident at PGA Championship

In a recent incident during the first round of the PGA Championship, professional golfer Jon Rahm expressed regret after accidentally hitting a volunteer with a divot. The occurrence took place on Thursday as Rahm, visibly frustrated, took a swing that resulted in the unintended contact.

### Details of the Incident

The incident unfolded on the course when Rahm, competing in one of golf's most prestigious tournaments, struck the ground with his club, sending a divot flying. Unfortunately, the divot struck a volunteer who was assisting at the event, leading to immediate concern for the individual’s well-being. Eyewitness accounts suggest that the volunteer was hit in both the face and shoulder, prompting a swift response from tournament officials.

### Rahm's Response

Following the incident, Rahm took to social media to address the situation. He described the event as "inexcusable," emphasizing his remorse for the unintended consequences of his actions. "I want to sincerely apologize to the volunteer for what happened," Rahm stated in his post. "It was never my intention to cause harm, and I hope they are okay."
