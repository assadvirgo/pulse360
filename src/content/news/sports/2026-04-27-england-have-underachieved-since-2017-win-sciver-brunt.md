## England's Underachievement in Women's Cricket Since 2017, Says Captain Sciver-Brunt

In a candid assessment of the state of women's cricket in England, captain Nat Sciver-Brunt has expressed concerns over the team's performance since their historic World Cup victory at Lord's in 2017. The win, which marked a significant milestone for the England women's cricket team, has since been overshadowed by a series of underwhelming performances on both domestic and international stages.

### Reflection on the 2017 World Cup Victory

The 2017 ICC Women's World Cup final, held at Lord's, was a defining moment for England cricket. The team, led by then-captain Heather Knight, triumphed over India in a thrilling match that captured the hearts of fans and brought significant attention to women's cricket. However, Sciver-Brunt, who has taken on the captaincy role, believes that the momentum generated by that victory has not been sustained.

### Current State of the Team

Since the 2017 win, England has faced challenges in maintaining their competitive edge. The team has struggled in various international tournaments and bilateral series, often failing to replicate the success that marked their World Cup campaign. Sciver-Brunt's comments highlight a growing concern within the cricketing community regarding the team's ability to build on past achievements.

The captain's reflections come at a time when the England women's team is preparing for crucial fixtures, including upcoming series against top-ranked teams. Sciver-Brunt emphasized the importance of learning from past experiences and the need for a renewed focus on performance and consistency.

### The Path Forward

Looking ahead, Sciver-Brunt has called for a collective effort from players, coaching staff, and cricketing authorities to reinvigorate the team's approach. She underscored the necessity of setting higher standards and fostering a culture of excellence within the squad. The captain's insights resonate with the broader narrative of women's sports, where the pursuit of parity and recognition continues to be a pressing issue.

As the team gears up for future challenges, the focus will be on not only reclaiming their status as a leading force in women's cricket but also inspiring the next generation of players. Sciver-Brunt's leadership will be pivotal in navigating this transition and ensuring that the legacy of the 2017 World Cup victory is not just a moment in time but a foundation for future success.

### Conclusion

Nat Sciver-Brunt's remarks serve as a wake-up call for the England women's cricket team, highlighting the need for introspection and proactive measures to address their underachievement since 2017. As they prepare for upcoming competitions, the focus will be on harnessing the spirit of their historic victory and translating it into consistent performances on the field. The journey ahead is crucial not only for the team's success but also for the continued growth and popularity of women's cricket in England and beyond.
