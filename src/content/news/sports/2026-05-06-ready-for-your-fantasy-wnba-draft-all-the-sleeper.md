## Analyzing Potential Sleeper Picks for Your Fantasy WNBA Draft

As the WNBA season approaches, fantasy basketball enthusiasts are preparing for their drafts with great anticipation. While established stars often dominate the early rounds, savvy players know that finding sleepers—those who are likely to outperform their draft positions—can be the key to building a competitive team. This article will explore several players who could surprise this season, along with a few who may underperform relative to expectations.

## Identifying the Sleepers

### Rising Stars

1. **Te'a Cooper (Los Angeles Sparks)**  
   Te'a Cooper has shown flashes of brilliance in her previous seasons, and with an expanded role on the Sparks this year, she is poised to take a significant leap forward. Her ability to score and facilitate makes her a valuable asset, especially in a league that rewards versatility. As the Sparks look to build around their young talent, Cooper's contributions could exceed her draft position.

2. **Kelsey Mitchell (Indiana Fever)**  
   Kelsey Mitchell has consistently been a scoring threat for the Fever. With a focus on improving her overall game, including playmaking and defense, she could emerge as a top performer. If the Fever can find more consistency as a team, Mitchell's contributions could be pivotal, making her a potential steal in the mid-to-late rounds of drafts.

3. **Aliyah Boston (Indiana Fever)**  
   The rookie sensation is generating buzz for her impressive performances in the preseason. Known for her rebounding and defensive skills, Boston could quickly become a cornerstone for the Fever. If she adapts well to the professional level, her impact could far exceed expectations, making her a solid late-round pick.

### Potential Underperformers

1. **Brittney Griner (Phoenix Mercury)**  
   After returning from a significant absence, Griner's performance is under scrutiny. While she remains a formidable presence in the paint, concerns about her conditioning and the team's overall dynamics may hinder her ability to replicate previous seasons' success. Fantasy managers should weigh these factors carefully when considering her draft position.

2. **Liz Cambage (Los Angeles Sparks)**  
   Cambage is a player who has historically been a dominant force in the league. However, her inconsistent performance and off-court issues have raised red flags. As she joins a new team, there are questions about her fit within the Sparks' system. Drafting Cambage may come with risks, and her potential to underperform could outweigh her upside.

3. **A'ja Wilson (Las Vegas Aces)**  
   While Wilson is undoubtedly one of the league's top talents, her high draft position may not align with her potential output this season. With the Aces boasting a deep roster, her scoring opportunities may be limited. Fantasy players should consider the possibility that her production could dip, making her a candidate for overvaluation in drafts.

## Conclusion

As the WNBA fantasy draft season heats up, identifying sleeper picks and potential underperformers is crucial for assembling a successful team. Players like Te'a Cooper and Kelsey Mitchell could provide significant value, while veterans like Brittney Griner and Liz Cambage may pose risks. By carefully analyzing these factors, fantasy managers can enhance their chances of success in the upcoming season.
