## Ranking the Best World Cup Finals: A Look Back at Iconic Moments

The FIFA World Cup has long been a stage for unforgettable moments in sports history, with the final matches often serving as the pinnacle of drama and athleticism. BBC Sport recently undertook the task of ranking the best World Cup finals, a discussion that inevitably stirs nostalgia among football fans worldwide. This article delves into some of the most iconic finals that have left an indelible mark on the sport.

## The Azteca Stadium and Pele's Brilliance

One of the most celebrated finals took place in 1970 at the Azteca Stadium in Mexico City. The match featured Brazil, led by the legendary Pele, who was playing in his final World Cup. Brazil's 4-1 victory over Italy is often hailed as a masterclass in football, showcasing not only Pele's extraordinary talent but also the team's collective brilliance. The match is remembered for its fluid play, technical skill, and the vibrant atmosphere of the Azteca, which became synonymous with World Cup glory.

## England's Triumph on Home Soil in 1966

Another landmark final occurred in 1966 when England hosted the tournament and faced West Germany at Wembley Stadium. This match is particularly significant as it marked England's first and only World Cup victory to date. The game is best remembered for Geoff Hurst's controversial goal, which was awarded after hitting the crossbar and bouncing down. Hurst went on to score a hat-trick, leading England to a 4-2 win after extra time. The final not only solidified England's place in football history but also ignited a passion for the sport that continues to thrive in the country.

## Messi's First World Cup Final

Lionel Messi's first appearance in a World Cup final came in 2014 when Argentina faced Germany in Brazil. The match, held at the Maracanã Stadium, was a tense affair that ended in heartbreak for Messi and his team, as they lost 1-0 after extra time. Despite the outcome, the final showcased Messi's extraordinary skills and determination, further enhancing his legacy as one of the greatest players in football history. The emotional weight of the match resonated with fans, as it represented a culmination of Messi's journey in the sport.

## The Evolution of World Cup Finals

Over the years, World Cup finals have evolved, reflecting changes in the game, tactics, and the global landscape of football. Each final carries its own story, rich with passion, rivalry, and unforgettable moments. From the tactical battles of the past to the high-octane displays of modern football, these matches encapsulate the spirit of competition and the unifying power of the sport.

## Conclusion

As discussions about the best World Cup finals continue, it is clear that each match holds a unique place in the hearts of fans. Whether it be Pele's artistry at the Azteca, England's historic win in 1966, or Messi's poignant journey in 2014, these finals have shaped the narrative of football. The rankings may vary, but the memories they evoke remain timeless, reminding us of the beauty and drama that the World Cup brings to the global stage.
