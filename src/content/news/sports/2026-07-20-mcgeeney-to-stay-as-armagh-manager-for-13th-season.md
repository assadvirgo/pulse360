## Kieran McGeeney to Continue as Armagh Manager for 13th Season

Kieran McGeeney has been officially ratified to continue as the manager of the Armagh senior football team for his 13th consecutive season. This announcement follows a significant achievement for the Orchard County, as they ended a lengthy wait for an Ulster title in 2026, a victory that has revitalized the team's prospects and boosted morale among players and supporters alike.

### A Long Journey of Leadership

McGeeney, who has been at the helm since 2010, has played a pivotal role in shaping the team's identity and performance over the years. His leadership has seen Armagh through various challenges, including fluctuating success in the competitive landscape of Gaelic football. The culmination of his efforts was evident in 2026 when Armagh secured the Ulster title, marking a historic moment for the county and reaffirming McGeeney's position as a vital figure in their sporting narrative.

### The Significance of the Ulster Title

The Ulster title represents a significant milestone for Armagh, a county with a rich history in Gaelic football but one that had faced a prolonged period without major silverware. The victory not only enhances the team's standing within the province but also serves as a catalyst for future ambitions. It has invigorated the fan base and instilled a renewed sense of purpose among the players as they look to build on this success in the coming seasons.
