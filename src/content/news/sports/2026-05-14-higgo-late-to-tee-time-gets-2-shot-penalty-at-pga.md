## Garrick Higgo Penalized for Late Arrival at PGA Championship

Garrick Higgo, the South African professional golfer, faced a setback during the first round of the 108th PGA Championship held on Thursday. The incident occurred when Higgo arrived late for his scheduled tee time, resulting in a two-stroke penalty.

### Details of the Incident

The PGA Championship, one of the four major championships in professional golf, commenced with a highly competitive field. Higgo, known for his impressive performances on the PGA Tour, was set to tee off at a designated time but failed to arrive punctually. According to tournament officials, the rules of golf stipulate that players must be present at the tee box when it is their turn to play. As a consequence of his tardiness, Higgo was assessed a two-stroke penalty, which could significantly impact his overall score and performance in the tournament.

### Implications for the Tournament

This penalty not only affects Higgo's current standing in the championship but also serves as a reminder of the strict adherence to rules that governs professional golf. The PGA Championship attracts some of the best golfers in the world, and every stroke counts in the pursuit of the prestigious Wanamaker Trophy. The two-stroke penalty could hinder Higgo's chances of advancing further in the tournament, depending on how he performs in the remaining rounds.

### Response from Higgo and Officials

While there has been no immediate public statement from Higgo regarding the incident, such occurrences are not uncommon in professional sports, where timing and discipline are crucial.
