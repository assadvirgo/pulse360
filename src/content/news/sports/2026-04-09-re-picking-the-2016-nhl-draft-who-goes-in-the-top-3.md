## Re-evaluating the 2016 NHL Draft: A Fresh Look at the Top Three Picks

The 2016 NHL Draft, held in Buffalo, New York, has become a pivotal moment in the league's history, shaping the futures of numerous franchises. As the years have passed, the performances of the players selected have provided a clearer picture of their potential and impact on the game. With this hindsight, it is worth revisiting the top three picks of that draft to determine how they might be re-evaluated today.

### Original Top Three Picks

In 2016, the Toronto Maple Leafs selected Auston Matthews first overall, followed by the Winnipeg Jets choosing Patrik Laine, and the Columbus Blue Jackets taking Pierre-Luc Dubois third. At the time, these selections were met with excitement and anticipation, as each player was heralded for their unique skill sets and potential.

### Auston Matthews: The Clear Choice

Auston Matthews' selection by the Maple Leafs has proven to be a franchise-defining decision. Since entering the league, Matthews has consistently been among the top scorers, showcasing an elite combination of size, skill, and hockey intelligence. He has not only won the Calder Trophy as the league's top rookie but has also been a perennial All-Star and a key player for Team USA in international competitions. Given his impact on the ice and his ability to elevate the play of those around him, Matthews remains the top choice in a re-draft scenario.

### Patrik Laine: A Dynamic Goal-Scorer

Patrik Laine, selected second by the Jets, has also made a significant mark in the NHL. Known for his incredible goal-scoring ability, Laine has recorded multiple seasons with 30 or more goals, establishing himself as one of the league's premier wingers. His powerful shot and offensive prowess have made him a fan favorite, although his consistency and defensive play have been points of contention. Nonetheless, Laine's offensive upside and game-changing capabilities warrant his position as the second overall pick in a re-evaluation of the 2016 draft.

### Pierre-Luc Dubois: Rising to the Occasion

The third overall pick, Pierre-Luc Dubois, has emerged as a strong two-way center, demonstrating versatility and leadership. Initially drafted by the Blue Jackets, Dubois has since been traded to the Winnipeg Jets, where he has continued to develop into a reliable top-six forward. His ability to contribute offensively while also being responsible defensively makes him a valuable asset to any team. In a re-draft, Dubois could be argued as a strong candidate for the third overall pick, especially considering the importance of having a skilled center in today’s game.

### Conclusion: A Reflection on Talent

In retrospect, the 2016 NHL Draft has yielded a wealth of talent, with Matthews, Laine, and Dubois all establishing themselves as key players in the league. While the original selections have proven to be sound choices, the exercise of re-evaluating the draft highlights the ongoing evolution of player development and the impact of hindsight on decision-making in professional sports.

As teams continue to assess their rosters and look toward the future, the 2016 draft serves as a reminder of the importance of scouting and player evaluation in the ever-competitive landscape of the NHL.
