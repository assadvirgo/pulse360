## UCLA's Megan Grant Sets NCAA Record with 38th Home Run

In a remarkable display of athletic prowess, UCLA's Megan Grant has etched her name in the annals of NCAA history by hitting her 38th home run of the season. This significant achievement occurred during the Big Ten championship game against Nebraska, held in College Park, Maryland, on Saturday afternoon. 

### A Historic Milestone

Grant's record-breaking home run not only solidifies her status as one of the premier sluggers in collegiate softball but also marks a new benchmark for Division I softball. The previous record for the most home runs in a single season was set at 37, making Grant's achievement a momentous occasion for both her and the UCLA Bruins.

### The Game Context

The home run came in the third inning of a high-stakes championship match, showcasing Grant's ability to perform under pressure. The game itself was a critical contest, as both teams vied for the title, adding an extra layer of significance to her record-setting hit.

Megan Grant, a standout player for UCLA, has consistently demonstrated exceptional skill and determination throughout her collegiate career.
