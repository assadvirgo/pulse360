## Patrick Godfrey, ‘Ever After: A Cinderella Story’ Star, Dies at 93

Patrick Godfrey, the esteemed English actor known for his portrayal of Leonardo da Vinci in the beloved 1998 fantasy film “Ever After: A Cinderella Story,” has passed away at the age of 93. His death was confirmed on June 5 through a statement released by his talent agency, Markham Froggatt & Irwin.

### A Celebrated Career

Born in 1930, Godfrey enjoyed a long and varied career in the performing arts, establishing himself as a versatile actor on both stage and screen. His role in “Ever After,” which reimagined the classic Cinderella tale, introduced him to a new generation of audiences and showcased his ability to bring historical figures to life with charm and depth.

Godfrey's career spanned several decades, during which he appeared in numerous television series, films, and theatrical productions. His talent was not limited to any one genre; he adeptly navigated drama, comedy, and fantasy, earning respect from peers and audiences alike. 

### Impact on Film and Theatre

In addition to his work in “Ever After,” Godfrey's filmography includes a variety of roles that highlight his range as an actor. He appeared in productions such as “The Man Who Knew Too Little” and “The Importance of Being Earnest,” demonstrating his ability to adapt to different styles and narratives. His contributions to theatre were equally significant, with performances that left a lasting impression on audiences.

Godfrey's portrayal of Da Vinci in “Ever After” was particularly notable for its warmth and wisdom, providing a guiding presence for the film's protagonist, played by Drew Barrymore. This role not only endeared him to fans of the film but also solidified his place in cinematic history as an actor who could blend humor with gravitas.

### Legacy and Remembrance

The announcement of Godfrey's passing has prompted an outpouring of tributes from colleagues and fans alike. Many have taken to social media to express their condolences and share memories of his performances. His agency's statement reflected the sentiments of many, stating, “It is with great sadness that we can confirm the passing of Patrick Godfrey, a beloved actor and a remarkable talent.”

As the entertainment industry mourns the loss of this distinguished figure, Godfrey's legacy lives on through his extensive body of work. His contributions to film and theatre will continue to inspire future generations of actors and filmmakers.

Patrick Godfrey's death marks the end of an era for a generation of audiences who grew up watching his performances.
