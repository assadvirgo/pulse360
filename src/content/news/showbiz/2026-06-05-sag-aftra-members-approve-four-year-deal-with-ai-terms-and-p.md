## SAG-AFTRA Members Ratify New Contract Addressing AI and Pension Fund Merger

Members of the Screen Actors Guild-American Federation of Television and Radio Artists (SAG-AFTRA) have overwhelmingly approved a new four-year contract with major film and television studios. The ratification reflects a significant shift in the industry, particularly concerning the use of artificial intelligence and the management of union pension funds.

### Strong Support for the New Contract

In a decisive vote, 91.4% of the participating members supported the contract, while only 8.6% opposed it. The turnout for this vote was 19.3% of eligible members, indicating a robust engagement from the union's base on this pivotal issue. The new agreement marks a crucial step in addressing the evolving landscape of the entertainment industry, especially as technology continues to shape production practices.

### Key Provisions on Artificial Intelligence

One of the most notable aspects of the contract is its provisions regarding synthetic actors and the use of artificial intelligence in production. As the industry grapples with the implications of AI, this contract aims to establish guidelines that protect the rights of performers. The specifics of these provisions are expected to set a precedent for how AI can be utilized in film and television, ensuring that human actors are not replaced or exploited by technological advancements.

### Merger of Pension Funds

In addition to the AI provisions, the new contract includes a merger of SAG-AFTRA's two pension funds. This consolidation aims to streamline benefits for members and enhance the financial stability of the union's retirement offerings. By merging the funds, SAG-AFTRA seeks to provide a more robust safety net for its members, particularly as the industry faces ongoing changes and uncertainties.

### Implications for the Entertainment Industry

The ratification of this contract signals a proactive approach by SAG-AFTRA in navigating the complexities of modern entertainment. As artificial intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into the production process, the union's commitment to protecting its members' rights is paramount. This contract not only addresses immediate concerns but also positions SAG-AFTRA as a leader in establishing ethical standards in the use of technology within the industry.

### Conclusion

The approval of the new four-year contract by SAG-AFTRA members reflects a significant moment in the entertainment industry. With strong support for provisions addressing AI and a strategic merger of pension funds, the union is taking essential steps to safeguard the interests of its members in an evolving landscape. As the industry continues to adapt to technological advancements, the implications of this contract will likely resonate for years to come, shaping the future of work for actors and performers across the country.
