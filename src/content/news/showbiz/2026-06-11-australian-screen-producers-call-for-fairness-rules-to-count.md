## Australian Screen Producers Advocate for Fairness in Streaming Market

In a significant move aimed at addressing the challenges faced by independent producers, Screen Producers Australia (SPA) has submitted a comprehensive 22-recommendation proposal to the Australian government's National Cultural Policy consultation. The submission highlights the growing market power of streaming platforms and calls for structural reforms to ensure a fairer landscape for local content creators.

### The Impact of Streaming on Independent Producers

The rise of digital streaming platforms has transformed the media landscape, fundamentally altering commissioning practices and market dynamics. SPA's submission emphasizes that this shift has disproportionately affected small and medium-sized production companies, which often struggle to compete against the financial and promotional resources of larger streaming giants.

SPA argues that the current environment favors major streaming services, which can dictate terms and conditions that may not be sustainable for independent producers. This imbalance, they contend, threatens the diversity and richness of Australian content, as smaller entities find it increasingly difficult to secure funding and distribution for their projects.

### Key Recommendations for Structural Reform

The 22 recommendations put forth by SPA encompass a range of strategies aimed at leveling the playing field. Among the key proposals are calls for enhanced regulatory frameworks that would require streaming platforms to invest a certain percentage of their revenue back into local content production. This would not only support independent creators but also foster a more vibrant and varied media landscape.

Additionally, SPA advocates for clearer guidelines regarding content commissioning and acquisition practices. By establishing more transparent processes, independent producers would have a better understanding of how their work is evaluated and selected by streaming services.

### The Role of Government in Supporting Local Content

SPA's submission underscores the critical role of government intervention in safeguarding the interests of Australian screen producers. The organization believes that a well-defined National Cultural Policy can help protect local content from being overshadowed by international offerings, ensuring that Australian stories continue to be told and celebrated.

The proposed reforms are not only about protecting the interests of producers but also about enriching the cultural fabric of Australia. By fostering a more equitable environment for content creation, the government can help ensure that a diverse array of voices and stories are represented in the media.

### Industry Response and Future Implications

The response from the broader industry has been largely supportive of SPA's initiatives, with many stakeholders recognizing the urgent need for reform. As streaming platforms continue to dominate the market, the potential consequences for independent producers could be profound if no action is taken.

The submission to the National Cultural Policy consultation marks a pivotal moment for Australian screen production. If the government acts on these recommendations, it could lead to a more balanced and sustainable media ecosystem that not only supports independent creators but also enriches the viewing experience for audiences across the nation.

As discussions around the National Cultural Policy progress, the spotlight will remain on the government’s commitment to fostering a fair and equitable environment for all players in the screen production industry. The outcome of this consultation could set a precedent for how Australia navigates the complexities of the digital age in the realm of media and entertainment.
