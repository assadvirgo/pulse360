## Celebrated Play "White Rabbit Red Rabbit" Makes a West End Return

In a significant cultural revival, the acclaimed play "White Rabbit Red Rabbit" is set to return to London's West End, marking its 15th anniversary. This unique production, created by Iranian-German playwright Nassim Soleimanpour, has garnered international attention for its innovative format and thought-provoking themes.

### A New Theatre Company

The return is facilitated by There & Then, a newly established independent theatre company founded by Soleimanpour and director Omar Elerian. The company aims to bring fresh perspectives to the theatrical landscape and will debut with "White Rabbit Red Rabbit" in collaboration with renowned producer Nica Burns. This partnership promises to uphold the artistic integrity and vision of the original work while introducing it to a new generation of audiences.

### The Unique Format

"White Rabbit Red Rabbit" is distinctive in its execution; each performance features a different actor who receives a sealed envelope containing the script just before stepping on stage. This element of surprise not only challenges the performers but also engages the audience in a shared experience of discovery and spontaneity. The play's structure allows for a diverse range of interpretations, making each performance a unique event.

### Star-Studded Lineup

The West End revival boasts a remarkable lineup of talent, including prominent actors such as David Tennant, Riz Ahmed, Jodie Whittaker, and David Harewood. Each of these performers is expected to bring their own flair and interpretation to the role, contributing to the play's dynamic nature. Their involvement highlights the play's appeal and its ability to attract high-caliber talent.

### Themes and Impact

"White Rabbit Red Rabbit" explores themes of freedom, oppression, and the power of storytelling. The play has resonated with audiences across the globe, reflecting the struggles faced by individuals under authoritarian regimes. Soleimanpour's work is not only a testament to the resilience of the human spirit but also serves as a reminder of the importance of artistic expression in challenging times.

### Looking Ahead

As the production gears up for its West End return, there is palpable excitement among theatre enthusiasts and critics alike. The combination of a revolutionary format, a star-studded cast, and a powerful narrative promises to create a memorable theatrical experience. The revival of "White Rabbit Red Rabbit" is not just a celebration of its 15th anniversary; it is a reaffirmation of the enduring power of theatre to provoke thought and inspire change.

With performances commencing soon, audiences are encouraged to secure their tickets early to witness this groundbreaking production. The return of "White Rabbit Red Rabbit" to the West End is poised to be a significant event in the theatrical calendar, reaffirming the play's relevance and impact in today's world.
