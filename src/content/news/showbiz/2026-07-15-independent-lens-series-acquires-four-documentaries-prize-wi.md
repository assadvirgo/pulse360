## Independent Lens Expands Its Documentary Portfolio with Four New Acquisitions

In a significant move for the documentary film landscape, PBS's acclaimed series "Independent Lens" has announced the acquisition of four new documentaries. This development is particularly encouraging for filmmakers and audiences alike, as it underscores the series' commitment to showcasing diverse and impactful storytelling.

### New Additions to the Lineup

The newly acquired films include "Jane Elliott Against The World," "Seeds," "My Omaha," and "The Other Side of Memory." Each of these documentaries has garnered attention for their unique perspectives and compelling narratives, making them fitting additions to the renowned series.

"Jane Elliott Against The World" focuses on the pioneering educator and anti-racism activist Jane Elliott, who is best known for her "Blue Eyes/Brown Eyes" exercise. This documentary delves into her lifelong commitment to combating prejudice and promoting social justice, offering viewers an intimate look at her philosophy and the challenges she faces in her advocacy.

"Seeds," on the other hand, explores the intricate relationship between agriculture and sustainability. It examines the vital role of seeds in food security and environmental health, showcasing innovative practices and the voices of those dedicated to preserving biodiversity.

"My Omaha" presents a personal and poignant narrative centered around the lives of individuals in Omaha, Nebraska. Through a series of vignettes, the film captures the essence of community and the diverse experiences that shape the city's identity.

Lastly, "The Other Side of Memory" invites viewers to reflect on the complexities of memory and identity. This documentary seeks to understand how personal histories are intertwined with broader societal narratives, offering a thought-provoking exploration of the human experience.

### A Commitment to Diverse Storytelling

"Independent Lens" has long been recognized for its dedication to presenting underrepresented voices and stories that challenge conventional narratives. With the addition of these four films, the series continues to uphold its mission of fostering dialogue and understanding through documentary filmmaking.

The 28th season of "Independent Lens" is set to air these documentaries, providing a platform for audiences to engage with important social issues and the stories of individuals who often go unheard. The series has been instrumental in bringing attention to various topics, from civil rights to environmental challenges, and this latest acquisition further solidifies its role as a vital resource for documentary enthusiasts.

### Looking Ahead

As "Independent Lens" prepares for its upcoming season, the acquisition of these four documentaries marks a promising expansion of its programming. The series not only enriches the viewing experience for audiences but also supports filmmakers in their efforts to share meaningful stories.

With the increasing demand for quality documentary content, the commitment of "Independent Lens" to showcase diverse narratives is a welcome development in the media landscape. As these films make their debut, they are expected to spark conversations and inspire viewers to reflect on the pressing issues of our time.
