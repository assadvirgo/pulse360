## Channel 4 and See-Saw Films to Adapt Benedict Lombe’s Play 'Shifters' for Television

In a significant development for the arts, Channel 4 and See-Saw Films have announced their collaboration to create a television adaptation of Benedict Lombe’s acclaimed play, *Shifters*. This announcement comes as the play prepares to transfer to A24’s Cherry Lane Theatre, marking a notable moment in its journey.

### A Groundbreaking Play

*Shifters* has garnered attention for its poignant exploration of the lives of two young Black individuals who reconnect after years apart. The narrative delves into themes of identity, belonging, and the complexities of personal relationships, resonating deeply with audiences. The play has received accolades from various quarters, including notable figures such as actor Idris Elba and musician Little Simz, who have publicly expressed their admiration for Lombe’s work.

Starring Tosin Cole, known for his role in *Doctor Who*, the play has been praised not only for its storytelling but also for its authentic representation of contemporary issues faced by young Black people in the UK. The production has created a significant impact since its debut, and its upcoming move to A24’s Cherry Lane Theatre in New York City is expected to further elevate its profile.

### Television Adaptation

The decision to adapt *Shifters* into a television series reflects a growing trend in the entertainment industry to bring stage productions to a wider audience. Channel 4, known for its commitment to innovative and diverse programming, aims to capture the essence of Lombe’s play while expanding its reach through the television medium. 

See-Saw Films, a production company renowned for its work on critically acclaimed projects, will bring its expertise to the adaptation, ensuring that the integrity of the original work is maintained while also appealing to a broader demographic.

### Anticipation and Expectations

The adaptation of *Shifters* is anticipated to attract a diverse audience, potentially introducing the powerful narrative to those who may not have had the opportunity to experience it in a theater setting. The collaboration between Channel 4 and See-Saw Films is expected to harness the strengths of both entities, combining Channel 4’s innovative storytelling approach with See-Saw’s cinematic expertise.

As the television landscape continues to evolve, adaptations of stage plays have become increasingly popular, allowing for deeper character exploration and richer storytelling. The adaptation of *Shifters* is poised to contribute to this trend, offering viewers a chance to engage with Lombe’s thought-provoking narrative in a new format.

### Conclusion

The upcoming television adaptation of *Shifters* represents an exciting intersection of theater and television, promising to bring a vital story to an even larger audience. With its themes of reconnection and identity, the adaptation is set to resonate with viewers, continuing the conversation around the experiences of young Black individuals in contemporary society. As the project develops, anticipation will undoubtedly grow for how Lombe’s powerful narrative will translate to the screen.
