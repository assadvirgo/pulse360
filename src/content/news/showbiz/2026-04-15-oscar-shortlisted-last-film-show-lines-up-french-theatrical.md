## Oscar-Shortlisted ‘Last Film Show’ Set for French Theatrical Release

Pan Nalin's critically acclaimed Gujarati-language film, "Last Film Show," is poised to make its debut in French cinemas on June 10. The film, which has garnered international recognition, will be released under the French title "La Dernière Séance" and is set to appear in over 100 theaters across France. This release follows an acquisition by Friday Entertainment from Studiocanal.

### A Glimpse into the Film

Originally titled "Chhello Show," "Last Film Show" is an India-France co-production that has captivated audiences with its poignant storytelling and rich cultural representation. The film tells the story of a young boy's love for cinema, exploring themes of nostalgia, childhood, and the transformative power of film. Its heartfelt narrative has resonated with viewers, leading to its selection as India's official submission for the 95th Academy Awards.

### Critical Acclaim and Awards

Since its premiere, "Last Film Show" has received numerous accolades, including several awards at international film festivals. Its unique approach to storytelling and its ability to evoke deep emotional responses have made it a standout piece in contemporary cinema. The film's artistic vision, combined with its exploration of the impact of cinema on personal and collective identities, has been particularly praised by critics.

### The French Market

The decision to release "Last Film Show" in France highlights the growing interest in international cinema within the French market. French audiences have a long-standing appreciation for diverse storytelling, and the film's themes are likely to resonate with viewers who value the art of filmmaking. By bringing this film to over 100 screens, Friday Entertainment aims to introduce the rich narrative of "Last Film Show" to a wider audience.
