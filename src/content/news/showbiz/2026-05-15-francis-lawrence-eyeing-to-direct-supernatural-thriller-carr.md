## Francis Lawrence Set to Direct Supernatural Thriller ‘Carry Me To My Grave’

In a strategic move to expand its slate of genre films, Lionsgate has secured the rights to the upcoming novel "Carry Me to My Grave," authored by New York Times bestselling writer Christopher Golden. This adaptation is poised to be directed and produced by Francis Lawrence, who has established a first-look deal with the studio.

### A Promising Collaboration

Francis Lawrence, known for his work on major film franchises such as "The Hunger Games," is no stranger to the supernatural genre. His involvement in "Carry Me to My Grave" signals Lionsgate's commitment to producing compelling narratives that resonate with audiences. Alongside Lawrence, Cameron Maconomy will also take on a producing role, ensuring a collaborative approach to the film's development.

### About the Novel

"Carry Me to My Grave" has generated significant buzz in literary circles ahead of its release. Christopher Golden, acclaimed for his ability to weave intricate tales that blend horror and fantasy, is expected to deliver another gripping narrative. While specific plot details remain under wraps, the novel is anticipated to explore themes of loss, memory, and the supernatural, elements that are likely to translate well to the screen.

### Lionsgate's Strategic Direction

Lionsgate's acquisition of the film rights reflects a broader trend within the industry to adapt popular literature into cinematic experiences. The studio has previously found success with adaptations, and "Carry Me to My Grave" appears to align with its strategy of tapping into existing fanbases while attracting new viewers.

### Industry Reception

The announcement has been met with enthusiasm from both fans of Golden's work and the film community. Francis Lawrence's track record suggests that he possesses the vision and expertise needed to bring this story to life, potentially elevating it beyond a standard thriller to a thought-provoking exploration of its themes.
