## Famke Janssen Critiques Marvel's Decision on 'Avengers: Doomsday'

As anticipation builds for the upcoming Marvel film, *Avengers: Doomsday*, actress Famke Janssen has expressed her disappointment regarding her absence from the project. Known for her iconic portrayal of Jean Grey/Phoenix in the *X-Men* franchise, Janssen believes that Marvel Studios "made a mistake" by not including her in the film, which is set to premiere on December 18.

### A Legacy in the X-Men Franchise

Famke Janssen first stepped into the role of Jean Grey in 2000, when *X-Men* was released, marking the beginning of a successful series that would span nearly two decades. Her character, a powerful mutant with telepathic and telekinetic abilities, has been a central figure in the narrative arc of the *X-Men* films. Janssen reprised her role in several sequels, including *X2: X-Men United* and *X-Men: The Last Stand*, where she portrayed the Dark Phoenix, a storyline that resonated deeply with fans.

### The Current Landscape of Marvel

With the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) continually evolving, the upcoming *Avengers: Doomsday* film is expected to bring back several beloved characters from the *X-Men* series. Notably, several of Janssen's former co-stars have confirmed their return, heightening the sense of nostalgia for fans of the franchise. However, Janssen's absence has sparked discussions about the direction Marvel is taking with its characters and storylines.

### Janssen's Perspective

In a recent interview, Janssen articulated her feelings about the situation, stating that she felt a strong connection to her character and the franchise as a whole. "It just feels like a missed opportunity," she remarked. Her comments reflect a broader sentiment among fans who have been vocal about their desire to see the original cast reunited on screen. Janssen's return could have provided a sense of continuity, bridging the gap between the older *X-Men* films and the new direction of the MCU.

### Fan Reactions and Future Implications

The response from fans has been mixed, with many supporting Janssen's viewpoint and expressing disappointment over her exclusion. Social media platforms have been abuzz with discussions about the potential for her character's return, and some fans are even calling for a reconsideration of her role in future Marvel projects.

As the film's release date approaches, it remains to be seen how *Avengers: Doomsday* will address the legacy of the *X-Men* franchise and whether Janssen's absence will impact the overall reception of the film. The MCU has a history of surprising its audience, and with the ever-expanding multiverse concept, there may still be opportunities for characters from previous iterations to make a comeback.

### Conclusion

Famke Janssen's comments highlight the complexities of character continuity in the MCU and the emotional connections that actors and fans have with their roles. As *Avengers: Doomsday* gears up for its release, the conversation around Janssen's absence serves as a reminder of the enduring impact of the *X-Men* franchise and the characters that have left an indelible mark on the superhero genre. Whether Marvel will heed the call for a reunion remains to be seen, but the dialogue surrounding this issue underscores the passionate investment of fans in the stories and characters they cherish.
