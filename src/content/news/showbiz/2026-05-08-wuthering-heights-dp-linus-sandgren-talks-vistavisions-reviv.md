## Linus Sandgren Discusses the Revival of VistaVision and Future Projects with IMAX

Linus Sandgren, the Academy Award-winning cinematographer known for his work on films such as "La La Land" and "Dune: Part 3," recently shared insights into the evolving landscape of film formats, particularly the revival of VistaVision. As the film industry continues to explore various technologies to enhance storytelling, Sandgren emphasizes the importance of selecting the right format as a means to serve the narrative.

### The Significance of Film Formats

In a recent interview, Sandgren elaborated on his approach to choosing the format for a film. He stated, "It's a tool to serve the story," highlighting the critical role that technical choices play in the filmmaking process. This perspective underscores the notion that the visual medium should complement and elevate the narrative rather than overshadow it.

VistaVision, a high-resolution film format originally developed in the 1950s, has seen a resurgence in interest among filmmakers. The format's ability to capture expansive images with a wider aspect ratio allows for a more immersive viewing experience. Sandgren's enthusiasm for VistaVision reflects a broader trend in the industry, where filmmakers are increasingly seeking to leverage classic techniques alongside modern advancements.

### Upcoming Projects and IMAX Collaboration

As audiences anticipate the release of "Dune: Part 3" this December, Sandgren's collaboration with director Denis Villeneuve has generated significant excitement. The film promises to further explore the intricate world of Frank Herbert's science fiction saga, and Sandgren's cinematographic expertise is expected to play a pivotal role in bringing this vision to life.

Looking ahead, Sandgren also expressed interest in working with IMAX, a format renowned for its large-scale presentation and immersive quality. The combination of VistaVision's rich visual storytelling and IMAX's expansive viewing experience could create a compelling synergy for future projects. Sandgren's commitment to exploring these formats indicates a dedication to pushing the boundaries of visual storytelling.

### The Future of Cinematography

As the film industry navigates the challenges posed by digital streaming and changing audience preferences, the revival of traditional formats like VistaVision represents a desire for authenticity and artistic expression. Cinematographers like Sandgren are at the forefront of this movement, advocating for the use of diverse tools to enhance the cinematic experience.

In a landscape where technology is constantly evolving, the emphasis on storytelling remains paramount. Sandgren's insights serve as a reminder that, regardless of the format, the ultimate goal of filmmaking is to connect with audiences on a deeper level.

As "Dune: Part 3" prepares to hit theaters, the film community will be watching closely to see how Sandgren's choices in cinematography will influence the film's reception and artistic impact. The revival of VistaVision, coupled with the potential of IMAX, may well redefine the way stories are told on the big screen, inviting audiences to experience cinema in new and exciting ways.
