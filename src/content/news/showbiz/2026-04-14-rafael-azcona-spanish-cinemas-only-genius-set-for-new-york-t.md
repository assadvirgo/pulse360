## Rafael Azcona: A Tribute to the Genius of Spanish Cinema in New York

This week, New York will pay homage to one of the most influential figures in Spanish cinema, Rafael Azcona, often referred to as “the only genius” of the medium by Oscar-winning filmmaker Fernando Trueba. The tribute is part of the showcase titled "The Goya Goes To – New Spanish Films," which aims to celebrate contemporary Spanish cinema and its rich artistic heritage.

### The Legacy of Rafael Azcona

Rafael Azcona, who passed away in 2008, was a prolific screenwriter whose work has left an indelible mark on Spanish film. His collaborations with prominent directors, including Fernando Trueba, have produced some of the most acclaimed films in the country's history. Trueba's statement underscores Azcona's unique contribution to the cinematic landscape, highlighting the depth and creativity he brought to his projects.

One of the key films being screened during this tribute is "Belle Époque," a romantic comedy that won the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film in 1994. Azcona's role as the lead writer was instrumental in shaping the film's narrative, which explores themes of love, war, and the complexities of human relationships. The film not only garnered international acclaim but also showcased the vibrant storytelling that characterizes Azcona's work.

### The Importance of the New York Showcase

The New York showcase serves as a platform to introduce a wider audience to the richness of Spanish cinema, particularly the works of Azcona. By screening "Belle Époque," the event aims to highlight the artistic achievements of Spanish filmmakers and the cultural significance of their narratives. The Goya Goes To initiative is designed to foster appreciation for Spanish films and encourage dialogue about their impact on global cinema.

In addition to the screening, the event will likely feature discussions and panels that delve into Azcona's legacy and the evolution of Spanish cinema. Such conversations are essential for understanding the context in which Azcona worked and the influences that shaped his storytelling style.
