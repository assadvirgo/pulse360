## Shelly Johnson Elected President of the American Society of Cinematographers

In a significant development for the film industry, Shelly Johnson, the director of photography (DP) known for her work on films such as "Greyhound" and "Jurassic Park III," has been elected as the 50th President of the American Society of Cinematographers (ASC). The announcement was made by the ASC board of governors, marking a pivotal moment in the organization's history.

## Background on Shelly Johnson

Shelly Johnson has established herself as a prominent figure in cinematography, with a career spanning several decades. Her contributions to the film industry have been recognized through various accolades, and her expertise in visual storytelling has made her a sought-after collaborator on numerous high-profile projects. Johnson previously served as the ASC president from 2023 to 2025, during which she demonstrated her commitment to advancing the art and craft of cinematography.

## Transition of Leadership

Johnson succeeds Mandy Walker, who has held the presidency prior to this election. Walker's tenure was marked by significant initiatives aimed at promoting diversity and inclusion within the cinematography community, as well as fostering educational opportunities for aspiring filmmakers. As Johnson takes the reins, she inherits a legacy of progress and innovation, with the opportunity to build upon the foundation laid by her predecessor.

## New Slate of Officers

In addition to Johnson's election, the ASC board also voted in a new slate of officers, including several vice presidents who will support the organization’s mission. These appointments reflect a commitment to ensuring that the ASC continues to be a leading voice in the cinematography field, advocating for the interests of its members and the broader film community.

## The Role of the American Society of Cinematographers

Founded in 1919, the American Society of Cinematographers is a professional organization dedicated to advancing the art and science of cinematography. The ASC plays a crucial role in promoting the work of cinematographers, providing educational resources, and fostering a sense of community among its members. With over 400 members worldwide, the organization serves as a platform for collaboration and innovation in the film industry.

## Looking Ahead

As Johnson steps into her new role, she has expressed a vision for the future of the ASC that includes a focus on mentorship, sustainability in filmmaking, and the integration of new technologies in cinematography. Her leadership is expected to inspire both established professionals and emerging talents in the field, encouraging a new generation of filmmakers to explore the possibilities of visual storytelling.

The election of Shelly Johnson as the ASC's 50th president is a testament to her dedication and influence within the industry. As the organization moves forward under her leadership, it will be interesting to observe how her initiatives will shape the future of cinematography and the broader cinematic landscape.
