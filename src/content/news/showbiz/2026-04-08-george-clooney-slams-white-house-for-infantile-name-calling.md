## George Clooney Responds to White House Criticism Over Comments on Trump

In a recent exchange that has drawn significant media attention, actor and filmmaker George Clooney has publicly criticized the White House following remarks made by Communications Director Steven Cheung. The controversy stems from Clooney's assertion that President Donald Trump committed a "war crime" through his aggressive rhetoric towards Iran, which he claimed threatened the existence of "a whole civilization."

### Background of the Dispute

The friction began when Clooney expressed his concerns regarding the Trump administration's foreign policy, particularly in relation to Iran. He characterized the president's threats as not only reckless but also as a potential violation of international law. Clooney's comments were made in the context of a ceasefire deal that was eventually reached, but he maintained that the implications of Trump's statements were severe and warranted condemnation.

In response, Cheung issued a statement that dismissed Clooney's criticisms, labeling the actor's remarks as "infantile name-calling." The White House's reaction seemed to aim at undermining Clooney's credibility, particularly focusing on his career as an actor, which Cheung described as "terrible acting."

### Clooney's Retort

Clooney did not take the White House's comments lightly. In a statement provided to The Independent, he rebuffed the administration's critique, emphasizing that the issue at hand was far more serious than personal attacks on his acting career. He argued that the focus should remain on the implications of the president's actions and statements regarding international relations and the potential consequences for global peace.

The actor's response highlighted a broader concern about the tone of political discourse in the United States, particularly under the Trump administration. Clooney's remarks reflect a growing frustration among some public figures who feel that the administration often resorts to personal insults rather than engaging in substantive discussions about policy and governance.
