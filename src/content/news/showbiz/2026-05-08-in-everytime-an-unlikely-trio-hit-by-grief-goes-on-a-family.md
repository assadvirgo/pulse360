## Austrian Director Sandra Wollner Premieres 'Everytime' at Cannes

In a poignant exploration of grief and the complexities of familial relationships, Austrian writer-director Sandra Wollner presents her third feature film, "Everytime," at the prestigious Cannes Film Festival. This marks Wollner's debut at the Croisette, showcasing her unique storytelling style in the Un Certain Regard section of the festival.

## Blurring the Lines of Reality and Fiction

"Everytime" delves into the lives of an unlikely trio brought together by shared sorrow. The film intricately weaves a narrative that challenges the boundaries between reality and fiction, inviting audiences to reflect on their perceptions of time and memory. Wollner's approach is characterized by a delicate balance of emotional depth and surrealism, a hallmark of her previous works.

The film's central theme revolves around a family holiday that never materializes, serving as a metaphor for unfulfilled hopes and the lingering presence of loss. As the characters navigate their grief, viewers are prompted to consider how personal histories shape relationships and the ways in which individuals cope with absence.

## A Unique Perspective on Grief

Wollner's storytelling is notable for its sensitivity and introspection. "Everytime" does not merely depict grief; it examines the multifaceted nature of loss and the varied responses it elicits from different individuals. The film's narrative structure allows for a fluid exploration of time, where past memories intermingle with present realities, creating a tapestry that is both haunting and relatable.

The casting choices further enhance the film's emotional resonance. The actors bring authenticity to their roles, embodying the complexities of their characters' experiences. Their performances are expected to evoke empathy and provoke thought, making "Everytime" a significant entry in this year's festival lineup.

## Reception and Impact

As it premieres at Cannes, "Everytime" is poised to capture the attention of both critics and audiences alike. The Un Certain Regard section is known for highlighting innovative and unconventional storytelling, and Wollner's film fits this description perfectly. Early reactions suggest that "Everytime" could provoke important discussions about grief, memory, and the human condition.

Wollner's participation in Cannes not only marks a personal milestone but also underscores the growing recognition of Austrian cinema on the global stage. With "Everytime," she contributes to the ongoing dialogue about the role of film in exploring complex emotional landscapes.

## Conclusion

"Everytime" stands as a testament to the power of cinema to reflect and interrogate the human experience. As audiences gather at Cannes to witness this world premiere, the film promises to leave a lasting impression, inviting viewers to contemplate their own encounters with grief and the passage of time. Wollner's work is a reminder of the profound connections that bind us, even in the face of loss.
