## Maria Bakalova and Rebekah Fortune Share Their Affection for 'Learning to Breathe Under Water'

The film industry is abuzz with anticipation as "Learning to Breathe Under Water," a poignant drama featuring Maria Bakalova and Rebekah Fortune, prepares for its debut in the Special Screenings program at the Karlovy Vary International Film Festival. This film, which also stars Rory Kinnear and Ezra Carlisle, explores the complexities of family dynamics through the lens of an autistic boy, his grieving father, and an au pair who profoundly alters their lives.

### A Unique Narrative

"Learning to Breathe Under Water" is not just another film; it is a narrative that delves deep into the emotional struggles of its characters. The story revolves around a young boy on the autism spectrum and the challenges he faces, alongside his father who is grappling with the loss of his partner. The introduction of an au pair adds a layer of complexity, as she becomes a catalyst for change and healing within the family unit.

Both Bakalova and Fortune have expressed their admiration for the film's unique storytelling. Bakalova, known for her breakout role in "Borat Subsequent Moviefilm," emphasized the importance of portraying authentic and nuanced characters. She noted that the film's exploration of grief and acceptance resonates with audiences, making it a significant addition to contemporary cinema.

### The Significance of the Shark on the Roof

A curious element of the film that has sparked discussion is the symbolic shark on the roof. While its exact meaning may be open to interpretation, it serves as a visual metaphor for the unexpected challenges and fears that the characters must confront. Fortune remarked on the shark's presence, stating, "It represents the looming presence of anxiety and the unknown, which is a central theme in the film. Just like life, the shark is both a threat and a reminder of the beauty that can exist in chaos."

### A Film for All Audiences

The film's narrative is crafted to appeal to a broad audience, addressing themes of love, loss, and resilience. The performances by Bakalova and Fortune, along with the supporting cast, are expected to deliver a powerful emotional experience. The film's director has skillfully woven together the various threads of the story, ensuring that viewers are engaged from start to finish.

As "Learning to Breathe Under Water" prepares for its premiere, both Bakalova and Fortune are eager to share their work with the world. They believe that the film not only entertains but also educates audiences about the realities of living with autism and the importance of empathy in understanding one another.

### Conclusion

With its premiere at the Karlovy Vary International Film Festival, "Learning to Breathe Under Water" is poised to make a significant impact. The film's exploration of deep emotional themes, combined with its unique visual elements, promises to resonate with viewers. As audiences prepare to experience this heartfelt story, the contributions of Bakalova, Fortune, and the entire cast and crew are sure to be recognized as a testament to the power of cinema in addressing complex human experiences.
