## Tom Morello Announces 'Power to the People' Festival Featuring Major Artists

Rage Against the Machine co-founder Tom Morello has unveiled plans for a new music festival titled "Power to the People." Scheduled for Saturday, October 3, 2026, the event will take place at the Merriweather Post Pavilion in Columbia, Maryland, located near the nation's capital, Washington D.C. 

### A Celebration of Music and Activism

The "Power to the People" festival is being promoted as a "non-partisan celebration of peace, justice, solidarity, music, and community action." Morello, known for his activism and advocacy for various social causes, aims to create a platform that unites people through music while promoting important social issues. 

The festival is expected to attract a diverse audience, bringing together music lovers and activists alike. The lineup includes prominent artists such as Bruce Springsteen, the Foo Fighters, and Dave Matthews, among others. This eclectic mix of musicians reflects Morello's vision of inclusivity and community engagement.

### A Call for Unity

In an era marked by political polarization and social unrest, Morello's initiative seeks to foster a sense of unity among attendees. By emphasizing themes of peace and justice, the festival aims to encourage dialogue and inspire action within communities. 

Morello has long been an advocate for various social movements, and this festival represents a continuation of his commitment to using music as a vehicle for change. The choice of venue, Merriweather Post Pavilion, is significant as it has a storied history of hosting major music events and serves as a cultural landmark in the region.

### Anticipation and Impact

As the festival date approaches, anticipation is building among fans and activists alike. The inclusion of high-profile artists is expected to draw significant attention, not only to the festival itself but also to the causes it champions. 

Morello's efforts to create a non-partisan space for discussion and celebration of social justice issues could serve as a model for future events. The festival's focus on community action highlights the potential of music to bring people together and inspire meaningful change.

### Conclusion

The "Power to the People" festival promises to be a landmark event in the music and activism landscape. With a lineup that features some of the most influential artists of our time, it aims to create a vibrant atmosphere of solidarity and hope. As the date approaches, both the music community and social activists will be watching closely to see how this initiative unfolds and the impact it may have on the broader conversation around peace and justice in America.
