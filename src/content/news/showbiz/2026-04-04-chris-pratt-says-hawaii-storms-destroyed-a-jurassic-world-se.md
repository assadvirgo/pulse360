## Chris Pratt Reveals Loss of 'Jurassic World' Set Due to Hawaii Storms

In a recent statement, actor Chris Pratt disclosed the unfortunate impact of severe storms that struck Hawaii last month, leading to the destruction of a significant piece of film history from the "Jurassic World" franchise. The storms, which were characterized by unprecedented rainfall, have left a lasting mark on the region and the film industry.

### The Impact of the Storms

The storms that swept through Hawaii were described as devastating, with reports indicating that they unleashed approximately 2 trillion gallons of rain. This extreme weather event resulted in widespread damage across the islands, affecting homes, infrastructure, and the natural environment. Among the casualties was a bungalow that served as the residence of Pratt's character, Owen Grady, in the 2015 film "Jurassic World," directed by Colin Trevorrow.

Pratt shared his sentiments regarding the loss during a recent interview, expressing his disappointment over the destruction of the set piece. He noted that a downed tree was responsible for the damage, symbolizing the broader impact of the storms on both the local community and the entertainment industry.

### A Piece of Cinematic History

The bungalow, which was part of the film's iconic set, held significance not only for the production team but also for fans of the franchise. "Jurassic World" was a major box office success, revitalizing interest in the beloved dinosaur saga that began with Steven Spielberg's original film in 1993. The set locations in Hawaii, particularly on the islands of Kauai and Oahu, have become synonymous with the franchise, drawing tourists and film enthusiasts alike.

The loss of this set piece highlights the vulnerability of film locations to natural disasters, a reality that filmmakers must increasingly consider in their planning and production processes. As climate change continues to influence weather patterns, the industry may need to adapt to ensure the preservation of such sites.

### Community Resilience

In the wake of the storms, local communities have demonstrated resilience and solidarity. Efforts to recover from the damage are underway, with residents and organizations coming together to support one another. The storms serve as a reminder of the importance of preparedness and the need for sustainable practices to mitigate the effects of extreme weather.

Pratt's acknowledgment of the loss serves to raise awareness about the broader implications of climate-related events, not only for the film industry but also for the communities that host these productions. As Hawaii continues to recover, the focus will be on rebuilding and restoring the natural beauty that makes the islands a sought-after destination for filmmakers and tourists alike.

### Conclusion

The destruction of the "Jurassic World" bungalow is a poignant reminder of the intersection between nature and the arts.
