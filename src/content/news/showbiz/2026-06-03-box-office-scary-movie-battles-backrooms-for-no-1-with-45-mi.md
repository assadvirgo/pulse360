## Box Office Showdown: 'Scary Movie' and 'Backrooms' Compete for Top Spot

As the weekend approaches, the domestic box office is set for a thrilling competition between two horror films: Paramount and Miramax's "Scary Movie" and A24's breakout hit "Backrooms." Both films are vying for the coveted No. 1 position, with "Scary Movie" projected to debut between $45 million and $50 million from approximately 3,400 theaters across North America.

### 'Scary Movie': A Return to Form

"Scary Movie," an R-rated comedy-horror film, marks a significant return for the franchise that has become synonymous with parodying popular horror tropes. The film's marketing has generated considerable buzz, appealing to both fans of the original series and new audiences. With its comedic take on horror, "Scary Movie" aims to attract a diverse crowd, particularly those looking for a lighthearted scare.

Industry analysts suggest that the film's strong opening could be bolstered by its established brand, which has a history of performing well at the box office. If ticket sales reach the higher end of projections, "Scary Movie" could secure a robust debut, setting the stage for a successful run.

### 'Backrooms': A Surprising Contender

On the other hand, "Backrooms" has emerged as a surprising contender in the horror genre. The film, which has garnered a strong following through word-of-mouth and social media buzz, taps into the contemporary fear of the unknown, utilizing a unique concept that resonates with audiences. A24, known for its distinctive and often critically acclaimed films, has positioned "Backrooms" as a fresh entry in the horror landscape.

The film's premise, which revolves around the unsettling experience of being trapped in an endless maze of empty rooms, has intrigued viewers, leading to high expectations for its box office performance. Analysts predict that "Backrooms" could also achieve significant earnings this weekend, potentially challenging "Scary Movie" for the top spot.

### 'Masters of the Universe': A Different Genre

In addition to the horror films, another title to watch is "Masters of the Universe," which is projected to start with around $33 million. While it may not be in direct competition with the horror films, its release adds another layer of complexity to the box office dynamics this weekend. The film aims to attract fans of the iconic franchise, leveraging nostalgia and the current trend of reboots in Hollywood.

### Conclusion

As the weekend unfolds, the box office battle between "Scary Movie" and "Backrooms" will be closely monitored by industry experts and fans alike. The outcome will not only reflect audience preferences but also the evolving landscape of the horror genre in cinema. With both films offering distinct experiences, the competition promises to be an intriguing showcase of creativity and audience engagement in the film industry.
