## Hungarian Composer Mihály Víg Reflects on Four Decades of Scoring Béla Tarr’s Films at Shanghai Masterclass

Mihály Víg, a prominent Hungarian composer, actor, and screenwriter, recently shared his insights and experiences from a remarkable career spanning over four decades at the 28th Shanghai International Film Festival. His masterclass followed a screening of the acclaimed film "The Turin Horse," a collaboration with renowned director Béla Tarr, with whom Víg has developed a profound creative partnership since 1984.

### A Lasting Partnership

Víg's relationship with Tarr has been a cornerstone of both their careers. The collaboration began when Tarr was an emerging filmmaker, and Víg's contributions have since become integral to the emotional and atmospheric depth of Tarr's films. Over the years, the duo has worked on several notable projects, including "Satantango," "Werckmeister Harmonies," and "The Turin Horse," each characterized by their unique narrative style and haunting musical scores.

During the masterclass, Víg reflected on the evolution of their artistic collaboration, highlighting how their shared vision has shaped the cinematic landscape of Hungarian cinema. He emphasized the importance of music in film, stating that it serves not only as a backdrop but as a vital component that enhances storytelling and evokes emotional responses from the audience.

### Insights on Film Scoring

Víg's approach to composing music for film is deeply rooted in his understanding of the narrative and visual elements. He described his process as one of immersion, where he engages with the film's themes and characters to create a score that resonates with the viewer's experience. This method has allowed him to craft music that complements Tarr's distinctive style, characterized by long takes and minimal dialogue.

In his discussion, Víg also addressed the challenges of scoring films, particularly the need to balance artistic expression with the director's vision. He noted that collaboration is key, as it fosters an environment where both the composer and the director can explore new ideas and push creative boundaries.

### Influence on Contemporary Cinema

Víg's work has not only influenced Hungarian cinema but has also garnered international recognition. His scores have been praised for their ability to transcend cultural barriers, connecting with audiences worldwide. The masterclass at the Shanghai International Film Festival served as a testament to his impact, attracting a diverse audience eager to learn from his experiences.

As the film industry continues to evolve, Víg remains committed to his craft, consistently seeking new ways to innovate and inspire. His reflections during the masterclass underscored the importance of collaboration in the arts, as well as the enduring power of music in enhancing the cinematic experience.

### Conclusion

Mihály Víg's masterclass at the Shanghai International Film Festival provided a rare glimpse into the mind of a composer whose work has significantly shaped the landscape of film music. As he continues to collaborate with Béla Tarr and other filmmakers, Víg's contributions will undoubtedly leave a lasting legacy in the world of cinema, inspiring future generations of artists to explore the intricate relationship between sound and storytelling.
