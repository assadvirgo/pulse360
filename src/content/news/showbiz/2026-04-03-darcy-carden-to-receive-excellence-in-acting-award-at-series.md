## D’Arcy Carden to Receive Excellence in Acting Award at SeriesFest

Emmy Award-nominated actress D’Arcy Carden is set to be honored with the prestigious “Excellence in Acting Award” at the upcoming SeriesFest, a prominent festival dedicated to celebrating the art of television. The 12th installment of SeriesFest will take place from May 6 to May 10, 2023, in Denver, Colorado.

### Recognition of Talent

The award acknowledges Carden's significant contributions to the television industry, highlighting her versatility and talent as an actress. Known for her compelling performances, Carden has garnered acclaim for her role in the critically acclaimed series "The Good Place," where she portrayed the lovable character Janet. Her ability to blend humor with emotional depth has made her a standout figure in contemporary television.

### Upcoming Projects

In addition to receiving this honor, Carden is also set to star in the upcoming Peacock series "The Five-Star Weekend," which is scheduled to premiere on July 16, 2023. This new project is anticipated to further showcase her range as an actress and solidify her status in the entertainment industry. The series, based on a novel, promises to deliver a mix of drama and comedy, aligning well with Carden's established strengths.

### SeriesFest: A Platform for Innovation

SeriesFest has become a vital platform for emerging and established creators in the television landscape. The festival not only celebrates outstanding performances but also serves as a venue for showcasing innovative storytelling and diverse voices in the industry. Carden's recognition at SeriesFest underscores the festival's commitment to honoring talent that pushes the boundaries of traditional television.

### Celebration Details

The celebration honoring Carden will take place on May 8, during a special event at SeriesFest. This occasion will bring together industry professionals, fans, and fellow artists to celebrate Carden's achievements and contributions to the field. The festival's atmosphere is expected to be one of camaraderie and appreciation, as attendees reflect on the evolving landscape of television and the artists who shape it.
