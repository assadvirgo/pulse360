## Gracie Abrams Announces New Album ‘Daughter From Hell’ Set for Summer Release

Gracie Abrams, the rising singer-songwriter, has officially announced her third studio album, titled "Daughter From Hell," which is scheduled for release on July 17, 2024. The album will be distributed by Interscope Records, marking another significant step in Abrams' burgeoning music career.

### Excitement and Anticipation

The announcement was made via Abrams' social media platforms, where she expressed her enthusiasm for the upcoming project. In her post, she wrote, “Whoa whoa whoa, freaking out. I am so ready for it to be years,” reflecting her eagerness and emotional connection to the album. This candid expression of excitement resonates with her growing fanbase, who have been eagerly awaiting new music from the artist since her last release.

### A Journey Through Music

Gracie Abrams first gained significant attention with her debut EP, "Minor," released in 2020. The project showcased her unique blend of introspective lyrics and melodic soundscapes, quickly establishing her as a voice to watch in the contemporary pop landscape. Her subsequent work, including the EP "This Is What It Feels Like," further solidified her reputation, earning critical acclaim and a loyal following.

"Daughter From Hell" is expected to delve deeper into the themes of personal growth, relationships, and self-discovery that have characterized Abrams' previous work. Fans are keen to see how her sound has evolved and what new narratives she will explore in this upcoming album.

### Collaborations and Production

While specific details regarding the collaborators and producers on "Daughter From Hell" have yet to be disclosed, Abrams has previously worked with notable figures in the music industry, including producers like Aaron Dessner of The National and fellow artist Charlie Puth. This collaborative spirit suggests that the upcoming album may feature a blend of innovative sounds and fresh perspectives.

As a young artist, Gracie Abrams has already made significant strides in the music industry, often drawing comparisons to established names while also carving out her own identity.
