## ‘Everytime’ Review: A Grieving Family Finds a Strange Path Towards Healing

Sandra Wollner's latest film, "Everytime," marks a significant entry into the realm of cinematic explorations of grief. This haunting narrative delves into the complexities of loss, time, and the human experience, offering an insightful look at how families navigate their sorrow.

### A Poignant Exploration of Grief

At the heart of "Everytime" is a family's struggle to cope with the death of a loved one. Wollner's direction captures the multifaceted nature of grief, illustrating how it can warp time and perception. The film portrays grief not merely as an emotional experience but as a force that alters reality itself. Viewers are invited to witness the characters’ journeys through various stages of mourning, which are depicted with a raw authenticity that resonates deeply.

### Temporal Distortions and Memory

One of the film's most striking elements is its exploration of time's fluidity in the context of grief. Wollner effectively conveys how moments of joy and sorrow can become intertwined, leading to a disorienting experience for those left behind. The narrative suggests that time can stretch, compress, or even become suspended, reflecting the psychological impact of loss. This thematic focus invites viewers to consider their own experiences with grief, prompting reflections on how memory can both preserve and distort the past.

### A Haunting Atmosphere

The film's visual and auditory elements contribute significantly to its haunting atmosphere. Wollner employs a meticulous aesthetic, using lighting and sound design to evoke a sense of unease and longing. The cinematography captures the nuances of the characters' emotional landscapes, enhancing the storytelling and immersing the audience in their world. This careful attention to detail creates a palpable tension that underscores the film's exploration of grief.

### Character Development and Performances

The performances in "Everytime" are commendable, with the cast delivering nuanced portrayals of individuals grappling with their loss. Each character embodies a different facet of grief, allowing for a rich tapestry of emotional experiences. The interactions among family members reveal the complexities of their relationships, showcasing how grief can both fracture and unite. As they navigate their pain, the characters embark on a journey toward healing, albeit one that is fraught with challenges.

### Conclusion: A Journey Towards Healing

"Everytime" stands as a testament to Sandra Wollner's ability to tackle profound themes with sensitivity and insight. The film's exploration of grief, time, and memory resonates on multiple levels, making it a poignant reflection on the human condition. Through its haunting narrative and compelling performances, "Everytime" invites audiences to confront their own experiences of loss and the strange, often unpredictable paths toward healing.

In a world where grief is a universal experience, Wollner's film serves as a reminder of the resilience of the human spirit and the importance of connection, even in the face of profound sorrow. As viewers leave the theater, they are left with a sense of contemplation, reflecting on the intricate ways in which time and memory shape our understanding of love and loss.
