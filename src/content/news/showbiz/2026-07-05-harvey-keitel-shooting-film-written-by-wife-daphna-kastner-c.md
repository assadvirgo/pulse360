## Harvey Keitel Discusses New Film and Concerns Over AI in Entertainment

Renowned actor Harvey Keitel is currently attending the Karlovy Vary Film Festival, where he is presenting a screening of Martin Scorsese’s classic film “Mean Streets.” During his time at the festival, Keitel shared insights about his latest project, a film written by his wife, Daphna Kastner. 

### New Project Excites Keitel

In an interview with Variety, Keitel expressed his enthusiasm for the new film, describing it as a project that "really excites" him. While details about the film remain under wraps, the collaboration with his wife marks a significant personal and professional milestone for the actor. Keitel's career spans several decades, and his involvement in a project that is both personal and innovative reflects his ongoing commitment to the craft of filmmaking.

### Concerns Over Artificial Intelligence in Film

In addition to discussing his new project, Keitel also addressed the growing influence of artificial intelligence (AI) in the entertainment industry. The actor pointed to the recent release of an AI-narrated audiobook of Homer’s “The Odyssey,” featuring the voice of veteran actor Michael Caine. Keitel expressed concern over the implications of AI technology in creative fields, emphasizing the potential dangers it poses to traditional artistry and storytelling.

The advent of AI in various sectors, including film and literature, has sparked a broader debate about the authenticity of creative work and the role of human artists. Keitel’s remarks resonate with a growing sentiment among industry professionals who are wary of AI's ability to replicate human creativity. He highlighted the importance of maintaining a balance between technological advancements and the irreplaceable value of human expression in art.
