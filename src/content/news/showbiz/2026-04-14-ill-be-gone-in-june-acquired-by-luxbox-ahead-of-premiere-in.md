## Luxbox Acquires ‘I’ll Be Gone in June’ Ahead of Cannes Premiere

In a significant move within the film industry, Paris-based sales company Luxbox has announced the acquisition of Katharina Rivilis’ drama “I’ll Be Gone in June.” The film has garnered attention as it is set to premiere in the prestigious Un Certain Regard section at the upcoming Cannes Film Festival.

### Overview of the Film

“I’ll Be Gone in June” tells the poignant story of Franny, a 16-year-old exchange student from Germany who arrives in a quiet desert town. The narrative explores themes of adolescence, cultural exchange, and the complexities of personal growth in an unfamiliar environment. The film promises to offer a unique perspective on the challenges faced by young individuals navigating new experiences.

Produced by notable filmmaker Wim Wenders, the film is expected to resonate with audiences, particularly those who appreciate character-driven narratives that delve into the intricacies of youth and identity. The involvement of Wenders, known for his acclaimed works in both German and international cinema, adds a layer of credibility and anticipation to the project.

### Distribution and Release

“I’ll Be Gone in June” is set to be distributed in Germany and Switzerland by DCM, a company recognized for its commitment to bringing diverse and compelling films to the forefront of the European market. The acquisition by Luxbox aligns with the company's strategy to promote innovative and thought-provoking cinema, further emphasizing their role in the global film landscape.

### Cannes Film Festival Significance

The Cannes Film Festival, particularly the Un Certain Regard section, is known for showcasing films that offer fresh perspectives and artistic innovation. The selection of “I’ll Be Gone in June” for this prestigious program underscores the film’s potential impact and the critical acclaim it has already begun to attract. The festival serves as a vital platform for filmmakers and distributors alike, often leading to increased visibility and opportunities for future projects.

As the film prepares for its debut in Cannes, industry insiders and audiences alike are keen to see how it will be received. The festival is renowned for its ability to elevate lesser-known films into the spotlight, potentially paving the way for wider distribution and recognition.

### Conclusion

The acquisition of “I’ll Be Gone in June” by Luxbox marks an important step for both the film and the sales company as they navigate the competitive landscape of international cinema. With its compelling storyline and the backing of esteemed producers, the film is poised to make a significant impact during its premiere at Cannes. As the festival approaches, anticipation continues to build around this promising new work, which seeks to capture the nuanced experiences of youth in a changing world.
