## Steven Soderbergh Discusses AI and New Documentary on John Lennon

Renowned filmmaker Steven Soderbergh has recently shared insights into his upcoming documentary, "John Lennon: The Last Interview," which is set to explore the final moments of the iconic musician's life through an unreleased interview recorded just hours before his tragic death. The project, developed in partnership with Meta, marks a significant intersection of art and technology, particularly in its incorporation of artificial intelligence.

### Exclusive Access to Unreleased Interview

Soderbergh was granted exclusive access to the final interview with John Lennon, which has remained largely unseen since it was recorded. The interview features Lennon in an intimate setting with his wife, Yoko Ono, and provides a poignant glimpse into the thoughts and feelings of one of music's most influential figures. This documentary aims not only to present Lennon's reflections but also to contextualize his legacy in today's world.

### Embracing Artificial Intelligence

In a recent discussion, Soderbergh articulated his "pro-choice" attitude toward artificial intelligence, suggesting that the technology should be embraced as a tool rather than feared. He emphasized the importance of using AI to enhance storytelling and creativity, rather than allowing it to replace the human element in filmmaking. "I owe people honesty," he stated, underscoring his commitment to transparency in the creative process.

Soderbergh's collaboration with Meta signifies a broader trend in the film industry, where AI is increasingly being utilized to facilitate various aspects of production, from scriptwriting to visual effects. The filmmaker believes that AI can serve as a powerful ally in the creative process, enabling artists to push the boundaries of their work while still maintaining the core of human expression.

### A New Era of Documentary Filmmaking

"John Lennon: The Last Interview" is poised to be a landmark documentary not only for its subject matter but also for its innovative approach to filmmaking. By integrating AI technologies, Soderbergh aims to create a more immersive and engaging experience for viewers. This project reflects a growing recognition within the industry of the potential benefits of AI, as filmmakers seek to navigate the challenges and opportunities presented by this rapidly evolving technology.

As Soderbergh prepares to unveil this documentary, he remains committed to honoring Lennon's legacy while also exploring the implications of modern technology in storytelling. The film is expected to resonate with both longtime fans of Lennon and new audiences, offering a fresh perspective on an artist whose influence continues to shape music and culture.

### Conclusion

Steven Soderbergh's "John Lennon: The Last Interview" represents a significant convergence of art and technology, highlighting the potential of AI in the creative realm. As the documentary approaches its release, it promises to provide not only a glimpse into the life of John Lennon but also a thoughtful exploration of the evolving landscape of filmmaking in the age of artificial intelligence.
