## Bentonville Film Festival Celebrates Top Winners in 12th Edition

The Bentonville Film Foundation, in partnership with Walmart and Coca-Cola, recently concluded the 12th edition of the Bentonville Film Festival. The festival, which aims to promote diversity and inclusion in film, held its awards gala on June 20, where it recognized outstanding contributions in the industry.

### Best Narrative Award Goes to "If I Go Will They Miss Me"

Walter Thompson-Hernández's poignant film, "If I Go Will They Miss Me," was awarded the Best Narrative prize. The film has garnered attention for its sensitive exploration of themes surrounding identity and belonging, resonating with audiences and critics alike. This recognition at the Bentonville Film Festival underscores the importance of storytelling that reflects diverse experiences and perspectives.

### "Jane Elliott Against the World" Wins Best Documentary

In the documentary category, Judd Ehrlich's "Jane Elliott Against the World" emerged as a standout winner. The film chronicles the life and work of Jane Elliott, a prominent educator and activist known for her anti-racism teachings. Elliott's impactful approach to confronting prejudice and fostering understanding has made her a significant figure in discussions about race and equality. The documentary not only highlights her contributions but also sparks critical conversations about social justice.

### A Celebration of Diversity in Film

The Bentonville Film Festival has established itself as a vital platform for underrepresented voices in the film industry. Since its inception, the festival has focused on showcasing films that promote diversity and inclusion, providing a space for filmmakers from various backgrounds to share their stories. This year’s winners exemplify the festival’s commitment to amplifying diverse narratives.

The awards gala was marked by a vibrant atmosphere, celebrating not just the winners but the collective efforts of all filmmakers who participated. The festival serves as a reminder of the power of cinema to foster empathy and understanding, bridging gaps between different communities.

### Looking Ahead

As the festival wraps up another successful year, the Bentonville Film Foundation continues to look towards the future. Plans for next year’s event are already underway, with hopes of expanding its reach and impact. The foundation remains dedicated to its mission of promoting diversity in film, encouraging filmmakers to explore and express their unique perspectives.

In conclusion, the Bentonville Film Festival's recognition of "If I Go Will They Miss Me" and "Jane Elliott Against the World" not only highlights exceptional filmmaking but also reinforces the festival's role as a champion of diversity in the arts. As the film industry evolves, festivals like Bentonville play a crucial role in shaping a more inclusive landscape.
