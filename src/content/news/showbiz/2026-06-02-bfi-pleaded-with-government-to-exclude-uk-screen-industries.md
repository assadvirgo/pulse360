## BFI Urges Government to Exclude UK Screen Industries from US Trade Deal

The British Film Institute (BFI) has made a significant appeal to the UK government, urging ministers to exclude the British film and television industries from any trade agreements negotiated with the United States under President Donald Trump. This request, articulated in a policy document submitted to the British Foreign Office in May 2025, underscores the BFI's concerns regarding the potential detrimental impact such a deal could have on the UK’s creative sectors.

### Concerns Over Trade Deal Implications

In its communication, the BFI expressed fears that including the screen industries in a trade agreement with the U.S. could lead to "fundamental harm" to the UK's film and television landscape. The institute emphasized that the unique characteristics and needs of the British creative sector must be safeguarded in any international negotiations. 

The BFI's position reflects a broader apprehension within the UK creative community about the implications of trade agreements that might prioritize commercial interests over cultural integrity. The institute's document outlines specific concerns regarding market access, regulatory frameworks, and the protection of intellectual property rights, all of which could be jeopardized if UK screen industries are subjected to U.S. trade practices.

### The Importance of the UK Screen Industries

The UK film and television industries are not only vital to the nation's cultural identity but also play a significant role in its economy. The sector has seen substantial growth in recent years, contributing billions to the UK economy and providing thousands of jobs. The BFI's lobbying efforts highlight the importance of maintaining a robust and independent creative sector that can thrive without the pressures of international trade agreements that may not align with its interests.

### Government Response and Future Considerations

As the UK government navigates its relationship with the United States, the BFI's plea raises important questions about the balance between economic growth and cultural preservation. While trade deals can offer new opportunities for businesses, they must also consider the long-term implications for industries that are crucial to national identity and heritage.

The government has yet to publicly respond to the BFI's request. However, the ongoing discussions surrounding trade agreements will likely continue to evoke strong reactions from various stakeholders within the creative industries. The BFI's proactive stance serves as a reminder of the complexities involved in negotiating trade deals that encompass not only economic factors but also cultural considerations.

### Conclusion

The BFI's appeal to exclude the UK screen industries from any trade deal with the U.S. reflects a growing concern about the potential risks associated with such agreements. As negotiations progress, it will be essential for the government to weigh the economic benefits against the potential threats to the cultural landscape of the UK. The outcome of this dialogue will have lasting implications for the future of the British film and television sectors.
