## Oscar and Emmy-Winning Director Daniel Junge Collaborates with Panama’s Abner Benaim on New Documentary

In a significant development for the documentary film landscape, acclaimed U.S. director Daniel Junge, known for his Oscar-winning work on "Saving Face," has joined forces with Panamanian filmmaker Abner Benaim. Junge will serve as the executive producer for Benaim's latest project, which is still untitled and focuses on the life of Jaime Alemán, a prominent figure in Panama.

### A Notable Collaboration

Daniel Junge's involvement in the project underscores the growing recognition of Panamanian cinema on the global stage. Benaim, who has represented Panama at the Academy Awards on three occasions, brings a wealth of experience and cultural insight to the documentary. His previous works, including "Invasion" and "Plaza Catedral," have garnered critical acclaim, establishing him as a leading voice in Latin American filmmaking.

### Spotlight on Jaime Alemán

The documentary centers on Jaime Alemán, a multifaceted individual whose career spans law, diplomacy, and business. Alemán is celebrated not only for his professional achievements but also for his extensive travels, making him one of the world's most accomplished globetrotters. The film aims to explore Alemán's unique perspectives and experiences, providing viewers with an intimate look at his life and the broader cultural context of Panama.

### Significance of the Project

This collaboration highlights the increasing synergy between American and Latin American filmmakers, fostering cross-cultural storytelling that resonates with diverse audiences. Junge's expertise in documentary filmmaking, combined with Benaim's deep understanding of Panamanian culture, promises to create a compelling narrative that transcends geographical boundaries.

As the documentary progresses, it is expected to shed light on not only Alemán's individual journey but also the rich history and vibrant culture of Panama. The film aims to capture the essence of a country that has played a pivotal role in global trade and diplomacy, particularly through the Panama Canal.
