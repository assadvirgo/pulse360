## Rif Hutton, Acclaimed Actor Known for Roles in 'Doogie Howser, M.D.' and 'General Hospital,' Passes Away at 73

Rif Hutton, a versatile actor celebrated for his extensive work in television, passed away on April 18, 2023, at the age of 73. His family confirmed that he died at his home in Pasadena, California, after a yearlong battle with glioblastoma, an aggressive form of brain cancer.

### A Distinguished Career in Television

Hutton's career spanned several decades, during which he became a familiar face on American television. He is perhaps best known for his role in the beloved series "Doogie Howser, M.D.," where he played the character of Dr. David Howser, the father of the show's titular character, portrayed by Neil Patrick Harris. The series, which aired from 1989 to 1993, garnered critical acclaim and remains a significant part of television history.

In addition to his work on "Doogie Howser," Hutton made notable appearances in other popular series, including "JAG" and "General Hospital." His performances showcased his ability to embody a range of characters, contributing to the rich tapestry of American television drama.
