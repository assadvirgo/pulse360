## Jon Stewart Mocks Donald Trump’s Bill Signing on Psychedelic Drug Treatments

On a recent episode of "The Daily Show," comedian Jon Stewart took the opportunity to humorously critique former President Donald Trump’s behavior during a bill signing event. The segment aired on Monday night, where Stewart highlighted the unusual nature of Trump’s actions while signing legislation aimed at expediting the FDA approval process for novel psychedelic drug treatments designed for veterans.

### Context of the Bill

The bill in question is significant as it seeks to address the mental health crisis among veterans, many of whom suffer from conditions such as PTSD. By fast-tracking the approval of psychedelic therapies, proponents hope to provide alternative treatment options that could be more effective than traditional methods. The initiative has garnered attention for its potential to revolutionize mental health care for those who have served in the military.

### Stewart's Commentary

Stewart opened the segment by acknowledging the importance of the bill and the positive impact it could have on veterans. However, he quickly pivoted to a comedic critique of Trump’s demeanor during the signing. "Did you see him? He looked like he was on something," Stewart quipped, suggesting that Trump’s behavior was reminiscent of someone under the influence of psychedelics. 

The comedian went on to joke, "Have you ever really looked at your signature, on weed?" This remark not only served to poke fun at Trump’s unusual signing style but also highlighted the broader cultural conversation surrounding psychedelics and their potential therapeutic benefits.
