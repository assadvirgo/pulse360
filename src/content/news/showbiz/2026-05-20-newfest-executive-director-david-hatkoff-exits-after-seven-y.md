## NewFest Executive Director David Hatkoff Steps Down After Seven Years

David Hatkoff, the executive director of NewFest, New York City's prominent LGBTQ film festival, has announced his departure after a notable seven-year tenure. His leadership has been instrumental in shaping the festival into a significant cultural event, showcasing diverse voices and stories within the LGBTQ community.

### Transition in Leadership

In light of Hatkoff's exit, Lesli Klainberg, a seasoned figure in the film industry and former executive director of NewFest, has been appointed as the interim executive director. Klainberg also serves as the president of Film at Lincoln Center, bringing a wealth of experience to the role during this transitional period. Her familiarity with NewFest and its mission is expected to provide stability as the organization seeks a permanent replacement.

### Search for a Permanent Replacement

The search for Hatkoff's successor is being conducted by the Kevin Chase Executive Search Group, which has been tasked with identifying candidates who can continue the festival's legacy while also innovating for its future. The search is expected to be national in scope, reflecting the festival's prominence and the importance of the role in the LGBTQ film community.

### Impact of Hatkoff's Leadership

Under Hatkoff's direction, NewFest has expanded its programming, increased audience engagement, and fostered a greater sense of community among filmmakers and audiences alike. His efforts have not only elevated the festival's profile but have also contributed to the broader dialogue surrounding LGBTQ representation in film. 

### Looking Ahead

As NewFest prepares for its next chapter, the organization is poised to continue its mission of celebrating LGBTQ cinema and supporting filmmakers from diverse backgrounds. The interim leadership of Klainberg, combined with the ongoing search for a new executive director, signals a commitment to maintaining the festival's high standards and expanding its impact in the years to come.

The festival community and supporters will be watching closely as NewFest navigates this transition, eager to see how it will evolve under new leadership while honoring the foundation laid by Hatkoff.
