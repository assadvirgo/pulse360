## Fernanda Torres Joins Willem Dafoe in Dystopian Film 'Cuddle'

Brazilian actress Fernanda Torres, renowned for her Oscar-nominated role in the international feature film "I’m Still Here," is set to star alongside four-time Oscar nominee Willem Dafoe in the upcoming dystopian feature "Cuddle," directed by Bárbara Paz. This collaboration marks an exciting intersection of talent from Brazil and Hollywood, further highlighting the global nature of contemporary cinema.

### A Unique Concept

"Cuddle" is set in a near-future metropolis where the concept of intimacy has been commodified, reflecting a society where emotional connections are traded like currency. The film centers around the character Dante, portrayed by Dafoe, who works as a professional cuddler. This premise raises thought-provoking questions about the nature of human relationships in an increasingly transactional world.

### Fernanda Torres: A Rising Star

Fernanda Torres has garnered international acclaim for her performance in "I’m Still Here," which won the Academy Award for Best International Feature in 2025. Her portrayal in the film has been described as stirring and impactful, establishing her as a formidable presence in the film industry. Torres's involvement in "Cuddle" is expected to bring a depth of emotion and authenticity to the film, complementing Dafoe's established reputation for nuanced performances.

Bárbara Paz, known for her innovative storytelling and unique directorial style, is at the helm of "Cuddle." Her previous works have often explored complex themes related to human emotion and societal constructs, making her an ideal choice for a film that delves into the intricacies of intimacy and connection. Paz's vision for "Cuddle" promises to challenge audiences while providing an engaging narrative that resonates with contemporary issues.
