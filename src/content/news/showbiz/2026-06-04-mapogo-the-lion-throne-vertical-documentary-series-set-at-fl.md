## New Wildlife Documentary Series "Mapogo: The Lion Throne" Set to Launch on FlareFlow

In an innovative move for wildlife storytelling, COL Group's FlareFlow has announced a partnership with Singapore-based Bomanbridge Media to produce "Mapogo: The Lion Throne," a premium vertical-format documentary series. This groundbreaking series is set to premiere in late Q3 2026 and will be available in over 200 countries, marking a significant step in the evolution of documentary filmmaking.

### A New Format for Wildlife Documentaries

"Mapogo: The Lion Throne" is notable for its vertical format, which is designed to cater to the viewing habits of modern audiences who increasingly consume content on mobile devices. This approach not only enhances the visual experience but also aligns with current trends in digital media consumption. The series promises to deliver an immersive experience that captures the majesty and complexity of lion life in the wild.

### Filming and Production Insights

The documentary has been filmed over several years, allowing the production team to capture the intricate behaviors and social dynamics of the Mapogo coalition, a group of lions known for their dominance in South Africa's Sabi Sands Game Reserve. The extended filming period has enabled the creators to document rare and intimate moments in the lives of these majestic animals, providing viewers with a unique perspective on their struggles and triumphs in the wild.

### Unveiling at the Vertical Media Summit

The announcement of "Mapogo: The Lion Throne" was made during the Vertical Media Summit held in Los Angeles, an event that showcases the latest trends in vertical video content. The series is expected to resonate with both wildlife enthusiasts and general audiences, as it combines stunning visuals with compelling storytelling. The collaboration between FlareFlow and Bomanbridge Media is indicative of a growing trend in the industry, where traditional formats are being reimagined to suit new platforms and viewer preferences.

### Global Reach and Impact

With its release planned for late 2026, "Mapogo: The Lion Throne" aims to reach a global audience, promoting awareness of wildlife conservation issues while entertaining viewers. The series is expected to contribute to ongoing discussions about the importance of preserving natural habitats and the challenges faced by apex predators like lions. By showcasing the lives of the Mapogo lions, the documentary will not only highlight their role in the ecosystem but also engage audiences in a dialogue about conservation efforts.

### Conclusion

As the landscape of media continues to evolve, "Mapogo: The Lion Throne" represents a significant advancement in the way wildlife stories are told. With its innovative vertical format and a focus on the lives of one of nature's most fascinating creatures, this documentary series is poised to make a lasting impact on viewers around the world. The collaboration between FlareFlow and Bomanbridge Media underscores a commitment to high-quality storytelling that is both visually stunning and socially relevant, setting a new standard for wildlife documentaries in the digital age.
