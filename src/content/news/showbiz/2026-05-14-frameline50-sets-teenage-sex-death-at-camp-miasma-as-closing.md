## Frameline50 Announces Closing Night Film and Honoree

The Frameline San Francisco International LGBTQ+ Film Festival, celebrating its landmark 50th edition, has officially announced that "Teenage Sex & Death at Camp Miasma" will serve as its closing night film. This highly anticipated screening will take place on June 27, 2026, following the film's recent world premiere at the Cannes Film Festival.

### A Milestone Year for Frameline

Frameline has long been recognized as a pivotal platform for LGBTQ+ cinema, showcasing diverse voices and stories that resonate with audiences worldwide. The festival's 50th anniversary marks a significant milestone in its history, reflecting decades of commitment to promoting and celebrating LGBTQ+ filmmakers and narratives. 

### "Teenage Sex & Death at Camp Miasma"

"Teenage Sex & Death at Camp Miasma," written and directed by Jane Schoenbrun, has garnered attention for its unique storytelling and bold themes. The film explores the complexities of adolescence, identity, and the often tumultuous journey of self-discovery during formative years. Its debut at Cannes has set high expectations for its festival circuit, and its selection as the closing film for Frameline50 underscores its relevance and appeal within the LGBTQ+ community.

### Honoring Jane Schoenbrun

In addition to the film's screening, Jane Schoenbrun will be honored with the Queer Lens Award during the festival. This accolade recognizes her significant contributions to LGBTQ+ cinema and her role in amplifying queer narratives through her work. Schoenbrun's previous projects have been noted for their innovative approaches and have sparked important conversations about representation in film.

### Festival Details

Frameline50 will take place from June 17 to June 27, 2026, in San Francisco, featuring a diverse lineup of films, panels, and events that celebrate LGBTQ+ culture and artistry. Attendees can expect a rich program that includes feature films, documentaries, and short films, all aimed at fostering dialogue and understanding within the community.

### Conclusion

As Frameline prepares to celebrate its 50th edition, the selection of "Teenage Sex & Death at Camp Miasma" as the closing night film and the recognition of Jane Schoenbrun with the Queer Lens Award highlight the festival's ongoing mission to uplift and support LGBTQ+ voices in film. This year's festival promises to be a memorable event, showcasing the vibrancy and diversity of queer storytelling in cinema.
