## Sarajevo’s CineLink Unveils Co-Production Market & Drama Sections Lineup

The Sarajevo Film Festival, a prominent cultural event in the Balkans, has announced the final selections for its CineLink Co-Production Market and CineLink Drama sections. This year's lineup includes a diverse array of projects, emphasizing the festival's commitment to fostering international collaboration in the film industry.

### Final Selections Announced

CineLink has confirmed a total of 16 feature film projects and eight drama series projects for this year's market. The selected works showcase a variety of genres and themes, reflecting the rich tapestry of storytelling from the region and beyond. Notably, a significant portion of the projects is directed by women, highlighting the festival's dedication to promoting gender equality in the film industry.

### New Partnership with Cannes’ Critics’ Week

In addition to the project announcements, CineLink has revealed a new partnership with the prestigious Critics' Week at the Cannes Film Festival. This collaboration aims to enhance the visibility of emerging filmmakers and their projects on an international stage. As part of this partnership, CineLink will introduce a new award worth €10,000, which will be presented to one of the selected projects. This initiative is designed to support the development of innovative cinematic works and to encourage filmmakers to pursue their creative visions.

### Significance of the Co-Production Market

The CineLink Co-Production Market serves as a vital platform for filmmakers, producers, and industry professionals to connect and explore potential collaborations. By facilitating co-productions, the market aims to bridge gaps between different film industries, allowing for the sharing of resources and expertise. This year’s selection reflects a growing interest in co-productions, which are increasingly seen as a means to enhance the quality and reach of films.

### Focus on Women Directors

The emphasis on women directors within this year’s lineup aligns with a broader movement in the film industry to promote diversity and inclusion. By showcasing the talents of female filmmakers, CineLink is contributing to a more balanced representation in cinema, which has historically been dominated by male voices. This initiative not only enriches the narrative landscape but also inspires future generations of filmmakers.

### Conclusion

As the Sarajevo Film Festival continues to evolve, its CineLink initiative remains a cornerstone of its programming. The combination of a robust project lineup and strategic partnerships, such as the one with Cannes’ Critics’ Week, underscores the festival's role as a key player in the global film industry. With a focus on collaboration and inclusivity, CineLink is poised to make a significant impact on the future of cinema in the region and beyond.
