## Filming Underway for 'A Land Remembered' Series in Florida

Filming has officially commenced in Florida for the highly anticipated television adaptation of "A Land Remembered," a best-selling historical novel by author Patrick D. Smith. This ambitious project, which boasts a budget of $25 million, is being independently produced with significant backing from the state of Florida.

### A Rich Literary Heritage

Published in 1984, "A Land Remembered" chronicles the life of a Florida cracker family over several generations, capturing the essence of the state's history and its transformation from wilderness to a modern society. The novel has garnered a dedicated following and is considered a staple in Florida literature, making it a fitting choice for adaptation into a television series.

### Notable Cast and Crew

The series features a talented cast, including Philip Ettinger, known for his roles in "First Reformed" and "Bupkis," alongside Lily McInerny, who gained recognition from Hulu's "Tell Me Lies" and "Palm Trees and Power Lines." William Catlett, celebrated for his performances in "A Thousand and One" and "Abigail," rounds out the principal cast. The involvement of such actors suggests a commitment to delivering a compelling narrative that resonates with both fans of the book and new audiences alike.

The series is being written and directed by a team that aims to honor the source material while also bringing a fresh perspective to the screen. Specific details about the creative team have yet to be disclosed, but their vision will be crucial in translating Smith's rich storytelling to a visual format.

### Florida's Role in Production

The production's backing from Florida is indicative of the state's growing investment in the film and television industry. Local government initiatives have been implemented to attract filmmakers, which not only boosts the economy but also highlights Florida's diverse landscapes and cultural history. "A Land Remembered" is expected to showcase the state's natural beauty, further enhancing its appeal as a filming location.

### Anticipation and Impact

As filming progresses, anticipation is building among fans of the novel and the broader television audience. The adaptation of "A Land Remembered" represents not only a significant cultural project but also an opportunity to explore themes of family, resilience, and the enduring connection to the land. 

The series is poised to contribute to the ongoing conversation about Florida's identity, particularly in the context of its historical narratives. With the combination of a beloved literary work, a skilled cast, and the support of the state, "A Land Remembered" aims to leave a lasting impact on both viewers and the local film industry.
