## Kristin Scott Thomas and Kurt Russell to Receive Crystal Nymph Awards at 65th Monte-Carlo Television Festival

The Monte-Carlo Television Festival has announced that acclaimed actors Kristin Scott Thomas and Kurt Russell will be honored with the prestigious Crystal Nymph Award at its upcoming 65th edition. This event, which celebrates excellence in television and recognizes the contributions of significant figures in the industry, will take place in June 2026 in the glamorous setting of Monte-Carlo.

### A Celebration of Career Achievements

The Crystal Nymph Award is bestowed upon individuals who have demonstrated outstanding career achievements and made a lasting impact on the creative industry. Both Scott Thomas and Russell have enjoyed illustrious careers, marked by a diverse range of roles that have captivated audiences worldwide.

Kristin Scott Thomas, a British actress known for her remarkable performances in films such as "The English Patient" and "Four Weddings and a Funeral," has also made significant contributions to television. Her ability to traverse between film and television has showcased her versatility and depth as an actress. 

Kurt Russell, an American actor celebrated for his roles in iconic films like "Escape from New York" and "Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2," has also made notable appearances in television series. His career spans several decades, during which he has garnered a loyal fan base and critical acclaim for his work.

### The Monte-Carlo Television Festival

The Monte-Carlo Television Festival, established in 1961, is one of the most prestigious events in the television industry. It attracts international stars, producers, and industry professionals who gather to celebrate the best in television programming. The festival features screenings, panel discussions, and awards ceremonies that highlight the achievements of both established and emerging talent.

In addition to the Crystal Nymph Awards, the festival showcases a wide array of television content from various genres and countries, reflecting the diversity and creativity of the global television landscape.
