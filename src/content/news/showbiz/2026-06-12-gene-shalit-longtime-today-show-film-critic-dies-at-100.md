## Gene Shalit, Iconic Film Critic of ‘The Today Show,’ Passes Away at 100

Gene Shalit, the beloved film critic known for his distinctive style and engaging personality, has died at the age of 100. His family confirmed the news to NBC News, stating that he "passed away peacefully today after 100 years of an amazing life." Shalit's contributions to television and film criticism have left an indelible mark on the industry and the audiences who grew up watching him.

### A Career Spanning Four Decades

Shalit began his tenure on NBC's "The Today Show" in 1970, where he would remain for 40 years until his retirement in 2010. His unique approach to film criticism combined humor, insight, and a flair for the theatrical, making him a standout figure in the world of entertainment journalism. With his trademark bushy hair and mustache, Shalit became a recognizable presence on morning television, captivating viewers with his engaging reviews and lively interviews with filmmakers and actors.

### Impact on Film Criticism

Throughout his career, Shalit reviewed thousands of films and books, often delivering his critiques in a light-hearted yet informative manner. His ability to connect with audiences was evident as he often infused his segments with playful puns and memorable catchphrases. This approach not only entertained viewers but also educated them about the art of filmmaking and storytelling.

Shalit's influence extended beyond just film reviews; he was also known for his interviews with some of the biggest names in Hollywood. His engaging style often led to candid moments that revealed the personalities behind the films, further endearing him to audiences.

### Legacy and Remembrance

As news of his passing spreads, tributes from colleagues and fans alike are pouring in, celebrating Shalit’s contributions to television and film. Many remember him not just as a critic but as a pioneer who helped shape the landscape of entertainment journalism. His ability to bridge the gap between critics and the general public made him a beloved figure in American culture.

Shalit’s legacy is also marked by his commitment to the craft of criticism. He was known for his thoughtful analysis and his ability to articulate the emotional and artistic elements of films, encouraging audiences to appreciate cinema on a deeper level. 

### Conclusion

Gene Shalit leaves behind a rich legacy that will continue to inspire future generations of film critics and enthusiasts. His unique voice and perspective in the world of entertainment will be missed, but his contributions to the field will endure. As fans and colleagues reflect on his remarkable life, they celebrate not only the critic but also the man who brought joy and insight to countless viewers over the decades.
