## "Salmokji: Whispering Water" Dominates South Korean Box Office in Second Weekend

The local horror-thriller "Salmokji: Whispering Water" has successfully maintained its position as the leading film at the South Korean box office during the weekend of April 17–19. According to data released by KOBIS, the tracking service operated by the Korean Film Council, the film generated an impressive $3.3 million from 472,121 admissions, securing a commanding 50% share of the weekend's total box office earnings.

## Continuing Success of "Salmokji: Whispering Water"

"Salmokji: Whispering Water," which has captivated audiences with its chilling narrative and suspenseful elements, continues to resonate well with viewers. The film's ability to attract a significant audience in its second weekend is indicative of its strong word-of-mouth and critical reception. The horror-thriller genre has seen a resurgence in popularity in South Korea, and "Salmokji" appears to be at the forefront of this trend.

The film's storyline, which intertwines elements of folklore and contemporary horror, has been praised for its originality and execution. As audiences increasingly seek engaging and immersive cinematic experiences, "Salmokji" has successfully tapped into this demand, further solidifying its status as a box office hit.

## The South Korean Box Office Landscape

The success of "Salmokji: Whispering Water" comes at a time when the South Korean film industry is gradually recovering from the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic. With restrictions easing and audiences returning to theaters, local productions have begun to thrive once again. The film's performance reflects a growing trend of domestic films gaining traction over international releases, as viewers show a preference for local storytelling and cultural representation.

In recent years, South Korean cinema has gained international acclaim, with films like "Parasite" and "Train to Busan" achieving global recognition. This trend has encouraged local filmmakers to explore diverse genres, including horror, which has traditionally been a popular choice among South Korean audiences.

## Future Prospects

As "Salmokji: Whispering Water" continues to draw in viewers, industry analysts are keenly observing its performance in the coming weeks. Should it maintain its momentum, the film could set new benchmarks for local horror films in terms of box office revenue and audience engagement.

With a strong opening weekend and a solid second weekend performance, "Salmokji" may pave the way for more innovative horror films in South Korea. The film's success could inspire filmmakers to explore the genre further, potentially leading to a new wave of horror cinema that resonates with both domestic and international audiences.
