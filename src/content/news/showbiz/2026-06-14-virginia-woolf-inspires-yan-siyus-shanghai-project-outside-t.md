## Virginia Woolf Inspires Yan Siyu’s Shanghai Project ‘Outside the Room of My Own’

The Shanghai International Film Festival (SIFF) is currently showcasing a work-in-progress cut of "Outside the Room of My Own," the debut feature film by Chinese filmmaker Yan Siyu. This project has garnered attention not only for its narrative but also for its literary inspiration, drawing from the themes and ideas presented by the renowned British author Virginia Woolf.

### A Journey of Self-Discovery

"Outside the Room of My Own" centers on the character Yuan Chengge, a writer who finds herself grappling with burnout amid the bustling life of Beijing. The film explores her journey as she travels to Chengdu and subsequently to a village, seeking solace and clarity. This narrative arc reflects Woolf's own exploration of the complexities of a woman's creative life, particularly in a world that often imposes societal constraints.

Yan Siyu's choice to reference Woolf is significant, as the author is celebrated for her contributions to feminist literature and her advocacy for women's independence in both life and art. The film's title itself echoes Woolf's famous essay "A Room of One's Own," which argues for the necessity of personal space and financial independence for women writers. 

### The Filmmaker's Vision

Yan Siyu, who has previously worked on various short films and documentaries, aims to create a poignant portrayal of a woman's struggle for identity and artistic freedom. The film's protagonist, Yuan, embodies the modern challenges faced by women in the creative industry, particularly in the context of contemporary Chinese society. 

In an interview, Yan expressed her desire to connect with audiences on a deeper level, stating, "I want to explore the internal and external conflicts that women face in their pursuit of creativity and self-expression." The film aims to resonate with viewers who may have experienced similar struggles, making it a relatable piece for many.

### Reception at the Shanghai International Film Festival

The work-in-progress cut of "Outside the Room of My Own" has been well-received at the SIFF Project, where it is being presented to industry professionals and audiences alike. The festival serves as a platform for emerging filmmakers to showcase their work and gain valuable feedback, which is crucial for the film's development before its official release.

The presence of Lucie Zhang in the lead role adds to the film's appeal, as she is recognized for her compelling performances in previous projects. Her portrayal of Yuan Chengge is expected to bring depth and nuance to the character's journey, further enhancing the film's exploration of its central themes.

### Conclusion

As Yan Siyu's "Outside the Room of My Own" continues to evolve, it stands as a testament to the enduring influence of Virginia Woolf on contemporary storytelling. The film not only aims to entertain but also to provoke thought and discussion about the challenges faced by women in creative fields. As it progresses through the festival circuit, it will be interesting to see how the final product resonates with audiences both in China and beyond.
