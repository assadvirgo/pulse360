## TelevisaUnivision Announces Leadership Change Ahead of TV Upfronts

In a significant shift within its advertising sales division, Spanish-language media giant TelevisaUnivision has appointed John Kozack as the new head of ad sales, replacing Tim Naividad. This unexpected change comes just weeks before the television industry's annual upfront marketplace, a crucial period for networks to secure advertising commitments for the upcoming season.

### Leadership Transition

Tim Naividad, who had been leading the ad sales team since June 2025, has been succeeded by Kozack, a seasoned professional with extensive experience within the company. Kozack's promotion reflects TelevisaUnivision's strategic intent to bolster its advertising capabilities as the competitive landscape of television continues to evolve.

This leadership change is particularly noteworthy given the timing, as the upfronts are a pivotal moment for television networks. During this period, advertisers negotiate and finalize their advertising budgets for the next season, making it essential for networks to present strong sales leadership and innovative strategies.

### The Importance of the Upfront Marketplace

The upfront marketplace is a key event in the television industry, where networks showcase their programming and secure advertising commitments from brands. This year, the stakes are high as networks vie for advertising dollars amid a rapidly changing media environment, characterized by the rise of digital platforms and shifting viewer habits.

TelevisaUnivision, which has positioned itself as a leader in Spanish-language media, is expected to leverage Kozack's experience to enhance its offerings and attract advertisers looking to reach diverse audiences. With the growing importance of multicultural marketing, the company aims to capitalize on its unique position in the market.

### Kozack's Background

John Kozack has been with TelevisaUnivision for several years, holding various roles that have equipped him with a deep understanding of the company's operations and the broader media landscape. His internal promotion suggests that the company is prioritizing continuity and internal expertise during a critical time.

Kozack's appointment may signal a shift in strategy as TelevisaUnivision seeks to adapt to the changing dynamics of advertising and consumer behavior. His familiarity with the company's goals and challenges could prove advantageous as he navigates the complexities of the upcoming upfronts.

As TelevisaUnivision prepares for the upfronts, the company faces the dual challenge of maintaining its competitive edge while responding to the evolving needs of advertisers. The leadership change at the top of the ad sales division is a clear indication of the company's commitment to strengthening its market position.
