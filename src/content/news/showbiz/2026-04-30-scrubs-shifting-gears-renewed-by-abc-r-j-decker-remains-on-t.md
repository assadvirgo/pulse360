## ABC Renews 'Scrubs' and 'Shifting Gears' for Upcoming Seasons

In a recent announcement, ABC has confirmed the renewal of two of its comedy series, "Scrubs" and "Shifting Gears," for the 2026-27 television season. This decision comes as part of the network's strategy to bolster its scripted programming lineup, particularly in the comedy genre.

### Renewal Details

The revival of "Scrubs," which originally aired from 2001 to 2010, will be officially considered its second season following its return to the airwaves. The series, known for its unique blend of humor and drama set in a hospital environment, has retained a loyal fan base over the years. The latest season aims to capture both long-time viewers and new audiences, continuing the storylines of beloved characters while introducing fresh narratives.

On the other hand, "Shifting Gears," a sitcom featuring Tim Allen, will enter its third season. The show has garnered attention for its comedic take on family dynamics and the challenges of modern living. Tim Allen's return to the sitcom format has been well-received, with audiences appreciating his signature style of humor.

### Current Programming Landscape

The renewal of these two series marks a significant moment for ABC, as they have become the remaining scripted comedies on the network. With the landscape of television continuously evolving, networks are increasingly selective about which shows to renew. The decision to continue "Scrubs" and "Shifting Gears" reflects ABC's confidence in their ability to attract viewers and generate ratings.

However, the future of another ABC scripted series, "R.J. Decker," remains uncertain. As the network evaluates its programming strategy, "R.J. Decker" is currently on the bubble, leaving fans and industry observers awaiting further developments regarding its fate.

### Industry Implications

The renewals of "Scrubs" and "Shifting Gears" come at a time when many networks are reassessing their content strategies amidst changing viewer habits and the rise of streaming platforms. Comedies have historically played a vital role in network programming, and ABC's commitment to these series may signal a broader trend of reaffirming traditional sitcom formats in an increasingly competitive market.

As networks navigate the complexities of viewer preferences and advertising revenue, the success of these renewed series will be closely monitored. The ability of "Scrubs" and "Shifting Gears" to resonate with audiences will likely influence ABC's future programming decisions and its overall approach to scripted content.

### Conclusion

The renewal of "Scrubs" and "Shifting Gears" by ABC highlights the network's dedication to maintaining a diverse and engaging comedy lineup. As the television landscape continues to evolve, the performance of these series will be crucial in shaping the future of scripted programming on the network. Fans of both shows can look forward to new episodes in the upcoming seasons, while the fate of "R.J. Decker" remains a point of speculation in the industry.
