## Tzruya “Suki” Lahav, Renowned Violinist and Collaborator with Bruce Springsteen, Passes Away at 74

Tzruya “Suki” Lahav, an influential figure in the music world and original violinist for Bruce Springsteen and the E Street Band, has died at the age of 74 in Jerusalem. Her son, musician Yonatan Lahav, confirmed her passing in a heartfelt post on social media, stating that she succumbed to health complications.

Born in Israel, Lahav made a significant impact on the American music scene during the mid-1970s. She is best known for her contributions to Springsteen's iconic track “Jungleland,” where her violin work added a distinctive layer to the song's emotional depth. Lahav's collaboration with the E Street Band not only showcased her musical talent but also highlighted her ability to blend classical elements with rock music, a combination that resonated with audiences.
