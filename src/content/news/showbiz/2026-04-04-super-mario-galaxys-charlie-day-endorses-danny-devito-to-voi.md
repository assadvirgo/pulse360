## Charlie Day Advocates for Danny DeVito as Wario in Super Mario Franchise

As the animated sequel to "The Super Mario Galaxy" movie continues to captivate audiences, actor Charlie Day, who reprises his role as Luigi, has expressed his enthusiasm for casting his fellow "It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia" star, Danny DeVito, as the voice of Wario. This endorsement has sparked renewed interest among fans who have been advocating for DeVito to take on the iconic character.

### The Growing Super Mario Universe

The Super Mario franchise has seen a resurgence in popularity, particularly with the recent animated films that have expanded the Nintendo universe on screen. The combination of nostalgia and modern animation techniques has drawn both longtime fans and new audiences alike. As the franchise evolves, the voice casting has become a topic of considerable discussion, with fans eager to see familiar faces take on beloved roles.

### Charlie Day's Enthusiasm

In a recent interview, Day highlighted his admiration for DeVito, stating, "Danny would fit so well as Wario." His comments resonate with many fans who see DeVito's unique voice and comedic timing as a perfect match for the character, known for his mischievous and sometimes villainous traits. Wario, a counterpart to Mario, has a distinct personality that could benefit from DeVito's signature style.

### Fan Campaigns and Community Support

The call for DeVito to voice Wario is not a new phenomenon; it has been a topic of discussion within the gaming and film communities for some time. Fans have taken to social media platforms to rally support for DeVito, sharing fan art and videos that imagine him in the role. This grassroots campaign reflects the deep connection fans have with both the characters and the actors who portray them.

### The Impact of Voice Casting

Voice casting in animated films plays a crucial role in shaping characters and enhancing storytelling. The right voice can bring a character to life, adding depth and personality that resonates with audiences. With the success of the current Super Mario films, the stakes are high for casting decisions moving forward. The inclusion of a well-known actor like DeVito could not only attract his existing fan base but also add a layer of humor and familiarity to the character of Wario.

### Conclusion

As discussions around the future of the Super Mario franchise continue, Charlie Day's endorsement of Danny DeVito as Wario highlights the importance of fan engagement and the impact of voice actors in animated storytelling. With the momentum building around this potential casting choice, it remains to be seen whether the creators will heed the call of the fans and bring DeVito into the fold. For now, the excitement surrounding the Super Mario universe continues to grow, promising more adventures and beloved characters for audiences to enjoy.
