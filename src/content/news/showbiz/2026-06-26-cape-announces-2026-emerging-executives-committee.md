## CAPE Announces 2026 Emerging Executives Committee

The Coalition of Asian Pacifics in Entertainment (CAPE) has unveiled its Emerging Executives Committee for 2026, an initiative designed to support the professional development of rising leaders within the Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander communities in the entertainment industry. This program, known as the CEEC, aims to fast-track the careers of assistant and coordinator-level employees, providing them with essential resources and networking opportunities.

### Objectives of the Emerging Executives Committee

The CEEC is a strategic response to the underrepresentation of Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders in leadership roles within the entertainment sector. By focusing on individuals at the early stages of their careers, the committee seeks to cultivate a new generation of executives who can bring diverse perspectives and experiences to the industry.

The initiative is backed by significant support from The Asian American Foundation (TAAF) and the Robert & Lien Chen Family Foundation, both of which are committed to advancing equity and representation for Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders. Their involvement underscores the importance of mentorship and community support in fostering talent and leadership within these underrepresented groups.

### Program Features

Participants in the CEEC will have access to a variety of resources aimed at enhancing their professional skills and expanding their industry networks. These resources may include mentorship programs, workshops, and networking events that connect emerging executives with established leaders in the entertainment field. The goal is to equip participants with the tools necessary to navigate the complexities of the industry and to prepare them for future leadership roles.

### Importance of Representation

The formation of the CEEC comes at a crucial time when the conversation around diversity and representation in Hollywood is more prominent than ever. While there have been strides made in recent years, the need for continued advocacy and support for Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders in entertainment remains critical. The CEEC aims to address these gaps by fostering an environment where emerging talent can thrive and contribute to a more inclusive industry.

### Looking Ahead

As the entertainment landscape continues to evolve, initiatives like the CEEC play a vital role in shaping its future. By investing in the next generation of leaders, CAPE and its partners are not only promoting diversity but also enriching the storytelling landscape with varied cultural narratives and perspectives.

The announcement of the Emerging Executives Committee for 2026 signals a commitment to long-term change within the industry. As the program unfolds, it will be closely watched by industry stakeholders and advocates for diversity, who hope to see tangible results in the representation and leadership of Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders in entertainment.

In conclusion, the CEEC represents a significant step forward in addressing the challenges faced by underrepresented communities in the entertainment industry. With the support of established organizations and a clear focus on mentorship and professional growth, the initiative aims to empower a new wave of executives who will shape the future of the industry.
