## Steve Kroft Reflects on His Time at '60 Minutes'

Steve Kroft, the esteemed journalist and longtime correspondent for CBS's iconic news program '60 Minutes,' has opened up about his complex feelings regarding his tenure on the show. In a recent interview, Kroft candidly expressed that he "hated" certain aspects of his experience, stating that he "probably wouldn't do it again." Despite these sentiments, he acknowledged the exhilaration that came with covering significant stories throughout his career.

### A Career Marked by Challenges and Triumphs

Kroft, who retired in 2019 after a distinguished career that spanned more than three decades, was known for his incisive reporting and ability to engage with high-profile figures. His work on '60 Minutes' included interviews with presidents, business leaders, and a range of influential personalities, earning him a reputation as one of the leading journalists of his generation.

While reflecting on his time with the program, Kroft pointed out the intense pressures and demands that came with the role. The high-stakes environment of investigative journalism often meant long hours and the need to meet tight deadlines, which contributed to his mixed feelings about the experience. "It was exhilarating to do the stories I did," he remarked, acknowledging the thrill of uncovering truths and presenting them to the public. However, the emotional toll and stress associated with such a rigorous profession were also evident in his reflections.

### The Legacy of '60 Minutes'

'60 Minutes' has long been regarded as a hallmark of American journalism, known for its in-depth reporting and commitment to uncovering the truth. The program has shaped public discourse and informed viewers on critical issues for over five decades. Kroft's contributions to the show have left an indelible mark, as he tackled complex subjects ranging from politics to social issues, often sparking conversations that extended beyond the screen.

Despite the challenges he faced, Kroft's work resonated with audiences and garnered numerous accolades, including multiple Emmy Awards. His ability to navigate difficult topics with sensitivity and insight has set a standard for future journalists.

### Looking Back and Moving Forward

As Kroft reflects on his career, he emphasizes the importance of storytelling in journalism. He believes that the role of a journalist is not just to report facts but to connect with audiences on a deeper level. This perspective is particularly relevant in today's media landscape, where the demand for compelling narratives continues to grow.

Kroft's comments serve as a reminder of the complexities inherent in journalism, where the pursuit of truth can often come with personal sacrifices. While he may have reservations about his time at '60 Minutes,' his contributions to the field remain significant, inspiring both current and aspiring journalists to strive for excellence in their work.

In conclusion, Steve Kroft's reflections on his time at '60 Minutes' highlight the dual nature of a career in journalism—one that can be both exhilarating and challenging. As he moves forward from his role, his legacy will undoubtedly continue to influence the next generation of reporters seeking to make their mark in the world of news.
