## David Zaslav Honors Ted Turner as a "Generational Entrepreneur"

In a recent earnings call for Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD), CEO David Zaslav paid tribute to the late Ted Turner, the founder of CNN, emphasizing his profound impact on the media landscape. This acknowledgment came shortly after Zaslav had shared a memo with WBD staff reflecting on Turner's legacy.

### A Legacy of Innovation

Zaslav described Turner as a "generational entrepreneur," highlighting his belief in the transformative power of ideas and storytelling. Turner, who founded CNN in 1980, revolutionized news broadcasting by introducing the concept of 24-hour news coverage, fundamentally changing how information is disseminated to the public. Zaslav's remarks underscore the significance of Turner's contributions not only to CNN but also to the broader media industry.

In his statement, Zaslav noted that Turner’s vision extended beyond just news. "He was someone who believed deeply in the power of ideas and in telling stories and building platforms," Zaslav said. This sentiment reflects the ethos that has guided WBD's operations and strategic direction, particularly as the company navigates a rapidly evolving media environment.

### Acknowledgment of Mentorship

Zaslav also took a moment to acknowledge the influence of John Malone, a prominent figure in the media industry and a mentor to both Turner and himself. Malone's role in shaping the landscape of cable television and his strategic insights have been instrumental in the growth of various media enterprises, including WBD. By referencing Malone, Zaslav positioned Turner's legacy within a broader context of mentorship and innovation in the media sector.

### The Future of Media

As WBD continues to adapt to the challenges posed by digital transformation and changing consumer preferences, Zaslav's tribute to Turner serves as a reminder of the foundational principles that have guided successful media ventures. The emphasis on storytelling and innovative platforms is particularly relevant as the company seeks to engage audiences in an increasingly competitive market.

The earnings call also provided a platform for Zaslav to discuss WBD's strategic initiatives and performance metrics for the first quarter of the fiscal year. While the focus was on financial outcomes, the acknowledgment of Turner's legacy added a personal and historical dimension to the conversation, reinforcing the interconnectedness of past and present in the media industry.

### Conclusion

Ted Turner's passing marks the end of an era for media entrepreneurs, but his influence continues to resonate within the industry. David Zaslav's remarks during the WBD earnings call not only honor Turner's contributions but also serve as a guiding light for the future of media. As companies like WBD navigate the complexities of modern broadcasting, the principles of innovation and storytelling that Turner championed will remain vital to their success.
