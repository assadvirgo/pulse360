## Natalia Solórzano Vásquez’s ‘Spells to Revive a Witch’ Secures International Backing

Costa Rican filmmaker Natalia Solórzano Vásquez is set to bring her latest project, “Spells to Revive a Witch,” to a broader audience with the announcement of new partnerships with Spain’s Testaferro and Uruguay’s Guay Films. This hybrid documentary, which explores the mythical fortune teller Soralla de Persia, is currently in post-production and aims to make a significant impact at the upcoming Costa Rica Media Market.

### A Unique Cinematic Exploration

“Spells to Revive a Witch” delves into the rich tapestry of folklore surrounding Soralla de Persia, a figure steeped in mystery and intrigue. The film promises to blend documentary elements with narrative storytelling, providing viewers with a unique perspective on the cultural significance of witchcraft and fortune-telling in Latin American societies. Solórzano Vásquez’s previous works have garnered attention for their innovative approach and depth, and this project is anticipated to continue that trend.

### Strategic Partnerships Enhance Production

The collaboration with Testaferro and Guay Films marks a significant step in the film’s development. Testaferro, based in Spain, has a reputation for supporting projects that explore cultural narratives, while Guay Films from Uruguay has been recognized for its commitment to quality storytelling in Latin American cinema. These partnerships not only provide financial backing but also enhance the film’s international appeal, potentially opening doors for distribution across Europe and Latin America.

### Costa Rica Media Market: A Platform for Emerging Talent

The Costa Rica Media Market serves as a crucial venue for filmmakers to showcase their projects to potential investors, distributors, and collaborators. As Solórzano Vásquez prepares to present “Spells to Revive a Witch” at this event, the spotlight will be on her ability to attract interest and support within the competitive landscape of international cinema. The market is known for fostering connections between local talent and global industry players, making it an ideal platform for the film’s debut.

### Previous Successes and Future Prospects

Solórzano Vásquez is no stranger to the film festival circuit. Her production company, Sputnik Films, has previously produced notable works, including Sofía Quirós Ubeda’s “Land of Ashes,” which was selected for the prestigious Cannes Film Festival. This track record of success bodes well for “Spells to Revive a Witch,” as it builds on a foundation of artistic credibility and industry recognition.

### Conclusion

As “Spells to Revive a Witch” moves forward with its production and prepares for its presentation at the Costa Rica Media Market, the collaboration with Testaferro and Guay Films signifies a promising future for the project. With its exploration of cultural folklore and the backing of established industry partners, the film is poised to resonate with audiences both locally and internationally. As the film industry continues to evolve, projects like Solórzano Vásquez’s remind us of the power of storytelling in bridging cultural divides and celebrating shared human experiences.
