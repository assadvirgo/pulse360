## STIM VFX Studio Expands to the Canary Islands, Creating Over 100 Jobs

French animation and visual effects company STIM has announced the establishment of a new production center in the Canary Islands, Spain. This strategic move is part of the company's broader initiative to expand its European operations, with plans to create more than 100 jobs in the region.

### New Production Center in Tenerife

The new facility, named STIM Tenerife, is set to commence operations in August 2023. Located in one of the most picturesque regions of Spain, the Canary Islands offer a unique blend of natural beauty and modern infrastructure, making it an attractive location for creative industries. The decision to open a production center in Tenerife aligns with STIM's commitment to harnessing local talent and resources to enhance its production capabilities.

### A Legacy of Animation and Visual Effects

STIM is recognized for its contributions to the animation and visual effects industry, with a portfolio that includes notable titles such as "The Wild Robot," "The Garfield Movie," and "Coyote vs. Acme." The company's expertise in creating high-quality visual content has positioned it as a key player in the competitive landscape of animation and VFX.

The expansion into the Canary Islands is expected to bolster STIM's ability to deliver innovative and engaging content while tapping into the growing demand for animation and visual effects in Europe. The new center will not only serve as a hub for production but also as a training ground for aspiring artists and technicians in the region.

### Economic Impact and Job Creation

The establishment of STIM Tenerife is anticipated to have a significant economic impact on the local community. By creating over 100 jobs, the company aims to provide opportunities for skilled professionals in animation, visual effects, and related fields. This influx of employment is expected to stimulate the local economy and contribute to the growth of the creative sector in the Canary Islands.

Local government officials have expressed support for STIM's decision to invest in the region, recognizing the potential benefits of attracting creative industries. The initiative is seen as a step towards diversifying the local economy, which has traditionally relied on tourism.

### Looking Ahead

As STIM prepares to launch its operations in Tenerife, the company is poised to enhance its presence in the European market. The new production center signifies a commitment to innovation and excellence in animation and visual effects, while also fostering local talent and contributing to the economic development of the Canary Islands.

With the global demand for high-quality visual content continuing to rise, STIM's expansion is a timely response to the evolving landscape of the entertainment industry. The company’s investment in the Canary Islands may pave the way for further growth and collaboration within the region, ultimately enriching the cultural and creative fabric of Spain.
