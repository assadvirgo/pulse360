## Kyrgyz Drama 'Kurak' Denied Censor Certificate Ahead of Bishkek Film Festival

In a surprising turn of events, the award-winning Kyrgyz drama "Kurak," directed by Erke Dzhumakmatova and Emil Atageldiev, has been denied a censor certificate by authorities in Kyrgyzstan. This decision has compelled the filmmakers to withdraw the film from the ongoing Bishkek International Film Festival, where it was scheduled to be screened.

### A Setback for Local Cinema

"Kurak," which has garnered acclaim on the international film circuit, was anticipated to have its homecoming at the Bishkek festival. The film's withdrawal is not only a disappointment for the filmmakers but also for local audiences eager to engage with a work that reflects contemporary Kyrgyz culture and storytelling. The film's success abroad had built significant momentum, making its absence from the festival particularly poignant.

### Context of the Censorship Decision

While specific reasons for the censorship have not been publicly disclosed, such decisions often reflect broader societal and political sensitivities. Despite the setback, the filmmakers behind "Kurak" remain committed to their vision.
