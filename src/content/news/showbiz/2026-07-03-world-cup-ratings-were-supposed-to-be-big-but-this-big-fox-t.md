## Surge in World Cup Ratings Signals Soccer's Mainstream Acceptance in the U.S.

As the 2026 FIFA World Cup approaches, executives from Fox and Telemundo are reflecting on the remarkable surge in viewership ratings for soccer in the United States. This rise has sparked discussions about the sport's growing status in a country traditionally dominated by American football, basketball, and baseball.

### A Turning Point for Soccer in America

Mike Mulvihill, the president of insights and analytics for Fox's entertainment and sports divisions, has expressed confidence that the increasing popularity of soccer is no longer a matter of speculation. “It’s a settled issue,” he stated, emphasizing that the upcoming World Cup serves as a definitive marker for soccer's acceptance in the U.S. sports landscape. This sentiment reflects a broader trend in which soccer has steadily gained traction among American audiences over the past few decades.

### Factors Contributing to the Surge

Several factors have contributed to the rising viewership numbers. The growing diversity of the U.S. population has played a significant role, as soccer is a beloved sport in many cultures represented across the country. Additionally, the success of Major League Soccer (MLS) and the increasing visibility of international leagues have helped to cultivate a more enthusiastic soccer fan base.

Telemundo, which has been a key player in broadcasting soccer, particularly for Spanish-speaking audiences, has also reported impressive ratings. The network's commitment to covering major soccer events has resonated with viewers, further solidifying soccer's place in American sports culture.

### The Role of Major Events

Major events like the FIFA World Cup serve as catalysts for interest in soccer. The excitement surrounding the tournament not only attracts dedicated fans but also engages casual viewers who may not typically follow the sport. The 2026 World Cup, which will be jointly hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, is expected to draw unprecedented attention, as it will be the first time the tournament is held in North America since 1994.

### Looking Ahead

As the World Cup draws nearer, both Fox and Telemundo are strategizing on how to maximize viewership and engagement. They are focused on innovative broadcasting techniques and comprehensive coverage that appeal to both seasoned soccer fans and newcomers alike. The goal is to create an immersive experience that showcases the sport's excitement and cultural significance.

### Conclusion

The surge in World Cup ratings underscores a pivotal moment for soccer in the United States. As executives from Fox and Telemundo work to harness this momentum, the 2026 FIFA World Cup promises to be a landmark event that could further entrench soccer's status as a mainstream sport in America. With the right strategies in place, the upcoming tournament may not only captivate existing fans but also inspire a new generation of soccer enthusiasts.
