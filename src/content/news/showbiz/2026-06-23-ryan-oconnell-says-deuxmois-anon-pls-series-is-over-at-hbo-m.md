## Ryan O’Connell Confirms Cancellation of DeuxMoi’s ‘Anon Pls’ Series at HBO Max

In a recent appearance on Bravo's "Watch What Happens Live," screenwriter Ryan O’Connell disclosed that the highly anticipated adaptation of DeuxMoi's novel "Anon Pls" will not be moving forward at HBO Max. This announcement marks the end of a project that had generated considerable interest among fans of the original material.

O’Connell, who is known for his work on the acclaimed series "Special," responded to host Andy Cohen's inquiry about the status of the adaptation. "Oh, yes, but that’s over. It didn’t move forward," he stated, confirming the cancellation without providing further details on the reasons behind the decision.

### Background on 'Anon Pls'

"Anon Pls" is a novel that gained popularity for its engaging narrative and exploration of contemporary social themes, particularly in the realm of anonymity and online culture. The book, which is based on the popular Instagram account DeuxMoi, captures the essence of modern celebrity gossip and the complexities of digital communication. Its adaptation was eagerly anticipated by fans who hoped to see the story translated to the screen.

### HBO Max's Content Strategy

The cancellation of "Anon Pls" at HBO Max reflects the platform's ongoing efforts to refine its content strategy. In recent months, HBO Max has made headlines for various shifts in its programming lineup, including the cancellation of several projects and the focus on high-profile series that align with its brand identity. The decision to halt the production of "Anon Pls" may be indicative of broader trends within the streaming industry, where platforms are increasingly selective about the content they choose to develop.

### Industry Reactions

The news of the series' cancellation has elicited reactions from fans and industry insiders alike. Many expressed disappointment, as the adaptation was seen as a promising opportunity to explore the themes of the novel in a new medium. Social media platforms have been abuzz with discussions about the implications of this cancellation, particularly in relation to the future of adaptations based on popular literary works.
