## Cannes’ Critics Week Announces 2026 Lineup with Animated Feature ‘In Waves’

The prestigious Cannes Critics Week is set to commence its 65th edition in 2026, with an exciting lineup that promises to showcase a diverse range of cinematic talent. The event will kick off with the animated feature "In Waves," directed by Phuong Mai Nguyen, marking a significant milestone as it becomes the first animated film to open this parallel selection at Cannes.

### Opening Film: ‘In Waves’

"In Waves" is a noteworthy debut feature from Nguyen, whose work has garnered attention for its innovative storytelling and artistic vision. The film is set against the backdrop of California, and while specific plot details remain under wraps, it is anticipated to blend captivating animation with a poignant narrative. This choice reflects a growing recognition of animated films in prestigious film festivals, traditionally dominated by live-action features.

### Closing Film: ‘Adieu monde cruel’

The Critics Week will conclude with Félix de Givry’s coming-of-age drama "Adieu monde cruel." This film is expected to resonate with audiences through its exploration of youth and the complexities of growing up in a rapidly changing world. The selection of both opening and closing films highlights Cannes Critics Week's commitment to showcasing a variety of genres and styles, encouraging a broader appreciation of cinematic art.

### Curatorial Vision

Ava Cahen, the curator of Critics Week, has emphasized the importance of diversity and innovation in this year’s lineup. Her vision aims to spotlight emerging filmmakers and unique narratives that challenge conventional storytelling. By featuring both an animated film and a coming-of-age drama, the selection underscores the festival's dedication to fostering new voices in the film industry.

### Significance of the Event

Cannes Critics Week is renowned for its role in promoting independent cinema and providing a platform for filmmakers who may not yet have received widespread recognition. The festival serves as a launching pad for many films that go on to achieve critical and commercial success. By including "In Waves" as the opening film, Critics Week not only honors the artistry of animation but also sets a tone of creativity and exploration for the festival.

### Looking Ahead

As the 2026 edition approaches, anticipation builds among filmmakers, critics, and cinephiles alike. The inclusion of groundbreaking works such as "In Waves" and "Adieu monde cruel" signals a promising year for the festival, one that is likely to ignite discussions about the evolving landscape of cinema. With the ongoing evolution of storytelling mediums, Cannes Critics Week continues to be a vital part of the global film community, celebrating both established and emerging talents.

In summary, the upcoming Cannes Critics Week promises an engaging lineup that reflects the dynamic nature of contemporary cinema. As it prepares to welcome audiences and industry professionals, the festival reaffirms its commitment to innovation and diversity in film.
