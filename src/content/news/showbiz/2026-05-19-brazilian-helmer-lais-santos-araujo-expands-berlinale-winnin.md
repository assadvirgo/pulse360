## Brazilian Director Laís Santos Araújo Expands Berlinale-Winning Short 'Infantaria' Into Feature Film

Following the success of her acclaimed short film "Infantaria," Brazilian director Laís Santos Araújo is set to expand her project into a feature-length film. The short, which premiered at the Berlin International Film Festival (Berlinale), garnered significant attention and praise, establishing Araújo as a rising talent in the international film community.

### Transition from Short to Feature

"Infantaria," which has resonated with audiences for its poignant storytelling and visual artistry, is now being developed into a full-length feature. This transition marks a significant milestone in Araújo's career, as she seeks to delve deeper into the themes and narratives introduced in the short film. The original work's success at Berlinale has laid a solid foundation for this next phase, attracting interest from various stakeholders in the film industry.

### Support from Prestigious Initiatives

The project is being produced by Pedro Krull of Aguda Cinema, known for his commitment to innovative storytelling and support for emerging filmmakers. Krull's involvement is crucial as the project navigates through the prestigious Cannes Cinéfondation Résidence, a program designed to support young filmmakers in developing their projects in a nurturing environment. This residency will provide Araújo with invaluable resources, mentorship, and networking opportunities to further refine her vision for the feature adaptation.

In addition to the Cannes residency, the project has also secured a grant from the Centre National du Cinéma et de l'Image Animée (CNC), a French public institution that supports the film industry in various capacities. This funding is expected to enhance the production quality and broaden the film's reach, enabling Araújo to bring her artistic vision to life on a larger scale.

### Themes and Setting

While specific plot details of the feature adaptation remain under wraps, "Infantaria" is set against the evocative landscapes of Brazil, exploring themes of childhood, identity, and resilience. The short film's narrative is characterized by its intimate portrayal of characters navigating their realities, a hallmark of Araújo's storytelling style. The expansion into a feature format promises to deepen these explorations, offering audiences a richer and more immersive experience.

### Conclusion

As Laís Santos Araújo embarks on this ambitious journey to transform "Infantaria" into a feature film, the support from Cannes Cinéfondation and the CNC underscores the potential impact of her work. With a growing reputation and a commitment to authentic storytelling, Araújo is poised to make significant contributions to the global cinema landscape. The film industry will be watching closely as she continues to develop this promising project, which aims to resonate with audiences both in Brazil and beyond.
