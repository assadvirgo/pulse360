## International Sales Rights Acquired for Documentary on Iconic 1986 World Cup Match

In a significant development within the film industry, Round 12 has secured the international sales rights for the documentary titled *The Match*, which focuses on the legendary quarter-final match between Argentina and England during the 1986 FIFA World Cup. This acquisition comes ahead of the film's premiere at the prestigious Cannes Film Festival, marking a noteworthy milestone for the sports-focused label established by Mediawan and Goodfellas.

## A Historic Match Revisited

The documentary, directed by Juan Cabral and Santiago Franco, revisits one of the most iconic moments in football history—the match that featured Diego Maradona's infamous "Hand of God" goal. This game not only showcased Maradona's extraordinary talent but also became a pivotal moment in the sporting rivalry between Argentina and England, particularly in the context of the political tensions following the Falklands War.

*The Match* is narrated by renowned football figures Gary Lineker and Jorge Valdano, both of whom played significant roles in the game. Lineker, who was a forward for England during the match, and Valdano, who represented Argentina, provide unique insights and perspectives, adding depth to the narrative of this historical encounter.

## A First for Round 12

This acquisition is particularly notable as it marks the first time Round 12 has acquired sales rights for a title included in the Cannes Official Selection since the label's inception. The Cannes Film Festival is known for showcasing high-quality films from around the world, and being part of this selection underscores the documentary's potential impact and appeal.

Round 12 has positioned itself as a key player in the international sales market, focusing on sports-related content. The acquisition of *The Match* aligns with its mission to bring compelling sports narratives to a global audience, highlighting the intersection of culture, history, and sport.

## Anticipation for the Cannes Premiere

As *The Match* prepares for its debut at Cannes, anticipation is building among film enthusiasts and football fans alike. The documentary not only promises to capture the essence of a pivotal moment in sports history but also aims to explore the broader implications of the match on both nations involved.

The film's premiere at Cannes offers it a platform to reach a diverse audience, potentially leading to further international sales and distribution opportunities. The combination of a compelling story, expert narration, and the rich backdrop of the 1986 World Cup positions *The Match* as a must-see for both sports aficionados and cinema lovers.

## Conclusion

With its acquisition by Round 12 and its upcoming premiere at Cannes, *The Match* is poised to make a significant impact in the documentary film landscape. By revisiting a historic sporting event through the eyes of those who lived it, the film not only honors the legacy of the players involved but also invites viewers to reflect on the enduring power of football as a unifying force across cultures and nations.
