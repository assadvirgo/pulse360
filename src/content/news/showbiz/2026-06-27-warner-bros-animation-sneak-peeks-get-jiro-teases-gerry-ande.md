## Warner Bros. Animation Expands Adult Animation Offerings at Annecy

Warner Bros. Animation has made significant strides in the realm of adult animation, as evidenced by its recent presentations at the Annecy International Animation Film Festival. The studio unveiled several new projects, including a reimagined animated series based on the cult classic "Dark Shadows," alongside sneak peeks of "Get Jiro" and a teaser for Gerry Anderson's "Hit Squad."

### New Directions in Adult Animation

The announcement of the "Dark Shadows" animated series marks a notable shift for Warner Bros. Animation, which has traditionally focused on adult comedies. This new project aims to breathe fresh life into the iconic 1960s television series, which originally blended elements of horror and soap opera. The series is currently in the early stages of development, with Lisa Holdsworth, known for her work on Channel 4’s “Dreamers,” attached as the creator. This move signifies the studio's ambition to explore diverse genres within the adult animation landscape.

### "Get Jiro" and Gerry Anderson's "Hit Squad"

In addition to "Dark Shadows," Warner Bros. Animation provided a glimpse into "Get Jiro," a project that has generated excitement among fans of graphic novels and culinary-themed narratives. The adaptation is based on the work of acclaimed writer Anthony Bourdain, known for his unique storytelling style that often intersects food culture and personal experiences. 

Moreover, the studio teased Gerry Anderson's "Hit Squad," a project that promises to bring a fresh perspective to Anderson's legacy in animation. While details remain sparse, the anticipation surrounding this project reflects a growing interest in reviving classic narratives for contemporary audiences.
