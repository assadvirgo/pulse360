## Tony Leung Reflects on the Rise of AI and His Hopes for the Future of Film

In a recent appearance in Shanghai, renowned Hong Kong actor Tony Leung shared his thoughts on the evolving landscape of the film industry, particularly in light of the rapid advancements in artificial intelligence (AI). As the head of the Golden Goblet jury at the Shanghai International Film Festival, Leung's insights carry significant weight, especially as the industry grapples with the implications of AI on creativity and audience engagement.

### The Threat of AI to Creativity

Leung expressed concern regarding the potential threats posed by AI to artistic expression in cinema. He articulated a belief that while technology can enhance filmmaking processes, it also risks diminishing the human element that is vital to storytelling. "AI can analyze patterns and generate content, but it cannot replicate the emotional depth and unique perspectives that human creators bring to their work," Leung stated. This sentiment echoes a broader conversation within the industry about the balance between technological innovation and the preservation of creative integrity.

### Engaging Young Audiences

As the film industry faces challenges in attracting younger audiences to theaters, Leung emphasized the importance of innovative storytelling and immersive experiences. He noted that young viewers are increasingly drawn to content that resonates with their experiences and values. "We need to create films that speak to their realities, that challenge them, and that inspire them," he remarked. This approach not only aims to keep cinema relevant but also to foster a new generation of film enthusiasts who appreciate the art form's nuances.

### A Shift to European Arthouse Cinema

In a notable shift in his career, Leung has recently ventured into European arthouse cinema, a move that he believes allows for greater artistic freedom and exploration. He described this transition as an opportunity to collaborate with diverse filmmakers and to delve into narratives that may not fit the conventional mold of mainstream cinema. "Arthouse films often push boundaries and invite audiences to think critically. I find that exciting," he said, highlighting his desire to contribute to a global dialogue through film.

### Hopes for the Future

Looking ahead, Leung remains optimistic about the future of cinema, despite the challenges posed by AI and changing audience dynamics. He believes that the film industry can adapt by embracing both technology and the timeless art of storytelling. "Cinema has always evolved, and I trust that it will continue to do so," he remarked. His commitment to exploring new narratives and engaging with audiences reflects a broader hope that the essence of film as a medium for connection and reflection will endure.

In conclusion, Tony Leung's reflections during the Shanghai International Film Festival underscore the complexities facing the film industry today. As AI continues to reshape various sectors, Leung's insights serve as a reminder of the importance of preserving the human touch in creative endeavors and the need to engage audiences in meaningful ways. His journey into European arthouse cinema further illustrates a commitment to innovation and exploration, marking a significant chapter in his illustrious career.
