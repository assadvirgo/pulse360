## Stephen Poliakoff to Return to Major TV Drama with New Historical Political Thriller

Acclaimed writer Stephen Poliakoff, known for his multi-BAFTA and Emmy-winning works, is set to make a significant return to large-scale television with his latest project, "The Order." This ambitious eight-part series is an international political thriller that delves into the historical context of the Great Siege of Malta, which took place in 1565.

### A Historical Context

The Great Siege of Malta was a pivotal event in European history, marked by the fierce conflict between the Knights of St. John and the Ottoman Empire. This dramatic backdrop provides a rich narrative landscape for Poliakoff, who has a reputation for weaving intricate stories that explore complex themes and characters. The series promises to capture the tension and political maneuvering of the time, offering viewers a compelling glimpse into a critical moment that shaped the course of European history.

### Announcement and Production Details

The announcement of "The Order" was made by Malta Film Commissioner Johann Grech at the Mediterranean Film Festival, highlighting the series' connection to its historical setting. Malta, with its rich cultural heritage and historical architecture, serves as an ideal backdrop for this epic narrative. The production aims to leverage the island's unique landscapes to authentically portray the events of the siege.

Poliakoff's return to television is highly anticipated, given his previous successes in the medium. His works often feature a blend of historical fact and fiction, allowing for a nuanced exploration of the human experience during tumultuous times. With "The Order," he aims to bring a fresh perspective to the genre of historical drama, combining meticulous research with engaging storytelling.

### Expectations and Impact

As the series is still in the early stages of development, details regarding the cast and release date remain under wraps. However, the announcement has already generated excitement among fans of historical dramas and Poliakoff's work alike.
