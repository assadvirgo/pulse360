## ‘LineOut’ Director Nazila Azizi On Using Magical Realism To Capture Afghan Refugee Experience

In a poignant reflection of her tumultuous past, Nazila Azizi, an Afghan native, has transitioned from a life of peril to one of artistic expression. Just a few years ago, she was navigating the chaos of Kabul as the Taliban regained control, desperately seeking safety and a chance to escape her homeland. Today, she stands as a filmmaker with a documentary, "LineOut," that is gaining recognition and is currently in contention for an Academy Award.

### A Journey from Danger to Artistry

Nazila Azizi's journey is emblematic of the struggles faced by many Afghan refugees. After enduring the harrowing experience of dodging bullets and racing against time to board one of the last evacuation flights out of Kabul, she relocated to the United States. In her new home, Azizi found solace and purpose in architecture and filmmaking, disciplines that allow her to weave her experiences into narratives that resonate with broader audiences.

"LineOut" serves as a testament to her commitment to telling the stories of Afghan refugees. The documentary employs elements of magical realism, a style that Azizi believes can effectively convey the complexities of the refugee experience. By blending the fantastical with the real, she aims to highlight the emotional and psychological landscapes that often accompany displacement.

### Magical Realism as a Narrative Tool

Azizi's use of magical realism is not merely a stylistic choice; it is a deliberate strategy to engage viewers on multiple levels. "The reality of being a refugee is often too harsh to depict directly," she explains. "By incorporating magical elements, I hope to create a space where audiences can empathize with the characters' struggles while also being drawn into a world that reflects their resilience and hope."

This approach allows Azizi to explore themes of identity, loss, and survival, while also celebrating the rich cultural heritage of Afghanistan. The film captures the essence of what it means to be uprooted from one's home, yet still holding onto dreams and aspirations. Through her lens, Azizi invites viewers to witness the beauty and strength inherent in the Afghan spirit, even amidst adversity.

### Recognition and Future Aspirations

"LineOut" is currently on an Oscar-qualifying run, a significant milestone for Azizi and her team. The recognition not only elevates the film but also brings attention to the ongoing plight of Afghan refugees, a topic that remains pressing in global discourse. Azizi hopes that her work will inspire further conversations about the experiences of displaced individuals and the importance of empathy in understanding their journeys.

As she continues to navigate her dual identity as an architect and filmmaker, Azizi remains committed to using her platform to advocate for those whose voices are often marginalized. She envisions a future where stories like hers and those of her fellow refugees are not just told but celebrated, fostering a greater understanding of the human experience across cultural divides.

In a world that often feels divided, Azizi's "LineOut" stands as a reminder of the power of storytelling to bridge gaps, foster connections, and ultimately, promote healing. As she reflects on her journey, Azizi emphasizes that art can be a powerful vehicle for change, and she is determined to use her talents to illuminate the narratives that matter.
