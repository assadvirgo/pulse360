## Tim Ryan, Former Variety Honolulu Correspondent, Dies at 80

Tim Ryan, a respected journalist known for his contributions to the entertainment industry in Hawaii, passed away on June 20 in West Seattle, Washington, at the age of 80. His death followed a prolonged illness, marking the end of a notable career that spanned several decades.

Ryan's journalism journey began at the Honolulu Star-Bulletin, where he served as the senior entertainment writer from 1984 until 2006. During his tenure, he became a prominent figure in covering Hawaii's vibrant film and television scene. His insightful reporting not only highlighted local productions but also brought national attention to the unique cultural landscape of the islands.

In addition to his work with the Star-Bulletin, Ryan made significant contributions to Variety, a leading publication in the entertainment industry. His role as a correspondent allowed him to report on various film and television projects that were either based in Hawaii or involved local talent. His work was instrumental in showcasing the state's appeal as a filming location, which has attracted numerous productions over the years.

Ryan's reporting was characterized by a deep understanding of the entertainment industry and a passion for storytelling. Colleagues and friends remember him as a dedicated professional who was committed to his craft. His ability to connect with sources and convey their stories resonated with readers, making him a beloved figure in the local journalism community.

Throughout his career, Ryan covered a wide array of topics, from major film releases to the intricacies of television production. His articles often featured interviews with filmmakers, actors, and other industry professionals, providing readers with an insider's perspective on the entertainment world. His work not only informed but also inspired many aspiring journalists and filmmakers in Hawaii.

In the years following his retirement, Ryan continued to be an influential voice in the community, sharing his knowledge and experiences with younger generations. He was known for mentoring aspiring writers and encouraging them to pursue their passions in journalism and the arts.

Ryan's legacy extends beyond his reporting; he played a vital role in fostering a sense of pride in Hawaii's artistic contributions to the broader entertainment landscape. His work helped to elevate the visibility of local talent and productions, paving the way for future generations in the industry.

As news of his passing spreads, tributes have poured in from colleagues, friends, and those who were touched by his work. Many have expressed their gratitude for his contributions to journalism and the arts, highlighting the impact he had on the community and the industry at large.

Tim Ryan's dedication to his craft and his unwavering support for Hawaii's film and television industry will be remembered fondly. His passing marks a significant loss for the journalism community and the many individuals he inspired throughout his career.
