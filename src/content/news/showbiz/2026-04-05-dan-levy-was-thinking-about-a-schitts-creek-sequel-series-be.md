## Dan Levy Reflects on Potential 'Schitt's Creek' Sequel Following Catherine O'Hara's Passing

In a recent interview with CBS Sunday Morning, Dan Levy, the co-creator and star of the acclaimed television series "Schitt's Creek," revealed that he had been contemplating a sequel series prior to the death of his co-star, Catherine O'Hara. O'Hara, who portrayed the beloved character Moira Rose, passed away earlier this year, leaving a significant void in the show's fan community and among its creators.

### Levy's Thoughts on a Sequel

During the interview, Levy expressed his admiration for O'Hara and the profound impact her passing has had on his creative process. "I was thinking about a sequel series," he stated, reflecting on the potential continuation of the story that captivated audiences for six seasons. However, he also acknowledged the challenges of moving forward without O'Hara, stating, "It can't be done now." This sentiment underscores the deep connection between the cast and the characters they portrayed, suggesting that any future project would need to honor O'Hara's legacy.

### The Legacy of 'Schitt's Creek'

"Schitt's Creek," which aired from 2015 to 2020, received widespread acclaim for its writing, character development, and representation of LGBTQ+ themes. The series follows the wealthy Rose family, who find themselves in financial ruin and are forced to relocate to a small town they once bought as a joke. The show's unique blend of humor and heartfelt moments resonated with audiences, leading to numerous awards, including several Primetime Emmy Awards.

The ensemble cast, which included Levy, O'Hara, Eugene Levy, and Annie Murphy, became beloved figures in the entertainment industry. O'Hara's portrayal of Moira Rose, with her eccentric fashion sense and memorable one-liners, became iconic, solidifying her status as a television legend.

### The Impact of O'Hara's Death

Catherine O'Hara's passing has prompted an outpouring of tributes from fans and fellow actors alike, highlighting her contributions to the arts and the indelible mark she left on the industry. Levy's comments reflect a broader sentiment within the entertainment community, where the loss of a key figure can significantly alter the trajectory of creative projects.

While the possibility of a sequel may seem dim in light of O'Hara's absence, Levy's reflections serve as a reminder of the show's enduring impact and the strong bonds formed among its cast members. The emotional weight of O'Hara's legacy will undoubtedly influence any future endeavors related to "Schitt's Creek."

### Conclusion

As fans continue to celebrate the legacy of "Schitt's Creek," Levy's acknowledgment of the potential sequel serves as a poignant reminder of the show's cultural significance. While the future remains uncertain, the love and admiration for Catherine O'Hara and her character will continue to resonate with audiences, ensuring that the spirit of "Schitt's Creek" endures.
