## Nathalie Baye, Acclaimed French Actress, Passes Away at 77

Nathalie Baye, a celebrated figure in French cinema and known internationally for her roles in films such as "Day for Night" and "Catch Me If You Can," has died at the age of 77. Her family confirmed that she passed away on Friday evening at her home in Paris due to complications related to Lewy body dementia.

## A Remarkable Career

Baye was born in France and began her acting career in the late 1960s. She quickly gained recognition for her talent and versatility, establishing herself as one of the leading actresses in French cinema. Her breakout role came in François Truffaut's 1973 film "Day for Night," which won the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film. In this film, Baye's performance was lauded for its depth and authenticity, showcasing her ability to portray complex characters.

Throughout her career, Baye collaborated with numerous prominent directors and actors, further cementing her status as a significant figure in the film industry. Her international appeal grew with her role in Steven Spielberg's "Catch Me If You Can," where she starred alongside Leonardo DiCaprio and Tom Hanks. This film introduced her talent to a broader audience and highlighted her adaptability in both French and English-language cinema.

## Legacy in Film and Television

In addition to her film work, Baye also made notable appearances in television.
