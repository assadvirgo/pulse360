## Palme d’Or Winner Juanjo Giménez to Begin Shooting ‘Pínkala’ in Spain

Spanish filmmaker Juanjo Giménez Peña, renowned for his acclaimed short film "Timecode," which won the prestigious Palme d’Or at the Cannes Film Festival in 2016 and received an Academy Award nomination, is set to embark on a new cinematic journey. His upcoming feature film, titled "Pínkala," is scheduled to begin production this July in Vigo, Spain.

### A New Chapter in Filmmaking

"Pínkala" marks a significant step for Giménez as he transitions from short films to feature-length storytelling. The film is described as a supernatural love story, a genre that allows for creative exploration of themes such as passion, connection, and the ethereal aspects of love. The choice of Vigo as a filming location is particularly interesting, as the city is known for its picturesque landscapes and vibrant culture, which may enhance the film's visual narrative.

### Casting and Production

The lead role in "Pínkala" will be played by Pol López, a rising star in Spanish cinema. López's previous work has garnered attention for his compelling performances, and his involvement in this project is anticipated to bring depth to the character he portrays. The film is produced by Frida Films, a production company known for its commitment to innovative storytelling, alongside M-Films from Lithuania, indicating a collaborative effort that spans across borders.

### Expectations and Impact

Given Giménez's track record, expectations for "Pínkala" are high. His ability to weave intricate narratives with emotional resonance has earned him a dedicated following, and the film is likely to attract both domestic and international audiences. The combination of a supernatural love story and Giménez's unique directorial style could result in a film that not only entertains but also provokes thought and discussion among viewers.

### The Broader Context of Spanish Cinema

The announcement of "Pínkala" comes at a time when Spanish cinema is experiencing a resurgence on the global stage. With filmmakers like Giménez leading the charge, there is a growing recognition of the creativity and talent emerging from Spain. This film could further contribute to the rich tapestry of Spanish storytelling, showcasing the country's ability to produce compelling narratives that resonate with audiences worldwide.

### Conclusion

As Juanjo Giménez Peña prepares to begin shooting "Pínkala," the film promises to be an exciting addition to his oeuvre and the broader landscape of Spanish cinema. With its supernatural elements and a focus on love, "Pínkala" is poised to capture the imagination of viewers and potentially secure a place in the pantheon of notable films emerging from Spain. The film's production in Vigo will undoubtedly add a unique flavor to its storytelling, making it a project to watch in the coming months.
