## Heretic Acquires Rights to Serbian Film 'Until the Day Ends'

Heretic, a prominent film distribution company, has secured the world rights to the debut feature film "Until the Day Ends," directed by Serbian filmmaker Jelena Maksimović. This significant acquisition highlights the growing interest in international cinema, particularly in narratives that resonate with contemporary social movements.

## Film Overview and Context

"Until the Day Ends" is set against the backdrop of Serbia's student protests, a pivotal moment in the country’s recent history. The film centers on Lena, portrayed by Lena Trifunović, an anarchist student who is deeply involved in Belgrade's anti-government movement. The narrative promises to explore themes of activism, youth engagement, and the struggle for political change, reflecting the sentiments of a generation seeking to make its voice heard.

The film also features Andrija, who plays a key role alongside Trifunović, contributing to the film's exploration of personal and political dynamics during a time of unrest. This focus on youth activism in Serbia aims to shed light on the broader implications of political dissent and the courage required to stand against authority.

## Premiere at Venice Film Festival

"Until the Day Ends" is set to make its world premiere in September as part of the Horizons section at the prestigious Venice Film Festival. This platform is known for showcasing innovative and avant-garde films, making it an ideal venue for Maksimović's debut. The film's selection for Venice underscores its artistic merit and the relevance of its subject matter in today's global context.

The Horizons section is particularly significant as it aims to highlight new trends in cinema and support emerging filmmakers. By featuring "Until the Day Ends," the festival not only recognizes Maksimović's talent but also emphasizes the importance of storytelling that engages with pressing social issues.
