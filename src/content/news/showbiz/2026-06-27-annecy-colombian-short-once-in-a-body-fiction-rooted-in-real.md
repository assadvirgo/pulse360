## Colombian Short Film ‘Once in a Body’ Explores Themes of Human Connection

At the recent Annecy Animation Festival, Colombian animator María Cristina Pérez showcased her latest work, "Once in a Body" ("Una vez en un Cuerpo"), as part of the festival's Perspectives sidebar. This experimental short film marks a significant milestone in Pérez's burgeoning career, highlighting her unique approach to storytelling that emphasizes the importance of human connection.

### A Personal Dedication

"Once in a Body" is Pérez's fourth short film, and she has dedicated this work to her sister, infusing it with personal significance. The film draws from real experiences, reflecting Pérez's commitment to exploring themes that resonate on a deeply emotional level. By weaving her own narrative into the fabric of the film, she invites viewers to engage with the story in a more intimate way.

### Thematic Depth

The central theme of human connection is prevalent throughout Pérez's body of work. In "Once in a Body," she examines the complexities of relationships and the profound impact they have on individual identity. The film's narrative structure and visual style are designed to evoke a sense of empathy, encouraging audiences to reflect on their own connections with others.

Pérez's work stands out not only for its thematic depth but also for its innovative animation techniques. By blending traditional and experimental methods, she creates a visually captivating experience that complements the emotional weight of her storytelling. This approach has garnered attention in the competitive landscape of animation, positioning her as a rising talent in the industry.

### Recognition at Annecy

The Annecy Animation Festival is renowned for celebrating the art of animation and providing a platform for emerging filmmakers. Competing in the Perspectives sidebar is a notable achievement for Pérez, as it showcases her work alongside other innovative projects from around the world. The festival serves as a vital space for dialogue and exchange among creators, and Pérez's participation highlights the growing recognition of Colombian talent in the international animation scene.

### Future Prospects

As María Cristina Pérez continues to develop her craft, "Once in a Body" serves as a testament to her artistic vision and dedication to storytelling. Her focus on human connection resonates with audiences, making her work relevant in today's increasingly fragmented world. With each new project, Pérez is poised to further explore the intricacies of relationships and identity, solidifying her place in the animation landscape.

In a time when the need for connection is more pronounced than ever, Pérez's work not only entertains but also invites reflection on the bonds that unite us. As she moves forward in her career, her commitment to authentic storytelling will undoubtedly continue to inspire and engage viewers worldwide.
