## Bowen Yang Discusses Career Decisions and Comedy Roots with Rachel Sennott

In a recent episode of the "Actors on Actors" series presented by Variety and CNN, comedian Bowen Yang opened up about his career trajectory, including a pivotal moment involving a phone call from Lorne Michaels, the creator and executive producer of "Saturday Night Live" (SNL). During the conversation with fellow comedian Rachel Sennott, Yang reflected on his early days in comedy and the influences that shaped his artistic journey.

### A Surprising Call from Lorne Michaels

Yang revealed that he had initially planned to exit SNL but decided to postpone his departure after receiving an unexpected call from Michaels. This conversation proved to be a turning point for Yang, who has been a prominent cast member on the show since 2019. The gravity of Michaels' influence in the comedy world and the opportunity to continue contributing to a storied institution like SNL led Yang to reassess his decision.

"I was ready to move on, but that call made me think twice," Yang shared. He emphasized the importance of mentorship and guidance in the industry, particularly from figures like Michaels, who have shaped the careers of many successful comedians.

### Comedy Origins and the Brooklyn Scene

The discussion also touched on Yang and Sennott's shared experiences in the alternative stand-up scene of Brooklyn, where both comedians began their careers. They reminisced about the vibrant community of performers who pushed boundaries and explored new comedic styles. This environment not only fostered their individual talents but also allowed them to connect with a diverse audience.

Sennott, known for her unique comedic voice and social commentary, expressed admiration for Yang's ability to navigate the complexities of modern comedy while maintaining authenticity. The two comedians highlighted the importance of staying true to oneself in an industry that often demands conformity.

### Reflections on "I Love L.A."

In addition to their personal anecdotes, Yang and Sennott discussed their experiences in Los Angeles, a city that has become a significant hub for the entertainment industry. Yang's affection for L.A. was evident as he described the city's blend of opportunity and challenges for aspiring comedians. He noted that while the city offers a platform for visibility, it also requires resilience and adaptability.

Sennott echoed these sentiments, emphasizing the importance of community support in the face of the competitive nature of the industry. Both comedians acknowledged that their success is not solely attributed to individual talent but also to the networks they have built over the years.

### Looking Ahead

As Yang continues his journey with SNL and Sennott explores her own comedic projects, both artists remain committed to pushing the boundaries of their craft. Their conversation serves as a reminder of the evolving landscape of comedy and the significance of mentorship, community, and personal growth.
