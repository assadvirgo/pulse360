## Indonesia Aims for Country of Honor Status at Cannes 2028

Indonesia is setting its sights on achieving the prestigious Country of Honor designation at the Cannes Film Festival in 2028. This ambitious goal was articulated by Culture Minister Fadli Zon during an exclusive interview at this year's Cannes event. Zon emphasized the importance of elevating Indonesia's presence in the global screen industry, aiming to transition the nation from a peripheral player to a central and influential force.

### A Vision for Global Recognition

Minister Zon outlined a comprehensive strategy aimed at enhancing Indonesia's cultural footprint on the international stage. He stated that the initiative is not merely about gaining prestige but is also focused on fostering a vibrant ecosystem for filmmakers and artists within the country. By targeting the Cannes Film Festival, one of the most prestigious film events globally, Indonesia seeks to showcase its rich cultural heritage and diverse storytelling traditions.

Zon highlighted that this endeavor aligns with broader efforts to promote Indonesia's cultural identity and creative industries. He believes that achieving recognition at Cannes will not only benefit filmmakers but will also attract international collaborations and investments, thereby bolstering the local economy.

### Strengthening the Film Industry

The Culture Minister's vision includes a multi-faceted approach to strengthen Indonesia's film industry. This involves increasing funding for local productions, providing training and resources for emerging filmmakers, and establishing partnerships with international film organizations. Zon noted that by investing in talent development and infrastructure, Indonesia can cultivate a new generation of storytellers who can compete on the global stage.

Moreover, the government plans to leverage digital platforms to enhance the distribution of Indonesian films, making them more accessible to international audiences. This strategy aims to showcase the unique narratives and artistic expressions that define Indonesian cinema, further solidifying the country's cultural relevance.

### Cultural Diplomacy and Global Engagement

Minister Zon emphasized the role of cultural diplomacy in achieving Indonesia's goals at Cannes. He believes that engaging with global audiences through film can foster mutual understanding and appreciation of diverse cultures. By participating actively in international film festivals, Indonesia can position itself as a key player in the global cultural dialogue.

The Culture Minister's aspirations for Cannes 2028 also reflect a broader trend among countries seeking to promote their cultural narratives on the world stage. As nations increasingly recognize the power of cinema as a tool for soft diplomacy, Indonesia's strategic focus on the Cannes Film Festival underscores its commitment to elevating its cultural narrative.

### Conclusion

As Indonesia embarks on this ambitious journey towards becoming the Country of Honor at Cannes 2028, the government's proactive approach and commitment to nurturing its film industry signal a promising future for Indonesian cinema. With the right investments and strategies in place, the nation is poised to emerge as a significant player in the global screen industry, showcasing its rich cultural heritage and diverse storytelling to the world.
