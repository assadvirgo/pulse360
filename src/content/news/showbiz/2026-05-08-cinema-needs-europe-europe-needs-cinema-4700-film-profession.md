## 4,700 Film Professionals Urge EU to Ensure Future of Film Funding

In a significant call to action, over 4,700 film professionals, including renowned directors, actors, and producers, have signed an open letter urging the European Union (EU) to safeguard and enhance its funding for the film industry. This collective effort highlights the critical role that cinema plays in European culture and the economy, as well as the urgent need for sustainable financial support in the face of evolving industry challenges.

## A Plea for the MEDIA Programme

The letter specifically addresses the EU's MEDIA programme, which has been instrumental in supporting the film and audiovisual sectors across Europe. Signatories include prominent figures such as Francis Ford Coppola, Juliette Binoche, and Stellan Skarsgård, who collectively emphasize that "Cinema needs Europe, and Europe needs cinema." Their statement reflects a shared belief that a robust film industry is essential not only for cultural expression but also for economic vitality and social cohesion within the EU.

The MEDIA programme, established in 1991, has provided funding for various aspects of film production, distribution, and promotion. However, as the landscape of the film industry continues to evolve—especially with the rise of streaming platforms and changing audience behaviors—there are growing concerns that existing funding mechanisms may not be sufficient to meet future challenges.

## The Importance of Cultural Identity

The signatories of the letter argue that European cinema is a vital part of the continent's cultural identity and diversity. They contend that without adequate financial support, many independent and innovative projects may struggle to find the resources needed to come to fruition. The letter calls for a "future-proof" approach to funding that not only maintains but enhances the MEDIA programme to ensure it can adapt to new technologies and market dynamics.

The film industry in Europe has faced significant disruptions in recent years, particularly due to the COVID-19 pandemic, which led to widespread cinema closures and production halts. As the industry begins to recover, the need for reliable funding sources becomes even more pressing. The open letter serves as a rallying cry for stakeholders to unite in advocating for policies that will sustain and elevate European cinema.

## A Broader Conversation on Funding and Support

This initiative is part of a broader conversation about the role of government and public institutions in supporting the arts. As cultural industries worldwide grapple with the implications of digital transformation, the EU's commitment to preserving and promoting its cinematic heritage is under scrutiny. The letter's signatories stress that investing in film is not merely a financial decision but a cultural imperative that has far-reaching implications for society at large.

The call to action has garnered attention across various media platforms, igniting discussions among policymakers, industry leaders, and audiences alike. As the EU prepares for future funding decisions, the voices of these 4,700 professionals may play a pivotal role in shaping the direction of film support in Europe.

## Conclusion

As the film industry navigates an increasingly complex landscape, the collective plea from these industry professionals underscores the necessity of robust support systems. The open letter serves as a reminder of the intrinsic value of cinema in fostering cultural dialogue, creativity, and economic growth across Europe. The outcome of this advocacy could have lasting implications for the future of European cinema and its global standing.
