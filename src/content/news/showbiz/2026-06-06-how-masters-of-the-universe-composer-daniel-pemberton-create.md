## Composer Daniel Pemberton Brings ‘Masters of the Universe’ to Life with Iconic Sound

In a bold move that merges nostalgia with contemporary flair, composer Daniel Pemberton has crafted the theme for the upcoming "Masters of the Universe" film, drawing inspiration from legendary bands Queen and ABBA. This collaboration marks a significant departure from Pemberton's usual approach, as he typically refrains from working on franchise films or sequels. However, the creative vision presented by director Travis Knight proved compelling enough to entice him into the project.

### A Unique Musical Vision

Pemberton's decision to join the "Masters of the Universe" team was contingent upon his ability to infuse the score with the vibrant and exuberant sounds reminiscent of Queen and ABBA. Both bands are known for their catchy melodies and theatrical flair, qualities that align perfectly with the adventurous spirit of the "Masters of the Universe" universe. Pemberton's intention was to create a score that is "unashamedly fun," capturing the essence of the characters and the fantastical world they inhabit.

### Collaborating with Rock Legends

A notable highlight of Pemberton's scoring process was his collaboration with Brian May, the iconic guitarist of Queen. May's distinctive "Red Special" guitar was utilized during the recording sessions, adding an authentic rock sound to the score. This partnership not only enriched the musical texture but also served as a nod to the legacy of Queen, whose influence is palpable throughout the score.

Pemberton's approach to scoring the film reflects a deep understanding of the source material, as well as a desire to evoke the same sense of joy and excitement that fans experienced with the original animated series. By incorporating elements that resonate with both new audiences and long-time followers of the franchise, Pemberton aims to create a memorable auditory experience that complements the visual spectacle of the film.

### The Role of Music in Storytelling

Music plays a pivotal role in storytelling, particularly in fantasy genres where the emotional landscape is often heightened. Pemberton's score is designed to enhance the film's narrative, guiding audiences through the various arcs and character developments. The combination of orchestral elements with rock influences allows for a dynamic soundscape that reflects the film's themes of adventure, heroism, and camaraderie.

Director Travis Knight's vision for the film is one of inclusivity and celebration of the original material while also pushing boundaries. Pemberton's score is a crucial element in achieving this vision, providing a soundtrack that is both modern and rooted in the beloved traditions of classic rock.

### Looking Ahead

As anticipation builds for the release of "Masters of the Universe," Pemberton's work serves as a reminder of the power of music in film. By embracing a playful and energetic sound, he invites audiences to embark on a journey filled with excitement and nostalgia. The collaboration with Brian May and the incorporation of iconic musical influences underscore the film's commitment to honoring its legacy while forging a new path forward.

In conclusion, Daniel Pemberton's score for "Masters of the Universe" is poised to resonate with audiences, offering a fresh take on a classic story. With its blend of fun, rock influences, and cinematic storytelling, the music promises to be a defining element of the film experience.
