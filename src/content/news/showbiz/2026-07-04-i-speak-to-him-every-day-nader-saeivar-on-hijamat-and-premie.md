## Nader Saeivar Discusses 'Hijamat' and the Absence of Jafar Panahi

In a recent interview, Iranian filmmaker Nader Saeivar shared insights into his latest project, "Hijamat," which premiered at the Karlovy Vary International Film Festival. The film, set in Berlin, explores themes of connection and cultural identity, drawing its title from an ancient practice that holds significant meaning within the narrative.

### The Impact of Jafar Panahi's Absence

A notable aspect of Saeivar's journey with "Hijamat" is the absence of his close friend and collaborator, Jafar Panahi. Panahi, a prominent figure in Iranian cinema, has faced restrictions preventing him from leaving Iran. Despite this, he played a crucial role in the film's development as its editor and producer. Saeivar emphasized the importance of their daily communication, stating, "I speak to him every day." This ongoing dialogue not only reflects their friendship but also underscores the collaborative spirit that permeates their work, even amidst challenging circumstances.

### The Significance of 'Hijamat'

The term "Hijamat" refers to a traditional practice that involves cupping therapy, which is believed to have both physical and spiritual benefits. Saeivar explained that this ancient method serves as a metaphor within the film, symbolizing the healing and connection that individuals seek in their lives. He expressed a belief that "everybody needs" such practices, especially in today's fast-paced world where personal connections can often be overlooked.

### A Berlin Setting with Universal Themes

Set against the backdrop of Berlin, "Hijamat" delves into the lives of its characters as they navigate their identities and relationships in a foreign land. Saeivar's choice of location is significant, as it reflects the experiences of many Iranians who find themselves in diaspora, grappling with their cultural heritage while adapting to new environments. The film's narrative is expected to resonate with a diverse audience, highlighting universal themes of belonging and the search for self.

### Reception at Karlovy Vary

The premiere at the Karlovy Vary International Film Festival marks a significant milestone for Saeivar, showcasing his work on an international platform. The festival is renowned for its celebration of cinematic artistry and provides a unique opportunity for filmmakers to present their stories to a global audience. As "Hijamat" unfolds its narrative, it invites viewers to reflect on the complexities of identity and the enduring bonds of friendship, even when physical separation exists.

### Conclusion

Nader Saeivar's "Hijamat" stands as a testament to the resilience of artistic expression in the face of adversity. Through his collaboration with Jafar Panahi and the exploration of culturally rich themes, Saeivar has crafted a film that not only entertains but also encourages introspection. As audiences engage with "Hijamat," they are reminded of the importance of connection, healing, and the shared human experience, transcending borders and cultural divides.
