The 2026 FIFA World Cup, which commenced on Thursday, has already made headlines with a remarkable achievement in viewership. The opening match, featuring Mexico against South Africa, has set a new record as the most-watched opening game in the history of the tournament.

According to reports, the match attracted an impressive 12.1 million viewers across various platforms, including NBCUniversal's Telemundo, Peacock, and Telemundo's streaming services. This figure highlights the growing global interest in soccer, particularly in the context of the World Cup, which is known for its ability to unite fans from diverse backgrounds.

In the opening game, Mexico secured a 2-0 victory over South Africa, showcasing their competitive spirit and skill on the field.
