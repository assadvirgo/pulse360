## Isa Briones Addresses Audience Behavior in Broadway's "Just in Time"

Isa Briones, a talented actress known for her recent role in HBO Max's "The Pitt," has made headlines as she returns to Broadway in the jukebox musical "Just in Time." Taking on the character of Connie Francis, Briones has found herself in the spotlight not only for her performance but also for addressing audience behavior during her shows.

### A Call for Respectful Engagement

In a recent statement, Briones expressed her frustration with audience members who have been vocal during performances, particularly those shouting references to her previous role as Dr. Trinity Santos. The actress emphasized the importance of maintaining a respectful atmosphere in the theater, reminding attendees that while engagement is welcome, disruptive behavior is not. 

"Y’all are pissin’ me off," Briones stated, urging theatergoers to refrain from yelling whatever they want during the performance. Her comments highlight a growing concern among performers about the impact of audience interactions on the live theater experience. 

### The Importance of the Theater Experience

Theater has long been a space for communal enjoyment, where audiences and performers share a unique bond. However, as live performances have evolved, so too have the expectations of both parties. Briones' remarks serve as a reminder that while spontaneity can enhance the experience, it should not come at the cost of the performance itself.

Briones' role in "Just in Time" marks a significant return to Broadway, and she is keen to ensure that the focus remains on the artistry of the production. The musical, which celebrates the music of Connie Francis, aims to transport audiences to a different era, and Briones is committed to delivering an authentic experience.

### A New Chapter for Briones

Having gained recognition for her work in "The Pitt," Briones is poised to make a lasting impression on Broadway audiences. Her transition from screen to stage highlights her versatility as an actress and her dedication to her craft. As she navigates this new chapter, Briones is not only focused on her performance but also on fostering a respectful environment for all attendees.

The theater community has rallied around the need for decorum in live performances, and Briones' candid remarks may resonate with many performers who have faced similar challenges. By advocating for a more considerate audience experience, she is contributing to a broader conversation about the dynamics of live theater.

### Conclusion

As Isa Briones continues her journey in "Just in Time," her call for respectful audience engagement serves as a crucial reminder of the collaborative nature of theater. While the excitement of live performances can lead to enthusiastic responses, it is essential for audience members to remember the impact of their behavior on the overall experience. Briones' commitment to her role and the integrity of the performance underscores the importance of mutual respect in the world of live entertainment.
