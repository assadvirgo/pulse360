## Cinetic Media Acquires North American Sales Rights to 'Manhunt'

Cinetic Media has secured the North American sales rights to the highly anticipated film "Manhunt," which is set to make its world premiere in the main competition at the Locarno Film Festival. This acquisition positions Cinetic Media to play a significant role in the film's distribution across the North American market, coinciding with its competition for the prestigious Golden Leopard award at the festival.

### A Promising Film and Director

"Manhunt" is the sophomore feature from Canadian filmmaker Wayne Wapeemukwa, who gained recognition for his debut film "Luk’Luk’I." The latter won the award for Best Canadian First Film, establishing Wapeemukwa as a noteworthy talent in the industry. With "Manhunt," expectations are high as it enters the competitive landscape of the Locarno Film Festival, which is known for showcasing innovative and thought-provoking cinema.

### The Significance of the Locarno Film Festival

The Locarno Film Festival, held annually in Switzerland, is one of the oldest and most respected film festivals in the world. It attracts filmmakers, critics, and audiences from around the globe, providing a platform for new voices and unique storytelling. Competing for the Golden Leopard, the festival's top honor, can significantly enhance a film's visibility and marketability, making Cinetic Media's acquisition particularly timely and strategic.

### International Sales Rights

In a parallel development, Aspect Ratio has acquired the international sales rights to "Manhunt." This dual acquisition by Cinetic Media and Aspect Ratio highlights the film's potential appeal beyond North America, suggesting that "Manhunt" may resonate with audiences worldwide. The collaboration between these two companies indicates a strong belief in the film's narrative and artistic merit.

### What to Expect from 'Manhunt'

While specific plot details about "Manhunt" remain under wraps, Wapeemukwa's previous work has been noted for its exploration of complex themes and character-driven narratives. Industry insiders are eager to see how he builds upon his debut with this new project. The film's participation in the Locarno Film Festival will likely provide a critical platform for it to garner attention from both critics and audiences alike.

### Conclusion

The acquisition of "Manhunt" by Cinetic Media and Aspect Ratio reflects a growing interest in diverse and compelling narratives within the film industry. As the film prepares for its world premiere at Locarno, it is poised to capture the attention of festival-goers and industry professionals, potentially paving the way for a successful release in North America and beyond. With Wapeemukwa at the helm, "Manhunt" promises to be a significant addition to contemporary cinema, and its journey is one to watch closely in the coming months.
