## Industry Leaders to Address Producers Guild Conference

The Producers Guild of America (PGA) has announced an impressive lineup of speakers for its annual Produced By Conference, set to take place on May 30 at the Universal Studios Lot in Los Angeles. Among the notable figures scheduled to speak are Jason Blum, Michael De Luca, and Tom Quinn, each of whom has made significant contributions to the film and entertainment industry.

### Keynote Speakers

Jason Blum, the CEO and founder of Blumhouse Productions, is renowned for his innovative approach to horror films, having produced critically acclaimed hits such as "Get Out" and "The Purge." His production company has become synonymous with low-budget, high-return films, and Blum's insights into the changing landscape of film production are highly anticipated.

Michael De Luca, co-chair and CEO of Warner Bros. Motion Picture Group, brings a wealth of experience from his tenure at major studios. De Luca has been instrumental in the production of numerous successful films across various genres, and his perspective on the future of studio filmmaking and the evolving role of producers will be invaluable to attendees.

Tom Quinn, the CEO and founder of Neon, a company known for its bold and unique film selections, will also share his experiences. Under his leadership, Neon has garnered critical acclaim for films like "Parasite," which won the Academy Award for Best Picture in 2020. Quinn's approach to marketing and distributing independent films in a competitive market will provide a fresh viewpoint for producers and industry professionals.

### Conference Overview

The Produced By Conference is a significant event in the entertainment calendar, bringing together producers, executives, and industry professionals to discuss the latest trends, challenges, and opportunities in film and television production. The conference offers a platform for networking, learning, and sharing ideas, with a focus on the art and business of producing.

This year's event is expected to attract a diverse audience, from emerging filmmakers to seasoned industry veterans, all eager to gain insights from leaders who have shaped modern cinema. In addition to keynote speeches, the conference will feature panel discussions, workshops, and networking opportunities designed to foster collaboration and innovation within the industry.

### Conclusion

As the entertainment industry continues to evolve, events like the Produced By Conference play a crucial role in shaping the future of film and television production. With industry giants like Blum, De Luca, and Quinn at the forefront of discussions, attendees can look forward to an enriching experience that will inspire and inform their work in the field. The conference promises to be a pivotal gathering for those passionate about storytelling and the art of producing.
