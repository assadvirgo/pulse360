## Disney Unscripted Chief Discusses Future of 'Secret Lives of Mormon Wives'

In a recent interview with The Hollywood Reporter, Rob Mills, the unscripted chief at Disney, provided insights into the future of the popular Hulu reality series "Secret Lives of Mormon Wives." Mills emphasized the importance of ensuring the cast's comfort and well-being as the show contemplates its next steps.

### Prioritizing Cast Well-Being

Mills stated, "We wanted to make sure [the cast feels] good about returning. We never would have gone back if it was anything other than that." This commitment to the cast's emotional and psychological safety reflects a growing trend in reality television, where producers are increasingly aware of the potential impact of filming on participants' lives. The emphasis on cast comfort is particularly relevant for a show that delves into the complex lives of its subjects, which may include sensitive topics related to faith, family, and personal choices.

### The Evolution of Reality Television

The landscape of reality television has evolved significantly over the years, with audiences becoming more discerning about the authenticity and ethical considerations of the content they consume. Mills' comments highlight a shift towards a more responsible approach in the industry, where the mental health of cast members is prioritized alongside entertainment value. This is especially crucial for a series like "Secret Lives of Mormon Wives," which explores the intricacies of life within a specific religious community.

### Broader Context: Disney's Unscripted Strategy

Mills also touched on the broader strategy for Disney's unscripted programming, noting the importance of attracting talent from other successful reality shows. He mentioned the recent courting of "Summer House" star Ciara Miller for "Dancing with the Stars" (DWTS), illustrating the interconnected nature of reality television franchises. This approach not only brings fresh faces to established shows but also enhances viewer engagement by leveraging the popularity of existing stars.

### The State of 'The Bachelor' Franchise

In addition to discussing "Secret Lives of Mormon Wives," Mills provided an update on the state of "The Bachelor" franchise, which has been a staple of reality television for nearly two decades. While specific details were not disclosed, the franchise has faced scrutiny and challenges in recent years, prompting producers to consider new directions and innovations to maintain its relevance in a competitive entertainment landscape.

### Conclusion

As "Secret Lives of Mormon Wives" navigates its future, the commitment to cast well-being remains a central focus for Disney's unscripted division. Mills' insights underscore a broader industry trend towards responsible reality programming that prioritizes the mental health of participants while continuing to engage audiences. As the series and others like it evolve, viewers can expect a more thoughtful approach to storytelling that respects the complexities of the lives being portrayed.
