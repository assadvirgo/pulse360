## Chace Crawford and P.J. Byrne to Star in Amazon's New Black Comedy Series 'We Were Once Men'

In a notable development for Amazon Prime Video, actors Chace Crawford and P.J. Byrne are set to reunite in a new black comedy series titled *We Were Once Men*. This project is currently in development and promises to showcase the duo in a different light, transitioning from their previous roles as adversaries in the hit series *The Boys*.

### A Shift in Dynamics

Crawford and Byrne, who portrayed characters with a contentious relationship in *The Boys*, will take on the roles of brothers in *We Were Once Men*. This shift from antagonism to familial bonds offers an intriguing narrative potential, allowing the actors to explore new dimensions of their on-screen chemistry. The series is expected to blend humor with darker themes, characteristic of the black comedy genre, and aims to resonate with audiences who appreciate both wit and depth in storytelling.

### Creative Team Behind the Project

The series is penned by Matt Berns, a writer known for his contributions to various successful projects. While specific plot details remain under wraps, the involvement of Berns suggests a thoughtful approach to character development and narrative structure. His previous work has garnered attention for its clever writing and engaging story arcs, which may bode well for *We Were Once Men*.

### Executive Production

Jennifer Salke, head of Amazon Studios, is set to executive produce the series. Salke's leadership has been pivotal in curating a diverse range of content for Amazon Prime Video, and her involvement in *We Were Once Men* underscores the platform's commitment to innovative storytelling. Under her guidance, Amazon has expanded its portfolio with a variety of genres and formats, aiming to attract a broad audience.

### The Future of 'The Boys'

The announcement of *We Were Once Men* comes as fans of *The Boys* continue to express interest in the franchise. The series has been praised for its satirical take on superhero culture and its complex character dynamics. As the universe of *The Boys* continues to evolve, projects like *We Were Once Men* may serve to deepen the connection between its cast and the audience, while also exploring new narratives within the same creative ecosystem.

### Conclusion

As *We Were Once Men* moves forward in development, anticipation builds around how Crawford and Byrne will navigate their new roles as brothers. The combination of a talented writing team and experienced executive production suggests that the series could become a noteworthy addition to Amazon's lineup. Fans of both actors and the black comedy genre will undoubtedly be keeping a close eye on this project as it progresses.
