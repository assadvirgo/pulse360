## John Oliver Critiques Trump’s Focus on Ballroom and Pool Maintenance

In the latest episode of "Last Week Tonight," host John Oliver took aim at former President Donald Trump, mocking his priorities regarding the construction of a ballroom and upgrades to the Reflecting Pool at the White House. Oliver's sharp commentary highlighted what he perceives as a misalignment between the former president's focus on aesthetic improvements and the pressing responsibilities of national governance.

### A Focus on Aesthetics Over Governance

During the segment, Oliver expressed his disbelief at Trump's reported inclination to prioritize the renovation of social spaces over critical policy issues. "If you are a president who'd rather focus on ballroom construction and pool maintenance than the crucial work of actually running a country, then ... you are not a smart f***ing person," Oliver stated, echoing Trump's own rhetoric to underscore the absurdity of the situation.

The critique is part of a broader narrative that Oliver has developed around Trump's presidency, often juxtaposing the former president's personal interests with the expectations of effective leadership. By framing the discussion around Trump's focus on luxury and maintenance, Oliver raises questions about the implications of such priorities in the context of national governance.

### Reflecting Pool Upgrades

The Reflecting Pool, a significant landmark in Washington, D.C., has been a subject of various discussions regarding its maintenance and upgrades. Oliver's commentary suggests that Trump’s attention to the pool's condition may reflect a broader tendency to prioritize image over substance. The host emphasized that while aesthetics can be important, they should not overshadow the fundamental responsibilities of a leader to address critical issues facing the nation.
