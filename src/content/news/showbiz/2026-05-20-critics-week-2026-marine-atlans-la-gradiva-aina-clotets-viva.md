## Critics’ Week 2026: Marine Atlan and Aina Clotet Shine at the Awards

The 65th edition of Critics' Week, a prestigious segment of the Cannes Film Festival, concluded with notable recognition for emerging filmmakers. Marine Atlan's film "La Gradiva" received the esteemed Ami Paris Grand Prize, while Aina Clotet's "Alive" was honored with the Louis Roederer Foundation Rising Star Award. 

### Celebrating New Talent

Critics' Week, curated by Ava Cahen, is dedicated to showcasing first and second films from directors around the globe. This year, the event continued its tradition of highlighting innovative storytelling and fresh perspectives in cinema. The awards presented during the ceremony reflect a commitment to nurturing new talent in the film industry.

### Marine Atlan's "La Gradiva"

"La Gradiva," directed by Marine Atlan, captivated audiences and critics alike, ultimately securing the top honor of the festival. The film's narrative and artistic vision have been praised for their depth and originality. Atlan's work is seen as a significant contribution to contemporary cinema, showcasing her ability to blend storytelling with striking visual elements.

### Aina Clotet's "Alive"

Aina Clotet's "Alive" also made a strong impact, earning the Louis Roederer Foundation Rising Star Award. This accolade is particularly noteworthy as it recognizes the potential of filmmakers who are just beginning their careers. Clotet's film has been commended for its engaging plot and emotional resonance, marking her as a talent to watch in the coming years.

### The Importance of Critics' Week

Critics' Week serves as a vital platform for new filmmakers to gain exposure and recognition in an industry often dominated by established names. By focusing on first and second films, the section encourages innovation and diversity in storytelling. The awards presented this year not only celebrate individual achievements but also highlight the importance of supporting new voices in cinema.

### Conclusion

As the film industry continues to evolve, events like Critics' Week play a crucial role in fostering new talent and celebrating creativity. The recognition given to Marine Atlan and Aina Clotet at this year's festival underscores the importance of nurturing emerging filmmakers who are poised to shape the future of cinema. Their successes at Cannes serve as an inspiration for aspiring directors and a reminder of the ever-changing landscape of film.
