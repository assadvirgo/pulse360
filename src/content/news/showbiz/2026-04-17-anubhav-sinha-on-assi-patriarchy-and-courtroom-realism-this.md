## Anubhav Sinha Discusses 'Assi': A Deep Dive into Patriarchy and Courtroom Realism

Renowned Indian filmmaker Anubhav Sinha has long been an advocate for socially relevant cinema, and his latest project, "Assi," continues this tradition. In a recent interview with Variety, Sinha opened up about the years of unease that preceded the film's creation, emphasizing the importance of addressing sexual violence within the context of Indian society.

### A Persistent Issue

Sinha's journey with "Assi" reflects a broader societal concern that has been echoed in various forms through protests and media coverage. The director, known for his impactful works such as "Mulk," which tackled communal tensions, has been deeply affected by the ongoing discourse surrounding sexual violence in India. "The subject of sexual violence never fully left me between news cycles and candle-march protests," he stated. This sentiment underscores the pervasive nature of the issue, suggesting that it lingers in the collective consciousness of society.

### Beyond Individual Narratives

In his discussion, Sinha made it clear that "Assi" is not merely a tale of an individual but a narrative that encapsulates the broader implications of rape and patriarchal structures. "This is a story of rape. This is not the story of an individual," he asserted. This perspective aims to shift the focus from personal tragedy to a societal critique, highlighting how systemic issues contribute to the prevalence of sexual violence.

### Courtroom Realism

"Assi" also delves into the complexities of the legal system as it pertains to sexual assault cases. Sinha's intention is to portray courtroom realism, shedding light on the often arduous journey victims face in seeking justice. By presenting a realistic depiction of the judicial process, the film aims to educate audiences about the challenges and obstacles that survivors encounter, further amplifying the conversation surrounding sexual violence.

### The Role of Cinema in Social Change

Sinha's commitment to addressing social issues through film reflects a growing trend among Indian filmmakers who are using their platforms to challenge societal norms and provoke thought. The director believes that cinema can serve as a powerful tool for social change, capable of influencing public perception and encouraging dialogue. "Assi" is poised to contribute to this movement, aiming to inspire audiences to confront uncomfortable truths about patriarchy and its ramifications.

### Conclusion

As "Assi" prepares for its release, Anubhav Sinha's insights remind us of the critical role that storytelling plays in addressing pressing social issues. By focusing on the systemic nature of sexual violence and the realities of the courtroom, the film seeks to foster a deeper understanding of the challenges faced by survivors. In a society grappling with these issues, "Assi" stands as a testament to the power of cinema to provoke thought, inspire change, and ultimately, contribute to a more equitable world.
