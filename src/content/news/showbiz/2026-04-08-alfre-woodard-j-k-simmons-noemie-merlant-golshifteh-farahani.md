## Arnaud Desplechin's 'The Thing That Hurts' Begins Production in France

Acclaimed French director Arnaud Desplechin has commenced production on his latest film, *The Thing That Hurts*, which boasts an impressive ensemble cast including Alfre Woodard, J.K. Simmons, Noémie Merlant, Golshifteh Farahani, and Felicity Jones. The film is currently being shot in Paris, a city that plays a pivotal role in the narrative.

### A Star-Studded Cast

The casting of *The Thing That Hurts* brings together a diverse group of talented actors, each known for their significant contributions to film and television. Alfre Woodard, an Emmy Award-winning actress, is recognized for her powerful performances in both drama and film. J.K. Simmons, an Oscar-winning actor, is celebrated for his roles in various genres, from intense dramas to light-hearted comedies. The film also features Jason Schwartzman and André Holland, both of whom have carved out distinctive careers in independent cinema and mainstream film.

Noémie Merlant, who gained international recognition for her role in *Portrait of a Lady on Fire*, and Golshifteh Farahani, an Iranian actress known for her work in both Iranian and international films, further enrich the cast. Felicity Jones, who has garnered acclaim for her performances in films such as *The Theory of Everything*, rounds out this impressive lineup.

### A Unique Narrative

Written by Kamen Velkovsky and Arnaud Desplechin, *The Thing That Hurts* explores the lives of a group of patients who converge in Paris following a significant event—the death of a loved one. This premise sets the stage for a deep exploration of grief, connection, and the human experience, themes that are often central to Desplechin's work.

### Executive Production by Wes Anderson

Adding to the film's allure is the involvement of Wes Anderson as an executive producer. Known for his distinctive visual style and storytelling approach, Anderson’s participation is expected to bring a unique perspective to the project. His previous collaborations and support for innovative storytelling in cinema have made him a respected figure in the industry.

### Financing and Production

*The Thing That Hurts* is financed by Silver Screen Global, a company known for its commitment to supporting diverse and engaging film projects. The collaboration between Desplechin and Silver Screen Global highlights the growing trend of international partnerships in film production, particularly in the European market.

As production unfolds in the picturesque settings of Paris, expectations are high for *The Thing That Hurts* to resonate with audiences worldwide. The film is anticipated to blend emotional depth with the artistic sensibilities characteristic of Desplechin’s previous works.

### Conclusion

With a compelling cast, a poignant narrative, and the backing of prominent figures in the film industry, *The Thing That Hurts* is poised to be a significant addition to the cinematic landscape. As the film progresses through production, it will be interesting to see how the collaboration between these talented individuals translates onto the screen, promising a rich exploration of human emotion and connection.
