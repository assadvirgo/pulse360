## Jim Gilstrap, Renowned R&B Singer and Session Musician, Passes Away at 79

Jim Gilstrap, the celebrated R&B singer and session musician, has died at the age of 79. His passing occurred on Saturday, July 18, from natural causes, as confirmed by his nephew, Gil Gilstrap, in a statement to various media outlets.

### A Legacy in Music

Gilstrap is perhaps best known for his contributions to the music industry during the 1970s, particularly for his work on the theme song of the iconic sitcom "Good Times." The show, which aired from 1974 to 1979, became a cultural touchstone, and its theme song remains a memorable part of television history. Gilstrap’s rich vocal style helped to define the sound of the era, making him a sought-after artist for both live performances and studio recordings.

In addition to his work on "Good Times," Gilstrap was featured prominently on Stevie Wonder's classic hit "You Are The Sunshine of My Life." This song, which won a Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Performance, showcases Gilstrap's vocal prowess and ability to blend seamlessly with Wonder's innovative sound. His contributions to the music of the 1970s and beyond have left an indelible mark on the industry.
