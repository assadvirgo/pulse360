## Barack Obama Joins Malcolm Gladwell in New Podcast Series on Reconstruction Era

Former President Barack Obama is lending his voice to a new podcast series titled “Reconstruction: The Unfinished Promise,” hosted by renowned author and podcaster Malcolm Gladwell. This eight-part audio series, which delves into the complexities and implications of the Reconstruction Era in American history, is produced in collaboration with the History Channel, the Obamas' Higher Ground production company, and Gladwell's Pushkin Industries in partnership with Audible.

### Exploring Historical Context

The podcast aims to shed light on the Reconstruction Era, a pivotal period following the Civil War that sought to address the inequalities faced by formerly enslaved individuals and to rebuild the nation. Despite its intentions, this era is often described as an "unfinished promise," as many of the goals set forth during this time were never fully realized. Obama’s participation is expected to bring significant attention to the series, given his prominence and commitment to discussions surrounding race, equality, and social justice.

### Insights from Notable Figures

In addition to Obama, the podcast features a range of voices and perspectives from historians, scholars, and cultural commentators who will provide insights into the historical, social, and political ramifications of the Reconstruction Era. The series aims to engage listeners not only with historical facts but also with the ongoing relevance of these issues in contemporary society.

### A Collaborative Effort

The collaboration between the Obamas and Malcolm Gladwell underscores a shared commitment to fostering meaningful dialogue about America’s past and its implications for the future. Higher Ground, the production company founded by Barack and Michelle Obama, has been involved in various projects that promote storytelling and educational content, reflecting their dedication to civic engagement and public discourse.

### Availability and Audience Engagement

“Reconstruction: The Unfinished Promise” is available for streaming on various platforms, including Audible, which allows for easy access to the series for a broad audience. The podcast format is particularly well-suited for engaging listeners who may be seeking to understand complex historical narratives in a more accessible manner.

### Conclusion

As the United States continues to grapple with issues of race and equity, the insights shared in this podcast series may serve as a crucial resource for understanding the historical context of these challenges. By featuring voices like Barack Obama and Malcolm Gladwell, the series is poised to contribute to a deeper understanding of America's past and its ongoing journey toward fulfilling the promises of equality and justice for all. 

Listeners are encouraged to tune in and reflect on the lessons of the Reconstruction Era, as they resonate with today’s societal dynamics.
