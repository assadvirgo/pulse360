## Producers Advocate for Early Involvement of Intimacy Coordinators in Production

The producers of the new German drama series "Game of Keys," which is available on Prime Video, are emphasizing the importance of integrating intimacy coordinators into the production process from the very beginning. This approach aims to enhance the portrayal of intimate scenes and ensure the safety and comfort of actors involved.

### Engaging Intimacy Coordinators Early

UFA Fiction's producers, Ulrike Leibfried and Sinah Swyter, have highlighted the benefits of collaborating with intimacy coordinators, specifically mentioning their partnership with Emilia Bähr. By involving Bähr from the casting stage, the team was able to create a more supportive environment for the actors, allowing them to engage in their roles with greater confidence and clarity.

Leibfried and Swyter's advocacy for early involvement of intimacy coordinators reflects a growing trend in the film and television industry. As productions increasingly recognize the complexities and sensitivities surrounding intimate scenes, the role of these professionals has become crucial in fostering a respectful and safe atmosphere on set.

### The Role of Intimacy Coordinators

Intimacy coordinators are trained professionals who specialize in choreographing intimate scenes in a way that prioritizes the well-being of actors. Their responsibilities include establishing clear boundaries, ensuring open communication, and creating a framework for consent. This is particularly important in an era where discussions around consent and the portrayal of intimacy are more prominent than ever.

In the case of "Game of Keys," the producers noted that having Bähr involved from the outset allowed for a more nuanced approach to storytelling. This proactive strategy not only enhances the quality of the production but also contributes to a healthier working environment for the cast and crew.

### Impact on the Industry

The call for early engagement of intimacy coordinators is part of a broader movement within the entertainment industry to address past shortcomings in the handling of intimate scenes. By prioritizing the emotional and physical safety of performers, productions can cultivate a culture of respect and professionalism.

As more creators adopt this practice, it is expected to lead to more authentic and responsible portrayals of intimacy on screen. The success of "Game of Keys" may serve as a case study for future projects, encouraging other producers to follow suit and integrate intimacy coordinators into their processes from the very beginning.

### Conclusion

The producers of "Game of Keys" are setting a precedent for the industry by advocating for the early involvement of intimacy coordinators. Their approach underscores the importance of creating safe spaces for actors and highlights the evolving standards in the production of intimate scenes. As the conversation around consent and safety continues to grow, the practices established in this series may influence future productions, leading to a more considerate and professional filmmaking landscape.
