## Tucker Carlson Critiqued on ‘SNL’ for Met Gala Commentary and Film Analysis

In a recent episode of "Saturday Night Live" (SNL), cast member Jeremy Culhane delivered a sharp impression of conservative commentator Tucker Carlson during the show's "Weekend Update" segment. This appearance marked another instance where SNL used humor and satire to comment on contemporary cultural events, including the Met Gala and the film "Michael."

### Met Gala Commentary

Culhane's portrayal of Carlson took aim at the Met Gala, an annual fundraising gala for the Metropolitan Museum of Art's Costume Institute in New York City. Known for its extravagant fashion and celebrity attendees, the event has often been a focal point for both admiration and critique. In his segment, Culhane mimicked Carlson's trademark style, questioning the essence of the event and its cultural significance. 

"What are we doing? Come on. Is this who we are now?" Culhane, as Carlson, asked rhetorically, reflecting a common sentiment among critics who view the gala as emblematic of excess and elitism in the fashion industry. The impression resonated with viewers, as it encapsulated the ongoing debate about the role of celebrity culture in society.

### Critique of the Film "Michael"

In addition to his commentary on the Met Gala, Culhane's Carlson also criticized the film "Michael," which has been the subject of discussion for its portrayal of the late pop icon Michael Jackson. Carlson's character expressed discontent with the film's narrative choices, specifically highlighting what he perceived as a significant omission: "the part when he was a white man." 

This comment alludes to the complexities surrounding Jackson's identity, particularly his changing appearance and the discussions around race that have emerged in the wake of his life and career. By addressing this topic, the segment not only provided a humorous take on Carlson's often controversial viewpoints but also touched upon deeper societal issues regarding race and representation in media.

### Cultural Impact and Reception

The SNL segment has sparked conversations on social media, with many viewers appreciating the show's ability to tackle sensitive topics through satire. The impression of Carlson, known for his polarizing opinions, serves as a vehicle for exploring broader cultural critiques, allowing audiences to reflect on the implications of celebrity culture and identity.
