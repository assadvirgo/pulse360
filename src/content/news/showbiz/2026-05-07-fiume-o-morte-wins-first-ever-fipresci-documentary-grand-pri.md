## FIPRESCI Honors 'Fiume O Morte!' with Inaugural Documentary Grand Prix

In a significant recognition of cinematic achievement, the international critics group FIPRESCI has awarded the inaugural FIPRESCI Documentary Grand Prix to the film *Fiume O Morte!*. This accolade marks a notable moment in the documentary film landscape, as it triumphs over established contenders, including the Oscar-winning *Mr. Nobody Against Putin* and the Oscar-nominated *The Perfect Neighbor*.

### Announcement at Millennium Docs Against Gravity

The announcement was made during the opening night of the Millennium Docs Against Gravity festival, held in Warsaw, Poland. This festival is known for showcasing a diverse array of documentary films from around the globe, and the introduction of the FIPRESCI Documentary Grand Prix adds a prestigious layer to its offerings. The award aims to highlight the best in documentary filmmaking, celebrating works that push boundaries and provoke thought.

### *Fiume O Morte!*: A Closer Look

*Fiume O Morte!* has garnered attention for its compelling narrative and artistic approach. Its victory over high-profile competitors underscores its impact and the strength of its storytelling.

### Competition Highlights

The competition for the FIPRESCI Documentary Grand Prix was fierce. *Mr. Nobody Against Putin*, which has already achieved critical acclaim and recognition at the Academy Awards, presents a powerful examination of political themes. Similarly, *The Perfect Neighbor* has been lauded for its intricate exploration of social issues. The fact that *Fiume O Morte!* has emerged victorious in such a competitive field speaks volumes about its quality and relevance.

### The Role of FIPRESCI

FIPRESCI, short for the Fédération Internationale de la Presse Cinématographique, is an organization dedicated to promoting cinema and supporting film critics worldwide. The group's recognition of *Fiume O Morte!* not only highlights the film's excellence but also emphasizes the importance of critical perspectives in the film industry. The establishment of the Documentary Grand Prix is a testament to FIPRESCI's commitment to fostering appreciation for documentary filmmaking.
