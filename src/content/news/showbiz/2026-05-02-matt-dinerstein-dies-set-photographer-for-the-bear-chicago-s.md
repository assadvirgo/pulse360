## Veteran Photographer Matt Dinerstein Passes Away at 69

Matt Dinerstein, a distinguished unit still photographer known for his work on acclaimed television series and films, passed away on May 1, 2023, in Chicago at the age of 69. His death was attributed to a long battle with brain cancer, a struggle he faced for 16 years, as confirmed by his brother, David Dinerstein, an Oscar-winning producer known for his work on the documentary "Summer of Soul."

## A Life Dedicated to Visual Storytelling

Born in 1956, Dinerstein developed a passion for photography early in his life, which eventually led him to a successful career in the entertainment industry. He became well-known for his ability to capture the essence of performances and behind-the-scenes moments, providing a visual narrative that complemented the stories being told on screen.

Dinerstein's portfolio includes work on popular television series, particularly the various "Chicago" shows, which have garnered critical acclaim and a loyal fan base. His contributions to the industry extended beyond television; he also worked on numerous films, showcasing his versatility and dedication to his craft.

## Impact on the Industry

Throughout his career, Dinerstein collaborated with a multitude of artists and filmmakers, earning respect and admiration from peers and colleagues alike. His photographs often served as promotional materials, capturing the spirit of productions and helping to generate interest among audiences. The images he created not only documented the creative process but also immortalized moments that resonated with viewers.

David Dinerstein highlighted his brother's unwavering dedication to his art, stating that Matt's passion for photography was evident in every project he undertook. "He had a unique ability to see the world through a different lens, and his work will continue to inspire future generations of photographers and filmmakers," he remarked.
