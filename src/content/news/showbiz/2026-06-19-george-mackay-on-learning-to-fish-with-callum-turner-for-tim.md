## George MacKay Discusses His Role in 'Rose of Nevada' and Bond Villain Aspirations

British actor George MacKay, known for his compelling performances in films such as "1917" and "Pride," is currently promoting his latest project, the time travel film "Rose of Nevada." In a recent interview, MacKay shared insights about his experiences on set, including a unique bonding moment with co-star Callum Turner while learning to fish for the film.

### A New Challenge: Learning to Fish

In "Rose of Nevada," MacKay and Turner portray characters who navigate the complexities of time travel, a theme that presents both narrative and character challenges. As part of their preparation, the actors engaged in various activities to enhance their on-screen chemistry and authenticity. One such activity was fishing, which MacKay described as a bonding experience that added depth to their characters.

"Learning to fish was not just about the skill itself; it was about connecting with Callum and understanding our characters' dynamics," MacKay explained. The process of fishing, he noted, mirrored the patience and focus required in their roles, allowing them to immerse themselves in the film's narrative.

### The Buzz Around James Bond Casting

As the conversation shifted to the ever-looming topic of James Bond casting rumors, MacKay acknowledged the excitement surrounding the iconic franchise. With names like Tom Francis and Turner himself being speculated as potential candidates for the next 007, the conversation naturally turned to MacKay's own thoughts on the matter.

While he expressed admiration for the franchise, MacKay humorously remarked that he would prefer to take on the role of a Bond villain. "You get the best scenes and then you get killed! It sounds like a fun ride," he quipped, highlighting the allure of playing a character that is both complex and memorable, even if their arc is short-lived.

### The Process of Filmmaking

For MacKay, the essence of acting lies in the process of filmmaking itself. He emphasized that each project provides a unique opportunity to explore different facets of storytelling and character development. "I love the process of diving into a character's world and discovering their motivations," he stated. This passion for his craft is evident in his diverse filmography, which showcases his versatility as an actor.

As he continues to navigate the landscape of contemporary cinema, MacKay remains focused on delivering authentic performances that resonate with audiences. His commitment to his roles, coupled with his willingness to embrace new challenges, positions him as a prominent figure in the British film industry.

### Conclusion

George MacKay's journey in "Rose of Nevada" is not only a testament to his talent but also an exploration of the collaborative nature of filmmaking. As he navigates the buzz surrounding potential Bond casting, his preference for playing a villain underscores a broader trend in contemporary cinema, where complex characters often steal the spotlight. With his dedication to the craft and a willingness to embrace new experiences, MacKay continues to captivate audiences and critics alike.
