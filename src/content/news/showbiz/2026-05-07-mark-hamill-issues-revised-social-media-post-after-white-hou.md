## Mark Hamill Revises Social Media Post Following White House Criticism

In a recent incident that has garnered significant attention, actor Mark Hamill has revised a social media post after receiving backlash from the White House. The original post featured an image that some interpreted as depicting a deceased former President Donald Trump. The White House responded by labeling Hamill's post as "sick," asserting that such rhetoric could contribute to a climate of violence and inspire assassination attempts against the president.

### Context of the Controversy

Mark Hamill, known for his iconic role as Luke Skywalker in the "Star Wars" franchise, has been an outspoken critic of Donald Trump throughout his presidency. His social media presence often includes political commentary and satirical content. However, this particular post crossed a line for many observers, including officials within the current administration.

In response to the criticism, Hamill took to the platform Bluesky to clarify his intentions. He stated, “Accurate Edit for Clarity: ‘He should live long enough to…’” suggesting that his original message may have been misinterpreted or taken out of context. This revision indicates a desire to mitigate the fallout from the initial post while maintaining his stance on political issues.

The incident has sparked a wide range of reactions from the public and political figures alike. Supporters of Hamill argue that artistic expression and satire should not be stifled, while detractors emphasize the potential dangers of inflammatory rhetoric, especially in a politically charged environment.
