## Ann Robinson, Star of ‘The War of the Worlds,’ Dies at 96

Ann Robinson, the renowned actress best known for her role in the 1953 science fiction classic "The War of the Worlds," has passed away at the age of 96. Her death marks the end of an era for fans of classic cinema and highlights her significant contributions to the film industry.

### A Career Defined by Iconic Roles

Robinson's career began in the early 1950s, and she quickly made a name for herself with her performance in "The War of the Worlds," directed by Byron Haskin and based on H.G. Wells' novel. In the film, she portrayed Sylvia Van Buren, a character who finds herself caught in the chaos of an alien invasion. Her portrayal of strength and resilience resonated with audiences, and the film itself has since become a cornerstone of the science fiction genre.

Reflecting on her career, Robinson once remarked that she got more mileage out of "The War of the Worlds" than "Vivien Leigh did on 'Gone With the Wind,'" a testament to the lasting impact of her role. The film not only showcased her talent but also solidified her place in cinematic history.

### A Life Beyond the Silver Screen

Before her rise to fame, Robinson worked as a stuntwoman, a role that undoubtedly contributed to her ability to perform in high-stakes scenes with authenticity. Her versatility allowed her to navigate various genres, and she appeared in numerous television shows and films throughout her career, including roles in series such as "The Twilight Zone" and "The Fugitive."

Despite the challenges of the entertainment industry, Robinson remained a beloved figure, known for her warmth and generosity. Colleagues and fans alike admired her dedication to her craft and her willingness to share her experiences with younger generations of actors.

### Legacy and Tributes

Robinson's passing has elicited an outpouring of tributes from fans and fellow actors. Many have taken to social media to express their condolences and share their favorite memories of her work. Her contributions to film, particularly in the science fiction genre, have left an indelible mark that will continue to inspire future filmmakers and actors.

As the film industry reflects on her legacy, it is clear that Ann Robinson will be remembered not only for her iconic role in "The War of the Worlds" but also for her pioneering spirit as a woman in a male-dominated industry. Her life and career serve as a reminder of the importance of representation and the power of storytelling.

### Conclusion

Ann Robinson's death is a significant loss to the world of cinema. Her remarkable journey from stuntwoman to celebrated actress is a testament to her talent and resilience. As fans remember her contributions, her legacy will undoubtedly endure, continuing to inspire those who love the art of film.
