## Screenxcope Acquires Rights to Bangladeshi Film 'Roid' for Australia and New Zealand

In a significant development for the international film market, Screenxcope has secured the rights to the Bangladeshi drama "Roid" for distribution in Australia and New Zealand. This acquisition comes in partnership with DeshiEvents, which is set to facilitate a theatrical release in early June across both territories.

### Background of 'Roid'

"Roid" premiered at the prestigious International Film Festival Rotterdam, where it was featured as part of the Tiger Competition selection during the festival's 55th edition. This recognition highlights the film's artistic merit and its potential to resonate with audiences beyond its home country. Following its debut in Rotterdam, "Roid" has also been selected for screening at the Seattle International Film Festival, further establishing its international appeal.

### The Significance of the Acquisition

The acquisition by Screenxcope reflects a growing interest in diverse storytelling from global cinema, particularly from South Asia. "Roid" is expected to bring unique cultural narratives to Australian and New Zealand audiences, enriching the cinematic landscape in these regions. The partnership with DeshiEvents, known for its focus on South Asian content, suggests a strategic move to engage communities that are eager for authentic representations of their cultures on screen.

### What to Expect from 'Roid'

While specific plot details have not been extensively publicized, "Roid" is anticipated to explore themes relevant to contemporary Bangladeshi society, possibly delving into social issues, personal struggles, and the complexities of identity. The film's reception at international festivals indicates a strong narrative and artistic direction, which could appeal to both critics and general audiences alike.

### Looking Ahead

The theatrical release in June marks an important milestone for "Roid" and its creators, providing an opportunity to reach a wider audience and foster discussions around its themes.
