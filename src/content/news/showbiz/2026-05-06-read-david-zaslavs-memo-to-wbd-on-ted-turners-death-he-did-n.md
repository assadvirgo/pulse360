## David Zaslav Reflects on Ted Turner’s Legacy Following His Passing

In a poignant memo to Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) staff, CEO David Zaslav expressed his reflections on the life and impact of media pioneer Ted Turner, who passed away earlier today. Turner, known for his revolutionary contributions to the media landscape, was a visionary who fundamentally changed how information is disseminated and consumed.

### A Visionary in Media

Turner, who founded CNN—the first 24-hour news channel—was a trailblazer who believed in the transformative power of media. In his memo, Zaslav highlighted Turner’s unwavering commitment to innovation and his belief in the importance of ideas. “He did not just disrupt media. He transformed it,” Zaslav stated, emphasizing Turner’s role in shaping modern journalism and entertainment.

Turner’s influence extended beyond traditional broadcasting; he was instrumental in creating platforms that not only informed audiences but also inspired and connected people globally. His pioneering spirit led to the establishment of various media outlets, including TBS and TNT, which further diversified the landscape of television.

### The Importance of Ideas

Zaslav’s tribute underscored Turner’s philosophy that media should serve as a conduit for powerful ideas and narratives. “He believed deeply in the power of ideas, in doing things differently,” Zaslav noted, reflecting on how Turner’s approach to media was characterized by a willingness to challenge the status quo. This mindset not only redefined news broadcasting but also set a precedent for future media entrepreneurs.

Turner’s legacy is marked by his commitment to social causes, including environmentalism and philanthropy. He was known for his efforts to promote awareness about global issues, using his platforms to advocate for change. This aspect of his career is a testament to his belief that media can be a force for good, capable of driving meaningful conversations and actions.

### A Lasting Impact

As the media industry continues to evolve, Turner’s contributions remain relevant. Zaslav’s memo serves as a reminder of the foundational principles that Turner instilled in the media landscape: the importance of creativity, innovation, and the responsibility that comes with informing the public. 

In reflecting on Turner’s passing, Zaslav called upon WBD staff to honor his legacy by continuing to innovate and push boundaries within the media space. “Let us remember Ted Turner not just for his achievements, but for the spirit of creativity and courage he embodied,” Zaslav urged.

### Conclusion

Ted Turner’s death marks the end of an era in media history, but his influence will undoubtedly persist. As the industry navigates the complexities of a digital age, the principles he championed will continue to inspire future generations of media professionals. David Zaslav’s heartfelt tribute encapsulates the profound impact Turner had on the world, reminding us of the power of media to inform, inspire, and connect.
