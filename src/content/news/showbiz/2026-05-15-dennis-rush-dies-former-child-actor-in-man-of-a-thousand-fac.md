## Dennis Rush, Former Child Actor, Passes Away at 74

Dennis Rush, a former child star renowned for his performances in classic television and film, has passed away at the age of 74. His death was confirmed by longtime friend and fellow actor Keith Thibodeaux, who shared the news with the media.

## Career Highlights

Rush is perhaps best remembered for his role as a young Creighton Chaney, the son of the legendary horror actor Lon Chaney, in the 1957 film "Man of a Thousand Faces." The film, which starred James Cagney as Chaney, showcased Rush's talent at a young age and marked a significant point in his early acting career.

In addition to his film work, Rush gained recognition for his role as Howie, the friend of Opie Taylor, played by Ron Howard, on the beloved television series "The Andy Griffith Show." The show, which aired from 1960 to 1968, remains a staple of American television history, and Rush's performance contributed to its charm and appeal.

## Legacy and Impact

Rush's contributions to the entertainment industry, particularly during his formative years, left a lasting impression on audiences. His ability to portray youthful innocence and camaraderie resonated with viewers and helped solidify the show's status as a classic.
