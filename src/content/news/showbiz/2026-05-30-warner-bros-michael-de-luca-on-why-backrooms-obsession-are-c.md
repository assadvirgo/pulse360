## Warner Bros’ Michael De Luca Discusses the Success of ‘Backrooms’ and ‘Obsession’

In a recent panel discussion at the Produced By conference, Michael De Luca, Co-Chair of Warner Bros. Motion Pictures, shared insights into the current cinematic landscape, particularly focusing on the success of films like ‘Backrooms’ and ‘Obsession.’ While the conversation did not touch on the significant merger between Paramount and Warner Bros. Discovery, De Luca emphasized the evolving relationship between filmmakers and their audiences.

### The Current Box Office Landscape

De Luca noted that the weekend box office has been thriving, attributing this success to a new wave of filmmakers who are adept at engaging with their audiences. He remarked on the unique ability of these creators to foster a dialogue with viewers, which he believes is crucial for resonating with contemporary audiences. This approach not only enhances the viewing experience but also creates a sense of community around the films.

### Engaging with Audiences

The films ‘Backrooms’ and ‘Obsession’ exemplify this trend, as they have successfully tapped into the cultural zeitgeist. De Luca pointed out that these filmmakers are not merely telling stories; they are actively listening to audience feedback and incorporating it into their work. This reciprocal relationship allows for a more dynamic and responsive filmmaking process, which can lead to greater audience satisfaction and box office success.

### The Role of Digital Platforms

De Luca also highlighted the impact of digital platforms, particularly YouTube, on the filmmaking process. He noted that many creators are leveraging these platforms to build their brands and connect with audiences before their films even hit theaters. This trend has changed the traditional marketing landscape, allowing filmmakers to cultivate a following and generate buzz around their projects organically.

### Looking Ahead

As the industry continues to evolve, De Luca expressed optimism about the future of cinema. He believes that the ability of filmmakers to adapt to changing audience preferences and technological advancements will be key to sustaining box office success. The dialogue between creators and viewers is not just a trend; it is becoming a fundamental aspect of the filmmaking process.

In conclusion, Michael De Luca’s insights reflect a broader shift in the film industry towards a more interactive and audience-centric approach. As films like ‘Backrooms’ and ‘Obsession’ demonstrate, engaging with viewers is essential for success in today’s competitive market. The ongoing evolution of this relationship will likely shape the future of cinema, making it an exciting time for both filmmakers and audiences alike.
