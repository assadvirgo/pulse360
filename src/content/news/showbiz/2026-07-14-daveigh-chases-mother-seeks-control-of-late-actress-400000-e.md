## Daveigh Chase’s Mother Seeks Control of Late Actress’ $400,000 Estate

In a recent development following the untimely death of actress Daveigh Chase, her mother has initiated legal proceedings to gain control of her daughter's estate, valued at approximately $400,000. Chase, best known for her role as the voice of Lilo in Disney's beloved animated film "Lilo & Stitch," passed away in June 2023 due to complications related to AIDS, a tragic end to a life marked by personal struggles.

### Background on Daveigh Chase

Daveigh Chase gained fame at a young age for her portrayal of Lilo, a spirited young girl who befriends a mischievous alien in the 2002 animated classic. The film became a cultural touchstone, celebrated for its heartwarming narrative and vibrant characters. However, Chase's life outside of her professional achievements was fraught with challenges, including homelessness and battles with addiction, which ultimately contributed to her premature death at the age of 32.

### Legal Proceedings

Chase's mother, who has not been publicly named, filed a petition in court seeking to be appointed as the administrator of her daughter's estate. This legal move comes in the wake of Chase's passing, as her mother aims to manage the financial affairs and assets left behind by the actress. The estate, valued at $400,000, reportedly includes various assets accumulated during Chase's career, though specific details have yet to be disclosed.

### Implications of the Estate

The proceedings surrounding Chase's estate highlight the complexities often faced by families dealing with the aftermath of a loved one’s death, particularly in the entertainment industry. The management of an estate can involve various legal and financial considerations, especially when the deceased has faced significant personal challenges.
