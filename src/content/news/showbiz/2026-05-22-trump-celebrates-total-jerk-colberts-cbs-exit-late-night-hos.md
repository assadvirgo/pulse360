## Trump Responds to Colbert's Departure from CBS

In a notable turn of events within the realm of late-night television, President Donald Trump has publicly commented on Stephen Colbert's final episode of "The Late Show" on CBS. Colbert, a prominent figure in the late-night comedy scene, has been known for his sharp and often critical humor directed at the former president.

### Colbert's Departure

Stephen Colbert signed off from "The Late Show" for the last time on Thursday, marking the end of a significant chapter in late-night television. Colbert has been a vocal critic of Trump throughout his tenure, using his platform to address political issues and satirize the actions of the former president. His comedic style, characterized by a blend of wit and pointed commentary, has garnered both acclaim and controversy.

### Trump's Reaction

In the early hours following Colbert's departure, Trump took to social media to express his sentiments. In a post made at 1:52 a.m. ET, he referred to Colbert as a "total jerk," a reflection of their contentious relationship over the years. This remark is consistent with Trump's history of responding to criticism from comedians and public figures, particularly those who have targeted him in their routines.

### The Context of Their Relationship

The relationship between Trump and Colbert has been marked by a series of exchanges that highlight the often adversarial nature of politics and comedy. Colbert has not shied away from confronting Trump directly, famously telling him to "go f— yourself" during a segment that captured significant media attention. This moment, among others, has contributed to a narrative of late-night hosts acting as a counterbalance to political figures, particularly in the context of the Trump administration.

### The Impact of Late-Night Comedy

Colbert's departure raises questions about the future of late-night television and the role of comedians in political discourse. Late-night shows have increasingly become platforms for political commentary, with hosts using humor to engage audiences on serious issues. As Colbert steps away, it remains to be seen how the landscape will evolve and whether new voices will emerge to fill the void.

### Conclusion

Trump's reaction to Colbert's exit underscores the ongoing interplay between politics and entertainment. As both figures have navigated their respective arenas, their interactions reflect broader societal dynamics, where humor and criticism often intersect. The legacy of Colbert's tenure on "The Late Show" will likely be remembered for its incisive commentary and its role in shaping public discourse during a tumultuous political era.
