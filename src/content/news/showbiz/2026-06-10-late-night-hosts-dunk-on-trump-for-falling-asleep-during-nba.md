## Late-Night Hosts Critique Trump’s NBA Finals Appearance

During the third game of the NBA Finals, President Donald Trump found himself in the spotlight not just for his presence at Madison Square Garden, but also for receiving a mixed reception from the crowd. The game featured the New York Knicks facing off against the San Antonio Spurs, and Trump's attendance was met with a chorus of boos from fans.

### A Mixed Reception at Madison Square Garden

Trump's appearance at the high-profile event was notable, as it marked a significant moment in the ongoing relationship between sports and politics. However, the former president's reception was less than favorable, with many attendees expressing their discontent vocally. The atmosphere in the arena shifted as Trump's presence became a focal point, overshadowing the on-court action.

### Late-Night Hosts Weigh In

In the wake of the game, late-night television hosts seized the opportunity to comment on Trump's attendance and the reactions he garnered. Desi Lydic, a correspondent on "The Daily Show," was among those who took to the airwaves to humorously critique the situation. Lydic quipped about the boos, suggesting that the fans' reaction might have been a jinx for the Knicks, who were already facing a challenging series against the Spurs.

Jimmy Kimmel, another prominent late-night host, also joined the conversation, poking fun at Trump for appearing to fall asleep during the game. This moment of apparent fatigue became fodder for comedic commentary, as Kimmel and others highlighted the irony of a former president dozing off at a major sporting event.
