## Filmhub Acquires Indie Dramedy ‘Burt’ Ahead of World Parkinson’s Day

In a significant move for independent cinema, global sales and distribution company Filmhub has secured the rights to the micro-indie dramedy “Burt.” This acquisition comes just ahead of World Parkinson’s Day, which is observed on April 11 each year, highlighting the film's timely relevance in raising awareness about Parkinson’s Disease.

### Overview of the Film

“Burt” tells the poignant story of an estranged father-son relationship, focusing on the complexities that arise as they navigate the challenges posed by Parkinson’s Disease. The film features Oliver Cooper in the role of the young man who confronts his estranged father, Burt, portrayed by Burt Berger. As an aging street musician grappling with the effects of the disease, Burt's journey reflects both the struggles and the resilience often experienced by those affected by Parkinson's.

The narrative is expected to resonate with audiences, not only for its emotional depth but also for its exploration of familial bonds in the face of adversity. The film's unique blend of drama and comedy aims to provide a nuanced perspective on the realities of living with a chronic illness, making it a compelling addition to the indie film landscape.

### Significance of the Acquisition

Filmhub's acquisition of “Burt” underscores the company's commitment to promoting diverse storytelling and supporting independent filmmakers. By choosing to distribute a film centered on a health-related issue, Filmhub is also contributing to the broader conversation surrounding Parkinson’s Disease, an affliction that affects millions worldwide.

World Parkinson’s Day serves as an annual reminder of the challenges faced by those living with the disease, as well as the importance of research and support for patients and their families. The timing of this acquisition aligns with efforts to raise awareness and foster discussions about the condition, making “Burt” an apt choice for distribution during this period.
