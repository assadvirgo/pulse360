## Chip Taylor, Renowned Songwriter of 'Wild Thing', Passes Away at 86

Chip Taylor, the celebrated singer and songwriter best known for his iconic hits "Wild Thing" and "Angel of the Morning," has passed away at the age of 86. His children, Kris and Kelly, confirmed the news in a heartfelt statement following their father's death, which reportedly occurred on Monday.

### A Legacy of Musical Influence

Born James Wesley Voight, Chip Taylor's career spanned several decades, during which he made significant contributions to the music industry. His most famous composition, "Wild Thing," originally performed by The Troggs in 1966, became an anthem of the era and has since been covered by numerous artists, including Jimi Hendrix and Cheap Trick. The song's infectious energy and catchy refrain solidified its place in rock history, showcasing Taylor's knack for creating memorable melodies.

In addition to "Wild Thing," Taylor wrote "Angel of the Morning," a song that has been recorded by various artists, including Merrilee Rush and Juice Newton. This track also gained immense popularity and further exemplified Taylor's versatility as a songwriter. His work transcended genres, influencing both pop and rock music.

### Family Ties and Personal Life

Chip Taylor was not only a talented musician but also part of a prominent family in the entertainment industry. He was the brother of acclaimed actor Jon Voight and the uncle of Oscar-winning actress Angelina Jolie. Taylor's familial connections often brought additional attention to his career, but it was his musical talent that truly defined his legacy.

### Remembering Chip Taylor

The news of Taylor's passing has elicited tributes from fans and fellow musicians alike, who have expressed their admiration for his contributions to music. Many have taken to social media to share memories and celebrate the impact his songs had on their lives. Taylor's ability to craft songs that resonated with audiences will be remembered for generations to come.

In addition to his songwriting prowess, Taylor was known for his engaging performances and charismatic presence on stage. His passion for music was evident in every note he played, and he continued to perform and write music well into his later years.
