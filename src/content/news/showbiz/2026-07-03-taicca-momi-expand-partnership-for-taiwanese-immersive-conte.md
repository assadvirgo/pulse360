## TAICCA and MoMI Strengthen Partnership to Showcase Taiwanese Immersive Content in New York

The Taiwan Creative Content Agency (TAICCA) has announced an expanded partnership with the Museum of the Moving Image (MoMI) in New York, aimed at promoting Taiwanese immersive content over the next three years. This collaboration builds on their previous successful engagement in 2025, which attracted over 3,000 screenings, achieving an impressive daily capacity of nearly 90%.

### A Commitment to Cultural Exchange

The renewed partnership reflects a commitment to cultural exchange and the promotion of Taiwanese creativity on an international stage. By showcasing immersive works, TAICCA and MoMI are not only enhancing the visibility of Taiwanese artists but also enriching the cultural landscape of New York. The initiative aims to foster a deeper understanding and appreciation of Taiwanese narratives and artistic expressions among global audiences.

### Launching with Promising Works

The partnership kicks off with two immersive works currently on display at MoMI, which will run through September 6. These works are expected to captivate audiences with their innovative storytelling techniques and unique perspectives, characteristic of Taiwanese creative talent. The selection of these pieces underscores the commitment of both organizations to highlight the diversity and richness of Taiwanese culture.

### Previous Success and Future Prospects

The initial collaboration in 2025 set a strong precedent for the partnership, demonstrating the potential for Taiwanese immersive content to resonate with audiences outside of Taiwan. The overwhelming response from viewers during that period indicates a growing interest in immersive experiences that challenge traditional storytelling formats. The upcoming three-year partnership is poised to build on this momentum, offering a platform for more Taiwanese creators to showcase their work.

### Implications for the Creative Industry

This partnership not only benefits the artists involved but also has broader implications for the creative industry in Taiwan. By establishing a presence in a major cultural hub like New York, TAICCA and MoMI are paving the way for future collaborations and opportunities for Taiwanese creators. This initiative may inspire other institutions to engage with international partners, further promoting Taiwanese culture and creativity on a global scale.

### Conclusion

The expanded partnership between TAICCA and MoMI marks a significant step in promoting Taiwanese immersive content in New York. As the collaboration unfolds over the next three years, it is expected to foster greater cultural exchange and appreciation for Taiwanese artistry. With the launch of two immersive works, both organizations are set to engage audiences and encourage a deeper exploration of the narratives that define Taiwanese culture.
