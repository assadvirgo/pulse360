## ‘Backrooms’ Review: A New Dimension in Experimental Horror

In a notable entry into the realm of independent horror cinema, Kane Parsons has made his feature directing debut with "Backrooms," a film that has garnered attention for its unique approach to storytelling and atmosphere. The film, scripted by Will Soodik, is a striking blend of experimental horror that draws comparisons to the early works of acclaimed director David Lynch.

### A Master of Mood

Parsons has demonstrated a remarkable ability to create an unsettling ambiance throughout "Backrooms." His direction showcases a keen understanding of sound design, reminiscent of Lynch’s own fascination with industrial and cosmic audio landscapes. The film immerses viewers in a world where sound plays a crucial role in building tension and evoking fear. The auditory experience is not merely a backdrop but a character in itself, enhancing the film's overall impact.

### Exploring the Unknown

The narrative of "Backrooms" delves into themes of confinement and the unknown, skillfully tapping into the primal fears associated with being trapped. Parsons effectively utilizes the concept of an infinite space that feels both claustrophobic and disorienting, akin to a serial killer's lair. This setting serves as a canvas for exploring the psychological terror that arises from being enclosed in a seemingly endless maze of stark, sterile environments.

### A Cinematic Experience

The film's visual style complements its sound design, with cinematography that captures the eerie essence of the backrooms. The use of lighting and framing contributes to the film's unsettling atmosphere, drawing viewers deeper into the narrative. As the protagonist navigates this disquieting space, the film invites audiences to confront their own fears and anxieties about isolation and the unknown.

### Reception and Impact

"Backrooms" has sparked discussions among critics and audiences alike, particularly for its innovative approach to horror. By stepping away from conventional tropes and embracing a more experimental style, Parsons has positioned himself as a filmmaker to watch. The film's ability to evoke genuine terror without relying on traditional jump scares or gore marks a refreshing shift in the genre.

### Conclusion

In summary, Kane Parsons' "Backrooms" stands as a testament to the potential of experimental horror. With its haunting sound design, captivating visuals, and a narrative that explores the depths of fear and confinement, the film is a noteworthy addition to contemporary cinema. As Parsons continues to develop his voice as a director, "Backrooms" may well be remembered as the starting point of a compelling new journey in the world of horror filmmaking.
