## Location Managers Guild International Elects Danny Finn as President

In a significant leadership transition, the Location Managers Guild International (LMGI) has announced the election of Danny Finn as its new president. This decision, made during a recent vote, reflects a unanimous consensus among the guild's members, marking a new chapter in the organization’s governance.

### Transition in Leadership

Danny Finn takes over the presidency from John Rakich, who has held the position previously. Finn's prior role as LMGI's 2nd Vice President involved overseeing communications and marketing strategies, equipping him with valuable experience that he will bring to his new responsibilities. His election signals a continuity of purpose within the guild, as he aims to build on the foundation laid by his predecessor.

### New Executive Team

Alongside Finn, the LMGI has elected a new team of executive officers and board members. While specific details regarding the other elected officials have yet to be disclosed, the changes in leadership are expected to invigorate the guild's initiatives and enhance its outreach within the industry. The LMGI plays a crucial role in representing location managers and scouts, advocating for their interests, and providing resources to support their work in film and television production.

### The Role of LMGI

The Location Managers Guild International serves as a vital organization for professionals involved in the location management sector of the entertainment industry. Its members are responsible for identifying and securing locations for film and television productions, often navigating complex logistical challenges to ensure that creative visions are realized on screen.

The guild not only advocates for the rights and recognition of location managers but also fosters a community that shares best practices and resources. With Finn at the helm, there is an expectation that the guild will continue to enhance its support for members and strengthen its position within the broader entertainment landscape.

### Looking Ahead

As the new president, Danny Finn faces the challenge of addressing ongoing changes in the film and television industry, particularly in light of evolving production practices and the impact of technology. His leadership will be pivotal in guiding the guild through these transitions while ensuring that the voices of location managers are heard and valued.

The unanimous support for Finn's presidency suggests a strong desire for collaborative leadership and a unified direction among the guild's members. As the LMGI embarks on this new phase, industry stakeholders will be watching closely to see how the organization adapts to the challenges ahead and continues to advocate for excellence in location management.

In conclusion, the election of Danny Finn as president of the Location Managers Guild International marks a significant moment for the organization. With a new executive team in place, the guild is poised to continue its essential work in supporting location managers and enhancing the visibility of their contributions to the film and television industry.
