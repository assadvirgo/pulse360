## Cannes Jury President Park Chan-wook Discusses Palme d'Or Dilemma

At the closing press conference of the 79th Cannes Film Festival, jury president Park Chan-wook shared insights into the challenges faced by the jury in selecting the prestigious Palme d'Or award. Known for his acclaimed films, Park's candid remarks provided a glimpse into the deliberative process that underpins one of the film industry's most coveted accolades.

### A Humorous Take on a Serious Decision

During the press conference, Park, who has been a prominent figure at Cannes but has never won the Palme d'Or himself, humorously admitted to the difficulties he encountered while evaluating this year's contenders. “To be completely honest, I didn’t want to award the Palme d’Or to any of the films,” he quipped, eliciting laughter from the audience. His self-deprecating humor not only lightened the atmosphere but also highlighted the weighty responsibility that comes with being a jury president at such a prestigious festival.

### The Role of the Jury in Film Selection

The Cannes Film Festival is renowned for its rigorous selection process, where films are evaluated based on artistic merit, storytelling, and innovation. The jury, composed of industry veterans and creative talents, engages in extensive discussions to determine the winners across various categories, including the highly sought-after Palme d'Or. Park's comments underscore the subjective nature of film appreciation and the complexities involved in making such decisions.

### Park Chan-wook’s Continued Influence

Despite not having won the Palme d'Or, Park Chan-wook is celebrated for his distinctive storytelling style and has garnered numerous accolades throughout his career. His latest film, which he promoted during the press conference, continues to showcase his unique vision and artistic prowess. The film's presence at Cannes further solidifies Park's status as a significant contributor to contemporary cinema.

### Reflections on the Festival

The 79th Cannes Film Festival has been marked by a diverse array of films, drawing attention to various cultural narratives and cinematic styles. Park's reflections on the selection process resonate with many filmmakers who understand the challenges of balancing personal preferences with the expectations of the audience and the industry.

As the festival concluded, Park's remarks served as a reminder of the ongoing dialogue within the film community regarding artistic merit and recognition. The Cannes Film Festival remains a vital platform for filmmakers to showcase their work and engage with global audiences, and the deliberations of its jury continue to shape the landscape of international cinema.

### Conclusion

Park Chan-wook's humorous yet insightful commentary at the Cannes Film Festival reflects the complexities of film evaluation and the subjective nature of art. As the festival wraps up, the discussions surrounding the Palme d'Or and the films presented will likely continue to inspire filmmakers and audiences alike, reinforcing Cannes' status as a premier event in the global film calendar.
