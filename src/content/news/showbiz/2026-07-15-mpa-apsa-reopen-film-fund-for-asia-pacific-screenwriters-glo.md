## MPA and APSA Reopen Film Fund for Asia Pacific Screenwriters

The Motion Picture Association (MPA) and the Asia Pacific Screen Awards (APSA) have announced the reopening of the MPA APSA Academy Film Fund for 2026, aimed at supporting emerging filmmakers in the Asia Pacific region. This initiative will provide five grants, each worth $10,000, specifically designated for script development.

### Announcement at MPA American Film Night

The announcement was made during the MPA American Film Night held in Bangkok, Thailand. This event, which celebrates cinematic achievements and fosters collaboration within the film industry, was complemented by the MPA Thailand Success Stories segment. The gathering attracted a diverse group of filmmakers, industry professionals, and enthusiasts, highlighting the importance of nurturing talent within the region.

### Supporting Emerging Talent

The MPA APSA Academy Film Fund is designed to empower screenwriters and filmmakers by providing them with the necessary financial resources to develop their scripts. This initiative underscores the commitment of both organizations to promote storytelling and creativity in the Asia Pacific film industry. The grants are intended to assist in the early stages of film production, which can often be a challenging phase for many aspiring filmmakers.

### Application Process

Eligible applicants are encouraged to submit their scripts for consideration. The selection process will focus on originality, creativity, and the potential impact of the stories being told. The fund aims to support diverse voices from various backgrounds, ensuring that a wide array of narratives is represented in the film landscape.

### A Step Forward for Regional Cinema

The reopening of the MPA APSA Academy Film Fund is a significant step forward for regional cinema, particularly in light of the ongoing challenges faced by the film industry due to the global pandemic. By investing in new talent, the MPA and APSA are not only fostering creativity but also contributing to the cultural richness of the Asia Pacific region.

### Conclusion

As the film industry continues to evolve, initiatives like the MPA APSA Academy Film Fund play a crucial role in shaping the future of cinema in the Asia Pacific. By providing financial support and resources to emerging filmmakers, the MPA and APSA are helping to ensure that the voices of the next generation are heard and celebrated on the global stage. Filmmakers interested in applying for the grants should stay tuned for further details regarding the application process and deadlines, as this opportunity could pave the way for their cinematic aspirations.
