## Estonian Director Oskar Lehemaa to Release English-Language Body Horror Film "Birth"

Estonian filmmaker Oskar Lehemaa is poised to make his mark on the international cinema landscape with his upcoming film "Birth," a body horror narrative that delves into the complexities of pregnancy and conception. The project was recently showcased at the Frontières Co-Production Market, where it garnered interest from various film funds, including the Tallifornia Film Fund.

## A Unique Concept in Body Horror

"Birth" tells the story of a couple who, after enduring years of unsuccessful attempts to conceive, decides to seek help at a secluded fertility retreat. The narrative unfolds in a remote woodland setting, where the couple hopes to explore alternative solutions before resorting to in vitro fertilization (IVF). However, what begins as a hopeful journey quickly spirals into a "grotesque and terrifying" exploration of the human experience surrounding pregnancy.

Lehemaa's film aims to challenge conventional portrayals of motherhood and fertility, offering a darker and more unsettling perspective that is characteristic of the body horror genre. The director's vision is to evoke not only fear but also a deep emotional response, prompting audiences to reflect on the societal pressures and personal struggles associated with parenthood.

## The Reception at Frontières Co-Production Market

The Frontières Co-Production Market, known for connecting emerging filmmakers with potential investors and collaborators, provided a platform for Lehemaa to present his ambitious project. The film's unique premise and the director's previous work in the Estonian film industry have attracted the attention of various stakeholders, particularly from the Tallifornia Film Fund, which is known for supporting innovative and boundary-pushing projects.

Industry insiders have expressed enthusiasm about the film's potential, noting that its blend of horror and deeply personal themes could resonate with audiences seeking fresh narratives in contemporary cinema. The reception at the market has been described as positive, with many attendees intrigued by the film's exploration of the psychological and physical aspects of fertility.

## A New Chapter for Estonian Cinema

"Birth" marks a significant step for Lehemaa, who is transitioning from producing films in his native Estonian language to creating content for an English-speaking audience. This shift not only broadens his creative horizons but also positions Estonian cinema on the global stage. By tackling universal themes through a unique lens, Lehemaa aims to contribute to the growing recognition of Estonian filmmakers in the international film community.

As the film progresses through its development stages, anticipation builds around its eventual release. "Birth" promises to be a thought-provoking addition to the body horror genre, inviting viewers to confront their perceptions of fertility, motherhood, and the often-unspoken fears that accompany them.

## Conclusion

With "Birth," Oskar Lehemaa is set to deliver a compelling and unsettling narrative that challenges traditional views on pregnancy and conception. As the film garners interest from international film funds and audiences alike, it stands as a testament to the evolving landscape of Estonian cinema and the power of storytelling to explore the depths of human experience.
