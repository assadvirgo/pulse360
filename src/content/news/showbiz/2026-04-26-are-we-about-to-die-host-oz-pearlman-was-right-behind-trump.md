## Incident at the White House Correspondents' Dinner

The annual White House Correspondents' Dinner, a prominent event in the U.S. political calendar, was abruptly interrupted by a security incident involving a shooter. The event, held at the Washington Hilton, was attended by numerous high-profile figures, including former President Donald Trump and mentalist Oz Pearlman, who was performing at the time of the incident.

## A Moment of Tension

As the evening unfolded, Pearlman was engaged in a mentalism act, revealing personal details to Trump, including the name of Karoline Leavitt's unborn daughter. This moment, which was intended to showcase Pearlman's talents, quickly turned into a scene of chaos when armed federal agents stormed the ballroom, responding to reports of a shooter.

Witnesses described a sudden shift in atmosphere as the agents entered, creating a palpable sense of tension and uncertainty. Attendees were left to grapple with the seriousness of the situation, with many reportedly asking, “Are we about to die?” as they processed the unfolding events.
