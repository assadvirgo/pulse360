## Joseph J. Collins: A Legacy in Cable Television

Joseph J. Collins, a prominent figure in the cable television industry and former president of HBO, has passed away at the age of 81. Collins died on April 2 at his residence in Weekapaug, Rhode Island, as confirmed by a family representative. The cause of his death has not been disclosed.

Collins was instrumental in shaping the cable television landscape during a pivotal time in the late 20th century when pay-TV began to solidify its place as a dominant force in media. His career spanned several decades, during which he held senior executive positions at major companies, including Time Warner Cable and HBO. 

His leadership at HBO was particularly notable, as he played a crucial role in the network's expansion and success, helping to establish it as a premier destination for original programming. Under his guidance, HBO launched iconic series that would redefine television storytelling and set new standards for quality in the industry.
