## Ranbir Kapoor and Yash’s ‘Ramayana’ Tops IMDb’s Most Anticipated Indian Movies List for Second Half of 2026

As the film industry gears up for the latter half of 2026, Nitesh Tiwari's highly anticipated adaptation of the epic "Ramayana Part 1" has emerged as the leading title on IMDb's Most Anticipated Indian Movies list. This ranking is based on page views recorded by IMDb's extensive user base, which exceeds 250 million monthly visitors.

### A New Take on a Classic Epic

"Ramayana Part 1" promises to offer a fresh perspective on one of India's most revered epics. Tiwari, known for his previous successful projects, aims to bring a modern sensibility to the age-old narrative while retaining its cultural significance. The film is expected to feature prominent actors, including Ranbir Kapoor and Yash, both of whom have established themselves as major stars in the Indian film industry.

### The Significance of the Ranking

The IMDb list serves as an insightful barometer of audience interest and anticipation. The top position of "Ramayana Part 1" reflects not only the star power of its cast but also the enduring appeal of the source material. The film's ranking is indicative of a growing trend in Indian cinema, where adaptations of classic tales are increasingly popular among filmmakers and audiences alike.

### Audience Engagement and Expectations

The anticipation surrounding "Ramayana Part 1" has generated significant buzz on social media platforms, with fans expressing their excitement and expectations for the film. The film's marketing strategy, which includes teasers and promotional content, has effectively engaged audiences, leading to a surge in page views on IMDb. This engagement is crucial, as it reflects the film's potential box office performance and cultural impact upon release.

The success of "Ramayana Part 1" on the IMDb list also highlights the evolving landscape of Indian cinema. As the release date approaches, industry analysts will be closely monitoring the film's performance and audience reception. In conclusion, Nitesh Tiwari's "Ramayana Part 1" stands at the forefront of Indian cinema's upcoming releases, capturing the attention of millions. With its compelling cast and rich source material, it is poised to make a significant impact in the latter half of 2026, both in India and beyond.
