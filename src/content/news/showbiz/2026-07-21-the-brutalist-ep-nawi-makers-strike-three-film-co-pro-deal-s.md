## New Film Partnership Announced in Kenya

In an exciting development for the Kenyan film industry, a new collaboration has been established between Thomas Pierce's Pierce Capital Entertainment and Kevin and Toby Schmutzler's FilmCrew. This partnership aims to produce a trilogy of films, with the first project set to be a documentary titled "Champions of Nowhere," which will focus on soccer in Kenya. 

### Filmmaker at the Helm

The documentary will be directed by Ugandan filmmaker and journalist Moses Bwayo, known for his insightful storytelling and commitment to social issues. Bwayo's involvement is expected to bring a unique perspective to the film, highlighting the challenges and triumphs of soccer in Kenya, a sport that holds significant cultural importance in the region.

### The Significance of "Champions of Nowhere"

"Champions of Nowhere" aims to explore the lives of local soccer players and the broader implications of the sport within Kenyan society. The documentary will delve into themes such as aspiration, community, and the socio-economic factors that influence the world of sports in the country. By focusing on the grassroots level of soccer, the film seeks to shed light on the untold stories of those who strive for success against the odds.

### A Growing Film Industry

This co-production deal reflects the growing interest in African cinema and the potential for storytelling that resonates with both local and global audiences. The partnership between Pierce Capital Entertainment and FilmCrew underscores a commitment to fostering talent and producing high-quality content that reflects the rich tapestry of African life.

### Future Projects

While "Champions of Nowhere" is the first film to emerge from this collaboration, the announcement of a three-film deal suggests that there are more stories to be told. The filmmakers have indicated that subsequent projects will continue to explore diverse narratives that capture the essence of life in Kenya and beyond.

### Conclusion

As the Kenyan film industry continues to evolve, partnerships like this one are crucial for nurturing creativity and expanding the reach of African stories. With Moses Bwayo at the helm of "Champions of Nowhere," audiences can anticipate a compelling documentary that not only entertains but also educates and inspires. As the project develops, it will be interesting to see how it contributes to the broader conversation about sports, culture, and identity in Kenya.
