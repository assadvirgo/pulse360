## Disney Elevates April Carretta to Executive Vice President of Communications

The Walt Disney Company has announced the promotion of April Carretta to the position of Executive Vice President (EVP) of Communications. This strategic move is part of a broader organizational shift aimed at enhancing the company's public relations efforts and supporting its leadership team.

### New Responsibilities

In her new role, Carretta will report directly to Paul Roeder, Disney's Chief Communications Officer. One of her key responsibilities will be to serve as the day-to-day communications lead for Dana Walden, who recently assumed the newly created position of President and Chief Creative Officer. This role was established under the leadership of CEO Josh D'Amaro, reflecting Disney's commitment to fostering creativity and innovation within its ranks.

Carretta's promotion comes at a time when the entertainment industry is navigating significant changes, including shifts in consumer behavior and the ongoing evolution of digital media. Her extensive experience in communications and public relations will be vital as Disney continues to adapt to these challenges.

### Career Background

April Carretta has been with Disney for several years, where she has built a reputation for her strategic thinking and effective communication skills. Prior to her promotion, she served as the head of communications for one of Disney's key divisions, where she successfully managed high-profile campaigns and initiatives. Her deep understanding of the company's culture and values positions her well to lead communications efforts across various platforms.

### Implications for Disney

The elevation of Carretta to this prominent role underscores Disney's focus on strengthening its communications strategy during a transformative period. With Walden's new responsibilities as a creative leader, Carretta's role becomes even more critical in ensuring that the company's messaging aligns with its creative vision and business objectives.

As the media landscape continues to evolve, effective communication will be essential for Disney to maintain its position as a leader in the entertainment industry. Carretta's experience and leadership will likely play a pivotal role in shaping the narrative around Disney's projects and initiatives in the coming years.

### Conclusion

April Carretta's promotion to EVP of Communications marks a significant step for both her career and Disney's strategic direction. As the company prepares to navigate the complexities of the modern entertainment landscape, Carretta's leadership will be instrumental in fostering strong relationships with stakeholders and audiences alike. With a renewed focus on creativity and innovation, Disney is poised to continue its legacy as a beloved global brand.
