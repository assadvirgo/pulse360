## Historical Drama 'The King’s Warden' Achieves Milestone at South Korean Box Office

The South Korean film industry has reached a significant milestone with the historical drama "The King’s Warden" surpassing 15 million admissions. This achievement, recorded during the weekend of March 27–29, further solidifies the film's status as the highest-grossing film of all time in South Korea.

### Record-Breaking Admissions

"The King’s Warden," which has captivated audiences with its compelling narrative and stunning cinematography, has now drawn over 15 million viewers since its release. This remarkable figure not only highlights the film's popularity but also reflects the growing appetite for domestic productions in South Korea. The film has consistently performed well at the box office, and its latest weekend earnings of $3.3 million have contributed to a staggering cumulative gross of $99.4 million.

### Cultural Impact and Production

Directed by a prominent figure in the South Korean film industry, "The King’s Warden" explores themes of loyalty, power, and historical legacy, resonating deeply with both local and international audiences. The film's success is attributed to its engaging storyline, strong performances, and high production values, which have set a new standard for historical dramas in the region.

The film's narrative is rooted in South Korea's rich history, allowing viewers to connect with their cultural heritage while being entertained. This blend of education and entertainment has proven to be a winning formula, attracting a diverse audience demographic.

### Industry Implications

The success of "The King’s Warden" is indicative of a broader trend within the South Korean film industry, which has seen a resurgence in domestic productions that appeal to local audiences.
