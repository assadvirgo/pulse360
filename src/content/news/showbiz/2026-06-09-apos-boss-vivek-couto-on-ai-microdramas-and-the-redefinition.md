## APOS 2026: Vivek Couto Discusses the Redefinition of Asia's Media Landscape

Vivek Couto, the CEO and executive director of Media Partners Asia, delivered a thought-provoking opening address at the Asia Pacific Online Summit (APOS) 2026, held from June 16 to 18 in Bali. The summit, a significant event in the media and entertainment industry, attracted key stakeholders from across the region, eager to engage in discussions about the future of media.

In his address, Couto emphasized the need for a comprehensive transformation rather than a mere reset in the media industry. He articulated that the landscape is not just changing; it is being fundamentally redefined. "It’s not actually being reset," Couto stated, highlighting the profound shifts that are taking place within the sector.

## The Impact of Artificial Intelligence

A central theme of Couto's address was the influence of artificial intelligence (AI) on the media industry. He pointed out that AI is not merely a tool for enhancing existing processes but is reshaping the very fabric of content creation and distribution. As media companies increasingly integrate AI technologies, they must also adapt their strategies to leverage these advancements effectively.

Couto stressed that the integration of AI presents both opportunities and challenges. Companies must navigate the complexities of AI while ensuring they remain relevant in a rapidly evolving market. "You can’t dabble in Asia," he cautioned, urging media firms to fully commit to understanding and utilizing AI to stay competitive.

## The Rise of Microdramas

Another significant trend discussed by Couto was the emergence of microdramas—short, engaging content pieces that cater to the evolving preferences of viewers. He noted that as audiences become more fragmented, there is a growing demand for bite-sized content that can be consumed quickly and easily. This shift reflects broader changes in consumer behavior, particularly among younger demographics who favor on-demand viewing experiences.

Couto's insights suggest that media companies must innovate their content strategies to accommodate these preferences. By embracing microdramas and other emerging formats, they can capture the attention of audiences and foster deeper engagement.

## Navigating a Complex Landscape

As the media industry grapples with these transformations, Couto emphasized the importance of understanding the unique dynamics of the Asian market. The region is characterized by diverse cultures, varying consumption habits, and distinct regulatory environments. Therefore, a one-size-fits-all approach is insufficient.

Couto's remarks underscore the necessity for media companies to develop tailored strategies that resonate with local audiences while also considering regional trends. This nuanced approach will be crucial for success in a landscape that is continuously evolving.

## Conclusion

Vivek Couto's address at APOS 2026 serves as a clarion call for media companies operating in Asia. As the industry undergoes significant redefinition driven by AI and changing viewer preferences, stakeholders must adapt and innovate to thrive. The insights shared during the summit highlight the importance of a committed and informed approach to navigating the complexities of the Asian media landscape.
