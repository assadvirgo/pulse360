## MPX Acquires World Sales Rights to ‘Swipe’ Ahead of Cannes Launch

In a significant move ahead of the prestigious Cannes Marché du Film, Motion Picture Exchange (MPX) has secured the world sales rights to the indie horror film "Swipe," directed by Sean Whalen. This acquisition marks an important step for MPX as it continues to expand its portfolio of unique cinematic offerings.

### Overview of "Swipe"

"Swipe" is a U.S.-based horror film that delves into the unsettling dynamics of a family comprised of sadistic mute women. The film is notable for its female-driven narrative, a trend that has been gaining traction in the horror genre. The story promises to deliver a chilling experience, utilizing suspense and psychological elements to engage audiences.

Sean Whalen, known for his acting career and previous directorial work, helms this project as his second feature film. Whalen's experience in the industry, combined with a compelling storyline, positions "Swipe" as a noteworthy contender in the independent film circuit.

### Cannes Marché du Film Presentation

MPX plans to showcase "Swipe" to potential buyers at the upcoming Cannes Marché du Film, an event renowned for facilitating international film sales and networking opportunities. The festival, which runs concurrently with the Cannes Film Festival, attracts a global audience of filmmakers, distributors, and industry professionals, making it an ideal platform for MPX to promote this new title.

The decision to present "Swipe" at Cannes underscores MPX's commitment to bringing innovative and diverse stories to the forefront of the film industry. As the horror genre continues to evolve, films like "Swipe" that offer fresh perspectives and unique narratives are increasingly in demand.

### The Growing Appeal of Female-Driven Horror

The acquisition of "Swipe" reflects a broader trend within the film industry towards female-driven narratives, particularly in genres traditionally dominated by male perspectives. As audiences seek more diverse storytelling, films that center on women's experiences and perspectives are not only gaining popularity but are also receiving critical acclaim.

Horror, in particular, has seen a resurgence of interest in female-led stories, with films like "The Babadook," "Hereditary," and "A Quiet Place" paving the way for more innovative and complex portrayals of women in the genre. "Swipe" aims to contribute to this evolving landscape, offering a unique take on horror that is both unsettling and thought-provoking.

### Conclusion

As MPX prepares to introduce "Swipe" to the international market at Cannes, the film is poised to capture the attention of buyers and audiences alike. With its intriguing premise and the creative vision of Sean Whalen, "Swipe" represents a promising addition to the independent horror genre. The film's presentation at such a prominent event highlights the ongoing evolution of storytelling in cinema, particularly in the realm of horror, where fresh narratives are continually reshaping audience expectations. 

With the Cannes Marché du Film just around the corner, all eyes will be on "Swipe" as it embarks on its journey in the competitive landscape of global cinema.
