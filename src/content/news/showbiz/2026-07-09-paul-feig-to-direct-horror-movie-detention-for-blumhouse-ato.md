## Paul Feig to Direct Horror Movie ‘Detention’ for Blumhouse Atomic Monster

In a notable development within the film industry, acclaimed director Paul Feig is set to helm a new horror project titled "Detention" for Blumhouse Productions' Atomic Monster division. Known for his work in comedy and television, Feig's foray into the horror genre has generated considerable interest among both fans and industry insiders.

### Collaboration with Notable Writers and Producers

The screenplay for "Detention" has been penned by Daniel Gold, who recently gained attention for his work on the upcoming film "Tough Guys," featuring Ryan Gosling and Will Ferrell. Gold's involvement signifies a blend of fresh talent and established industry experience, which could bode well for the film's narrative depth and engagement.

In addition to Blumhouse, the project also involves Michael Bay's production company, Platinum Dunes. Known for its successful horror franchises, including "A Nightmare on Elm Street" and "The Texas Chainsaw Massacre," Platinum Dunes brings a wealth of experience and a proven track record in the genre. This collaboration suggests that "Detention" may benefit from a combination of innovative storytelling and high production values.

### Paul Feig's Shift to Horror

Feig, who has previously directed popular comedies such as "Bridesmaids" and "Spy," is making a significant shift in genre with "Detention." While he is best known for his comedic sensibilities, Feig has expressed a long-standing interest in horror, indicating that this project allows him to explore new creative avenues. His unique perspective may bring a fresh take to the horror genre, potentially blending elements of humor and suspense.

### The Rising Popularity of Horror Films

The horror genre has seen a resurgence in popularity in recent years, with films like "Get Out," "A Quiet Place," and "The Invisible Man" achieving both critical and commercial success. Blumhouse Productions has been at the forefront of this trend, consistently delivering films that resonate with audiences. The combination of Feig's directorial vision and Blumhouse's production expertise could result in a compelling addition to the genre.

### Conclusion

As "Detention" moves forward in development, anticipation is building among fans of both horror and Feig's previous works. With a talented writer and a well-established production team, the film has the potential to carve out its own niche within the crowded landscape of contemporary horror cinema. As details emerge, it will be interesting to see how Feig's unique style influences the project and whether "Detention" can stand out in a genre that continues to evolve.
