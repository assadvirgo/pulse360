## Tony Leung Discusses the Importance of Cinematic Experience at Shanghai Masterclass

At the Shanghai International Film Festival, acclaimed actor Tony Leung Chiu-wai engaged a captivated audience during a masterclass following the screening of his latest film, "Silent Friend." Leung, known for his nuanced performances and collaborations with renowned director Wong Kar-wai, emphasized the significance of experiencing films in a cinema setting to fully appreciate the subtleties of his acting style.

### The Art of Subtlety in Performance

During the event, Leung articulated how his restrained performance style often requires viewers to pay close attention to the smallest details. "Sometimes it might just be something on my fingers," he remarked, highlighting how even the slightest gesture can convey profound emotion and narrative depth. He urged the audience to experience his films in theaters, where the immersive environment allows for a deeper connection to the story and characters.

Leung's comments resonate with many cinephiles who believe that the cinematic experience is irreplaceable. The large screen, combined with high-quality sound, enhances the emotional impact of a film, allowing viewers to engage with the material in a way that home viewing cannot replicate.

### Wong Kar-wai's Influence

Leung's collaboration with Wong Kar-wai has been pivotal in shaping his career and acting approach. The two have worked together on several iconic films, including "In the Mood for Love" and "Chungking Express." Their partnership is characterized by a shared understanding of the importance of visual storytelling and emotional resonance.

In the masterclass, Leung reflected on the unique creative process he shares with Wong, noting that their films often explore themes of love, longing, and the passage of time. He expressed gratitude for the opportunity to work with such a visionary director, whose distinctive style has left an indelible mark on contemporary cinema.

### The Role of Film Festivals

The Shanghai International Film Festival serves as a crucial platform for filmmakers and actors to showcase their work and engage with audiences. Events like Leung's masterclass not only highlight the talents of established artists but also foster discussions about the art of filmmaking and the importance of preserving the cinematic experience in an age dominated by streaming services.

As the film industry continues to evolve, Leung's insistence on the value of theatrical screenings serves as a reminder of the communal aspect of watching films. It is in the shared experience of viewing a film that audiences can collectively feel the emotional weight of the story being told.

### Conclusion

Tony Leung's reflections at the Shanghai International Film Festival underscore the importance of the cinematic experience, particularly for performances that rely on subtlety and nuance. His insights into the collaborative nature of filmmaking, especially with Wong Kar-wai, highlight the rich tapestry of storytelling that cinema offers. As audiences navigate an increasingly digital landscape, Leung's call to return to the theater serves as a timely reminder of the magic that can only be found on the big screen.
