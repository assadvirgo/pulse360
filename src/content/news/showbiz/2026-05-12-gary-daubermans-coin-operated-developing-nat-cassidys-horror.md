## Gary Dauberman's Coin Operated to Adapt Nat Cassidy's Horror Novella 'Rest Stop'

In a significant development within the film industry, Gary Dauberman's production company, Coin Operated, has acquired the rights to adapt Nat Cassidy's recently published horror novella, "Rest Stop." This announcement marks an exciting step for both the filmmaker and the author, as they aim to bring Cassidy's chilling narrative to the big screen.

### Overview of 'Rest Stop'

"Rest Stop" tells the unsettling story of a young musician who finds himself trapped inside a gas station bathroom. The novella explores themes of isolation and fear, tapping into the primal anxieties that can arise in seemingly mundane situations. Cassidy's storytelling has garnered attention for its ability to blend psychological horror with relatable human experiences, making it a compelling choice for adaptation.

### The Creative Team

Nat Cassidy, who has proven his prowess in both writing and acting, will take on the task of adapting his own novella into a screenplay. This decision allows for a unique vision to be maintained throughout the transition from page to screen, as Cassidy is intimately familiar with the characters and themes of his work. 

Gary Dauberman, known for his successful contributions to the horror genre, including films like "It" and "The Nun," will produce the adaptation alongside Mia Maniscalco. Dauberman's experience in horror filmmaking positions him well to navigate the nuances of translating a literary work into a cinematic experience.

### Industry Implications

The acquisition of "Rest Stop" by Coin Operated highlights the ongoing trend of adapting literary works into films, particularly within the horror genre. As audiences continue to seek out fresh and innovative horror narratives, projects like this one are likely to resonate with viewers looking for new stories that evoke fear and suspense.

Moreover, the collaboration between Dauberman and Cassidy brings together established industry talent with emerging voices, potentially leading to a dynamic and engaging film. The success of this adaptation could pave the way for further collaborations and adaptations of other literary works in the horror sphere.
