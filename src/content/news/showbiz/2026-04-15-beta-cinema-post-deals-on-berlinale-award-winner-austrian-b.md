## Beta Cinema Secures Deals for Berlinale Award-Winning Film 'Four Minus Three'

Beta Cinema has announced a series of international distribution deals for its award-winning film 'Four Minus Three,' which recently garnered attention at the Berlinale 2026 Panorama section. The film, directed by Adrian Goiginger, has not only made a significant impact at the prestigious festival but is also experiencing a robust theatrical launch in Austria, its home country.

### A Compelling Narrative

'Four Minus Three' is an adaptation of the autobiographical bestseller by Barbara Pachl-Eberhart. The film narrates a poignant story of loss, exploring themes of grief and resilience. This emotional narrative has resonated with audiences and critics alike, contributing to its success in local film awards and its strong box office performance in Austria.

### Awards and Recognition

The film's accolades at the Berlinale and nominations for key local film awards highlight its artistic merit and the powerful storytelling that Goiginger has brought to the screen. Such recognition not only elevates the film's profile but also enhances its appeal to international distributors, as evidenced by the recent deals secured by Beta Cinema.

### Strong Theatrical Launch

In its home territory, 'Four Minus Three' has enjoyed a strong theatrical launch, indicating a positive reception from the Austrian audience. The film's ability to connect with viewers on an emotional level has likely contributed to its box office success, making it one of the standout films of the year in Austria.

### International Distribution

The distribution deals brokered by Beta Cinema are expected to expand the film's reach beyond Austria, allowing international audiences to engage with Pachl-Eberhart's compelling story. As the film continues to gain traction, it may pave the way for further adaptations of literary works in the cinematic landscape, showcasing the potential of personal narratives to resonate on a global scale.

### Conclusion

With its combination of critical acclaim, a strong local performance, and international distribution deals, 'Four Minus Three' stands as a testament to the power of storytelling in cinema.
