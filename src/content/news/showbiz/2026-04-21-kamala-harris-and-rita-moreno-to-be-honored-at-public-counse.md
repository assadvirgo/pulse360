## Kamala Harris and Rita Moreno to Be Honored at Public Counsel Awards Dinner

In a significant recognition of their contributions to justice and the arts, Vice President Kamala Harris and acclaimed actress Rita Moreno will be honored at the upcoming Public Counsel’s annual William O. Douglas Award Dinner. The event is scheduled for April 29, 2024, in Beverly Hills, California.

### Honoring Distinguished Achievements

The William O. Douglas Award, named after the former U.S. Supreme Court Justice, is presented to individuals whose work has profoundly advanced justice and equality. This year, Vice President Harris will receive this prestigious award for her extensive career dedicated to public service and advocacy for civil rights. Since taking office, Harris has been a prominent figure in addressing issues such as racial justice, women's rights, and immigration reform, making her a fitting recipient of this honor.

Rita Moreno, a trailblazer in the arts, will be awarded the Trailblazer in the Arts Award. Moreno’s career spans over seven decades, during which she has broken barriers for Hispanic performers in Hollywood. She is one of the few artists to have achieved the coveted EGOT status, winning an Emmy, Grammy, Oscar, and Tony. Her work has not only entertained audiences but has also paved the way for future generations of artists from diverse backgrounds.

### A Celebration of Justice and Creativity

The annual dinner serves as a platform for Public Counsel, the largest pro bono law firm in the United States, to celebrate individuals who exemplify the spirit of justice and creativity. The organization has a long history of advocating for the underrepresented and marginalized communities, providing legal assistance and championing systemic change.

Public Counsel’s Executive Director, Elizabeth D. McGowan, expressed enthusiasm for this year’s honorees, stating, “Both Vice President Harris and Rita Moreno embody the values of courage and resilience. Their contributions have made a lasting impact on our society, and we are proud to honor them at this year’s dinner.”

### Looking Ahead

The event promises to be a gathering of influential figures from various sectors, including law, entertainment, and social justice advocacy. It aims not only to recognize the achievements of Harris and Moreno but also to inspire attendees to continue the fight for equality and justice in their own communities.

As the date approaches, anticipation builds for what is expected to be a memorable evening celebrating the intersection of law, art, and advocacy. Tickets for the dinner are expected to sell out quickly, reflecting the high regard in which both honorees are held.

In conclusion, the Public Counsel’s William O. Douglas Award Dinner stands as a testament to the ongoing commitment to justice and equality, with Harris and Moreno at the forefront of this important mission. Their achievements serve as a reminder of the power of advocacy and the arts in shaping a more equitable society.
