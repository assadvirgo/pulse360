## Jennifer Harmon, Renowned Broadway Actor and Soap Star, Passes Away at 82

Jennifer Harmon, a celebrated figure in the world of theater and television, has passed away at the age of 82. Her family announced her death on Saturday, May 9, marking the end of a remarkable career that spanned several decades and left a lasting impact on both Broadway and daytime television.

### A Legacy in Theater

Harmon was known for her extensive work on Broadway, where she often took on the role of understudy for some of the industry's most prominent stars. Throughout her career, she shared the stage with notable actors such as Stockard Channing, Judi Dench, and Jessica Lange, showcasing her versatility and talent. Her ability to step into leading roles at a moment's notice demonstrated her dedication to the craft and her skill as a performer.

Her contributions to the theater were not limited to her performances; Harmon was also admired for her professionalism and the support she provided to her fellow actors. Colleagues often praised her for her ability to enhance the performances of those around her, making her a beloved figure in the theater community.

### Television Career

In addition to her work on stage, Harmon gained recognition for her role as a regular cast member on the ABC soap opera "One Life To Live" during the 1970s. The show, which became a staple of American daytime television, allowed Harmon to reach a wider audience and showcased her acting prowess in a different medium. Her character resonated with viewers, and her performances contributed to the show's enduring popularity.
