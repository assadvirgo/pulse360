## Dan Bucatinsky Discusses Season 3 of 'The Comeback' and Collaborations with Lisa Kudrow

As anticipation builds for the return of HBO's acclaimed mockumentary series 'The Comeback', co-producer and star Dan Bucatinsky shares insights into the show's third season, the evolving landscape of television, and his long-standing partnership with Lisa Kudrow. 

### A New Direction for Valerie Cherish

In the upcoming season, Valerie Cherish, portrayed by Kudrow, is set to make her much-anticipated return. The character, known for her quirky personality and relentless pursuit of fame, will navigate a new storyline that Bucatinsky describes as “agenda-less.” This shift suggests a departure from traditional plot structures, allowing for a more organic exploration of Valerie's character and her interactions with others, including her somewhat unreliable friend and manager, Billy Stanton, played by Bucatinsky himself.

Bucatinsky emphasizes the contrast between his real-life persona and that of Billy Stanton, stating, "I am nothing like the kind of partner that Billy is." This distinction highlights the comedic and often chaotic nature of Billy's role, which serves as a foil to Valerie's ambitions.

### The Evolution of Television and the Role of AI

The third season's exploration of an "agenda-less" narrative coincides with broader trends in the television industry, particularly the increasing influence of artificial intelligence (AI). Bucatinsky notes that the integration of AI into storytelling presents both challenges and opportunities for creators. The absence of a strict agenda allows for spontaneity, reflecting the unpredictable nature of modern life and the ways in which technology shapes personal and professional relationships.

As the industry grapples with the implications of AI, Bucatinsky's approach encourages a more fluid narrative style that resonates with contemporary audiences. This evolution in storytelling aligns with the series’ satirical take on Hollywood and the entertainment industry, providing a fresh lens through which to view the complexities of fame and creativity.

### A 25-Year Partnership with Lisa Kudrow

Bucatinsky's collaboration with Kudrow spans over two decades, marked by mutual respect and creative synergy. The duo's partnership has been instrumental in shaping 'The Comeback', allowing them to push boundaries and explore new comedic territory. Bucatinsky reflects on their journey, acknowledging that their shared vision has been a driving force behind the series' success.

Their longstanding relationship extends beyond the screen, as both have navigated the ever-changing landscape of television together. This camaraderie fosters an environment where innovative ideas can flourish, contributing to the show's unique charm and appeal.

### A 'Hacks' Crossover Fantasy

In a light-hearted moment, Bucatinsky expresses his "fantasy" of a crossover between 'The Comeback' and HBO's hit series 'Hacks'. The prospect of blending the worlds of Valerie Cherish and the characters from 'Hacks' presents an intriguing opportunity for comedic exploration. Such a crossover would not only entertain audiences but also highlight the interconnectedness of narratives within the television landscape.

### Conclusion

As 'The Comeback' prepares for its return, Dan Bucatinsky's insights shed light on the creative processes behind the series. With an agenda-less narrative, a focus on AI's role in storytelling, and a deep-rooted partnership with Lisa Kudrow, the upcoming season promises to deliver a fresh and engaging perspective on the complexities of fame and friendship in the modern world. Fans eagerly await Valerie Cherish's latest escapades, hopeful for a season that captures the essence of both humor and heart.
