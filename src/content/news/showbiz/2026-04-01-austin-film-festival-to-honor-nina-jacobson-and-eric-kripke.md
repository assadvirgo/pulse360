## Austin Film Festival to Honor Nina Jacobson and Eric Kripke at 2026 Awards Luncheon

The Austin Film Festival has announced that acclaimed producer Nina Jacobson and renowned television writer Eric Kripke will be the first honorees for its 2026 edition. The festival, known for celebrating the art of storytelling in film and television, will recognize Jacobson with the Polly Platt Award for Producing and Kripke with the Outstanding Television Writer Award during its annual awards luncheon on October 31, 2026.

### Honorees' Contributions to the Industry

Nina Jacobson has made significant contributions to the film industry, particularly through her work on major projects that have garnered both critical and commercial success. As the founder of Color Force, Jacobson has produced a variety of films and television series, including the popular "Hunger Games" franchise. Her ability to bring compelling stories to life has made her a respected figure in Hollywood, and the Polly Platt Award acknowledges her impact on the industry as a producer.

Eric Kripke, on the other hand, is celebrated for his innovative storytelling in television. Best known as the creator of the long-running series "Supernatural," Kripke has also been involved in other notable projects such as "The Boys," which has received widespread acclaim for its fresh take on the superhero genre. The Outstanding Television Writer Award highlights Kripke's ability to craft engaging narratives that resonate with audiences, solidifying his status as a leading voice in contemporary television.

### The Significance of the Awards Luncheon

The annual awards luncheon is a highlight of the Austin Film Festival, which aims to honor those who have made significant contributions to the film and television industries. The event not only recognizes individual achievements but also fosters a sense of community among filmmakers, writers, and industry professionals. By honoring figures like Jacobson and Kripke, the festival underscores the importance of storytelling in shaping culture and society.

### Looking Ahead to the 33rd Austin Film Festival

The 33rd Austin Film Festival is scheduled to take place from October 26 to November 2, 2026. This year's festival promises to showcase a diverse array of films, panels, and workshops, providing a platform for emerging talent and established industry veterans alike. The inclusion of Jacobson and Kripke as honorees reflects the festival's commitment to celebrating excellence in storytelling across various mediums.

As the festival approaches, anticipation is building among filmmakers and audiences alike. The recognition of Jacobson and Kripke not only highlights their individual achievements but also serves as an inspiration for aspiring writers and producers looking to make their mark in the industry.
