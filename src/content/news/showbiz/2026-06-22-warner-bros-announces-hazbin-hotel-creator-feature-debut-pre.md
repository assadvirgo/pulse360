## Warner Bros. Unveils New Animated Features at Annecy Animation Festival

At the 2026 Annecy Animation Festival, Warner Bros. Pictures Animation made significant announcements that have generated excitement among animation enthusiasts. The studio introduced two new animated feature films: "Prehistoria," created by the renowned "Hazbin Hotel" creator, and "Bad Fairies," which also received updates on its voice cast.

## "Prehistoria" - A New Venture for the "Hazbin Hotel" Creator

"Prehistoria" marks a notable departure for the creator of "Hazbin Hotel," who has gained a substantial following for their unique storytelling and artistic style. While details about the plot remain under wraps, the announcement has sparked curiosity about how the creator will translate their signature approach into a prehistoric setting. The film is anticipated to blend humor and adventure, appealing to both children and adults alike.

## Updates on "Bad Fairies"

In addition to "Prehistoria," Warner Bros. provided further insights into "Bad Fairies," an animated feature that has been in development for some time. The studio announced additional voice cast members, enhancing the film's roster of talent. While specific names were not disclosed in the initial announcement, the inclusion of well-known voice actors is expected to elevate the film's appeal.

"Bad Fairies" promises to deliver a fresh take on fairy tales, potentially subverting traditional narratives and offering a modern twist on familiar characters. The film aims to explore themes of empowerment and individuality, which resonates with contemporary audiences.
