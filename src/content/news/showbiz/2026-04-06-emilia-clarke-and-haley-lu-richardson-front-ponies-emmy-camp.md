Peacock, the streaming service owned by NBCUniversal, is gearing up for a significant push for its upcoming series "Ponies" in the lead-up to the 2026 Primetime Emmy Awards. The campaign aims to secure nominations across a broad spectrum of 20 comedy categories, positioning the show as a strong contender in the competitive landscape of television comedy.

### Overview of "Ponies"

"Ponies" is described as a spy dramedy that blends elements of comedy and drama, a genre that has seen increasing popularity in recent years. The series stars Emilia Clarke, known for her iconic role in "Game of Thrones," alongside Haley Lu Richardson, who has garnered attention for her performances in various independent films and television series. The combination of Clarke's star power and Richardson's rising profile is expected to attract significant attention from Emmy voters.

### Emmy Campaign Strategy

The Emmy campaign will focus on highlighting the performances of both lead and supporting cast members, with Clarke being submitted for the Lead Comedy Actress category. The show aims to capitalize on the growing trend of genre-bending series that resonate with audiences and critics alike. By submitting across multiple categories, Peacock hopes to maximize its chances of recognition and accolades during the awards season.
