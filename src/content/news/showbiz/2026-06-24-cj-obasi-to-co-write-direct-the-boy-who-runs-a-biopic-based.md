## CJ Obasi to Direct Biopic on Ugandan Athlete Julius Achon

Renowned filmmaker CJ Obasi, known for his work on the acclaimed film *Mami Wata*, has announced his next project: a biopic titled *The Boy Who Runs*. This film will chronicle the life of Julius Achon, a celebrated Ugandan distance runner who transitioned into a political career. 

### A Journey from Athlete to Politician

Julius Achon is a prominent figure in Uganda's sports history, having gained international recognition for his achievements on the track. His journey from a small village in Uganda to becoming an elite athlete and later a politician is a compelling narrative that highlights not only his athletic prowess but also his commitment to public service. Achon’s story is one of resilience, determination, and the pursuit of excellence, making it a fitting subject for a biographical film.

### Collaborative Effort in Screenwriting

In an exciting development for the film, CJ Obasi will not only direct but also co-write the screenplay alongside Kirimi Kiage and Teddy Gitau. This collaborative effort aims to bring a nuanced perspective to Achon’s life, ensuring that the film resonates with both local and international audiences. The involvement of multiple writers indicates a commitment to capturing the complexities of Achon’s experiences, both as an athlete and as a leader.

### Executive Production Team

Kudi Maradzika has been attached as the executive producer for *The Boy Who Runs*. Maradzika's experience in film production is expected to enhance the project's potential, providing the necessary resources and industry insights to bring Achon’s story to the big screen. The combination of Obasi’s directorial vision and Maradzika’s production expertise sets a promising foundation for the film.

### Significance of the Project

This biopic not only aims to celebrate Julius Achon’s athletic achievements but also seeks to inspire future generations. By highlighting the intersection of sports and politics, the film will explore themes of perseverance, community, and the impact of sports on societal change. As Uganda continues to grapple with various socio-political issues, Achon’s life story serves as a reminder of the power of individual agency and the potential for athletes to influence positive change in their communities.

### Conclusion

*The Boy Who Runs* is poised to be an important addition to the canon of sports biopics, particularly within the African context. With CJ Obasi at the helm, audiences can anticipate a thoughtfully crafted narrative that honors the legacy of Julius Achon. As production progresses, the film is expected to garner attention not only for its inspirational storyline but also for its cultural significance in portraying the life of a remarkable Ugandan figure. 

As the project develops, further details regarding the cast and production timeline are anticipated, promising an engaging cinematic experience that celebrates both athleticism and leadership.
