## ‘MasterChef Asia’ Set to Return After a Decade

In a significant development for culinary television, Banijay Rights has announced the revival of "MasterChef Asia," a beloved cooking competition series that has been on hiatus for the past ten years. This new iteration of the show is being produced in collaboration with Warner Bros. Discovery and CreAsia Studio, a subsidiary of Endemol Shine India.

## A New Chapter for a Popular Format

The upcoming series will consist of ten episodes, each running for approximately 60 minutes. This revival marks an important return of the "MasterChef" format to the Asian market, where it previously garnered a dedicated following. The announcement has generated excitement among fans and culinary enthusiasts alike, who are eager to see how the show will evolve in its new season.

## The Legacy of MasterChef

Originally launched in 1990 in the UK, the "MasterChef" format has expanded globally, with numerous adaptations across different countries. The Asian version first premiered in 2013, quickly becoming a staple of the region's entertainment landscape. The show combines elements of competition, culinary artistry, and personal storytelling, allowing contestants to showcase their skills while navigating challenges designed to test their culinary prowess.

## Production Insights

Banijay Rights, a leading global production and distribution company, has a strong track record in bringing successful formats to various markets. Their collaboration with Warner Bros. Discovery and CreAsia Studio is poised to leverage a wealth of experience in producing engaging content that resonates with audiences. The new series is expected to feature a diverse range of contestants from across Asia, highlighting the rich culinary traditions and innovations within the region.

## Anticipated Impact on Viewership

The revival of "MasterChef Asia" comes at a time when cooking shows are experiencing a resurgence in popularity. With an increasing number of viewers turning to culinary programming for entertainment and inspiration, the timing of this announcement is particularly strategic. The series is likely to attract not only long-time fans but also new viewers eager to engage with the culinary arts.
