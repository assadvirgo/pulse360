## ‘Selamlik’ Wins Eurimages Co-Production Development Award at Karlovy Vary Film Festival

The Karlovy Vary International Film Festival (KVIFF) has announced the winners of its prestigious Eurimages Co-Production Development Award during its industry program, KVIFF Promises. This year, the award has been bestowed upon the film "Selamlik," a collaborative project between Sweden and Denmark, directed by Jerry Carlsson and written by Khaled Alesmael.

### Overview of the Award

The Eurimages Co-Production Development Award is a significant accolade aimed at fostering international co-productions in the film industry. It carries a cash prize of €20,000 (approximately $22,800), which is intended to support the development of projects that demonstrate strong potential for international collaboration. The award is part of a broader initiative by Eurimages, a cultural support fund established by the Council of Europe, to promote the European film industry.

### About ‘Selamlik’

"Selamlik" stands out as a notable entry in this year's festival, reflecting the growing trend of cross-border collaborations in cinema. Directed by Jerry Carlsson, the film is co-written by Khaled Alesmael, who has garnered attention for his compelling storytelling that often explores themes of identity and cultural intersection. While specific plot details of "Selamlik" have not been disclosed, its recognition at KVIFF signals a promising trajectory for the film as it moves toward production.

### Significance of the Festival

The Karlovy Vary International Film Festival, held annually in the Czech Republic, is one of the oldest and most prestigious film festivals in Central and Eastern Europe. It serves as a vital platform for filmmakers from around the world to showcase their work, engage with industry professionals, and foster new partnerships. The festival's industry program, KVIFF Promises, plays a crucial role in supporting emerging talents and innovative projects.

### Conclusion

The recognition of "Selamlik" at the Karlovy Vary International Film Festival underscores the importance of international collaboration in the film industry. As the global landscape of cinema continues to evolve, awards like the Eurimages Co-Production Development Award serve not only to celebrate artistic achievement but also to encourage the development of diverse narratives that resonate across cultural boundaries. The film's journey from concept to screen will be closely watched by industry insiders and audiences alike, as it embodies the spirit of cooperation and creativity that defines contemporary filmmaking.
