## Riot Games Introduces On-Demand Anti-Cheat Feature for Valorant and League of Legends

In a significant update for players of its popular titles, Riot Games has announced a new feature that allows users to control the activation of its Vanguard anti-cheat software. This change aims to enhance user experience while maintaining the integrity of competitive gameplay.

### New Feature Overview

Starting today, players of **League of Legends** and **Valorant** can opt into a feature known as "Vanguard Pre-Check." This new functionality enables players to switch the Vanguard anti-cheat from a constant, always-on state to an on-demand mode. This means that the kernel-level driver, which is integral to the anti-cheat system, will not automatically launch when the player's system starts up.

### Hardware Requirements

To utilize this new feature, players must ensure that they have the appropriate hardware and must also enable "pre-boot security mechanisms" alongside Windows' native protection features. This requirement underscores Riot's commitment to maintaining a secure gaming environment while offering players more control over their systems.

### Implications for Players

The introduction of the on-demand anti-cheat feature is a response to ongoing discussions within the gaming community regarding privacy and system performance. Many players have expressed concerns about the impact of always-on software on system resources and overall gameplay experience. By allowing users to activate the anti-cheat only when needed, Riot Games aims to alleviate these concerns while still safeguarding against cheating.

### Balancing Security and User Experience

The decision to implement this change reflects a broader trend in the gaming industry towards balancing security measures with user autonomy. As competitive gaming continues to grow, so does the necessity for effective anti-cheat solutions. However, the implementation of such solutions must also consider the preferences and comfort of the player base.

Riot Games has been proactive in addressing player feedback regarding anti-cheat measures. The introduction of Vanguard Pre-Check is part of a larger effort to create a more user-friendly environment while ensuring that the integrity of competitive play is preserved.

### Conclusion

Riot Games' rollout of the on-demand Vanguard anti-cheat feature marks a notable shift in how anti-cheat systems are managed in competitive gaming. By providing players with greater control over their gaming environment, Riot not only enhances user experience but also reaffirms its commitment to maintaining a fair and competitive landscape in its games. As the gaming community continues to evolve, such innovations will likely play a crucial role in shaping the future of online gaming.
