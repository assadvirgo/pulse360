## Flatbush Zombies’ Erick the Architect Reflects on His Affection for the BlackBerry Keyboard

Erick the Architect, a prominent figure in the hip-hop collective Flatbush Zombies, has recently expressed his nostalgia for the BlackBerry keyboard. As a founding member and primary producer of the group, which has garnered international acclaim, Erick's reflections on this once-popular device highlight the intersection of technology and creativity in the music industry.

### A Journey Through Music

Flatbush Zombies, known for their unique blend of hip-hop and psychedelic influences, has made significant strides since their inception. The group has toured extensively, captivating audiences around the globe and performing on high-profile platforms such as "Jimmy Kimmel Live!" and "The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon." Their performances at major music festivals, including Coachella, have further solidified their status in the contemporary music scene.

Erick's collaborations extend beyond the confines of his group, showcasing his versatility as a producer. He has worked with a diverse array of artists, including Joey Bada$$, RZA, and James Blake, as well as hardcore punk band Trash Talk. These partnerships reflect a willingness to explore various genres and push creative boundaries.

### The BlackBerry Keyboard: A Tool for Creativity

In a recent conversation, Erick the Architect reminisced about the BlackBerry keyboard, a device that many may associate with the early 2000s. He noted the tactile experience of typing on a physical keyboard, which he believes fostered a different kind of creativity compared to modern touchscreen devices. For Erick, the BlackBerry was not just a communication tool; it was a medium that facilitated the flow of ideas and music.

The nostalgia surrounding the BlackBerry keyboard speaks to a broader trend in the tech world, where many creatives are seeking a more tangible connection to their work. The rise of smartphones has transformed the way artists create and communicate, but for some, the simplicity and reliability of older technology remain appealing.

### The Intersection of Technology and Art

Erick's fondness for the BlackBerry keyboard raises intriguing questions about the relationship between technology and artistic expression. As the music industry continues to evolve with advancements in digital tools and platforms, artists must navigate a landscape that is increasingly influenced by technology. While modern devices offer unparalleled convenience and connectivity, they can also introduce distractions that may hinder the creative process.

In this context, Erick's reflections serve as a reminder of the importance of finding a balance between embracing new technologies and honoring the tools that have historically shaped artistic practices. The tactile experience of a physical keyboard may seem outdated to some, but for artists like Erick, it represents a connection to a different era of creativity.

### Conclusion

As Flatbush Zombies continues to push the boundaries of hip-hop, Erick the Architect's appreciation for the BlackBerry keyboard highlights the ongoing dialogue between technology and art. In an age dominated by digital innovation, his sentiments resonate with many artists who seek to maintain a sense of authenticity in their work. As the music industry evolves, it is essential to remember the tools that have paved the way for creative expression, regardless of their technological advancements.
