## Final Call for Startup Battlefield 200 Applications

Today marks the final opportunity for startups and founders to apply or nominate candidates for the prestigious Startup Battlefield 200. The application window closes at 11:59 p.m. PT, concluding a critical phase for entrepreneurs seeking to elevate their ventures.

### What is Startup Battlefield 200?

Startup Battlefield 200 is a renowned competition organized by TechCrunch, designed to showcase innovative startups from around the globe. This year, the event promises to be a significant platform for emerging companies to gain exposure, connect with potential investors, and compete for a substantial prize. The selected startups will have the unique opportunity to launch their products on the TechCrunch Disrupt stage, a high-profile event that attracts industry leaders, investors, and media attention.

### Benefits of Participation

Participating in Startup Battlefield 200 offers numerous advantages for startups. One of the most enticing aspects is the chance to win $100,000 in equity-free funding. This financial support can be pivotal for early-stage companies looking to scale their operations or enhance their product offerings. 

In addition to the monetary prize, startups will gain invaluable visibility on a global scale. The exposure from presenting at TechCrunch Disrupt can lead to significant networking opportunities, allowing founders to connect directly with investors who are actively seeking innovative solutions and technologies.

### How to Apply

Interested founders and startups can apply through the official TechCrunch website. The application process requires candidates to provide detailed information about their business model, product, and market strategy. Nominations can also be submitted for startups that may not have applied directly but are deemed worthy of consideration.

### The Importance of Timeliness

As the deadline approaches, entrepreneurs are encouraged to finalize their applications and ensure that all necessary information is submitted before the cutoff time. This final push is crucial, as missing the deadline could mean losing out on a transformative opportunity for growth and recognition within the tech community.
