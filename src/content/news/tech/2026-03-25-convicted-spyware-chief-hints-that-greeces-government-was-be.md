## Convicted Spyware Chief Alleges Government Involvement in Phone Hacks

In a striking revelation, the founder of Intellexa, a company known for developing spyware, has suggested that the Greek government may have been involved in the hacking of numerous phones belonging to high-profile individuals. This assertion marks the most explicit claim from anyone within Intellexa regarding the alleged authorization of such activities by the administration of Prime Minister Kyriakos Mitsotakis.

### Background on Intellexa and Its Operations

Intellexa, which has gained notoriety for its spyware products, has been at the center of controversy surrounding the surveillance of political figures and journalists. The company has been accused of facilitating illegal hacking operations, raising significant concerns about privacy and the rule of law in Greece. The recent comments from its founder, who has been convicted for his role in these activities, add a new layer of complexity to the ongoing discussions about government surveillance and accountability.

### Allegations Against the Mitsotakis Government

The implications of the founder's statements are profound. They suggest that the Greek government may have sanctioned the hacking of devices belonging to senior government ministers, opposition leaders, military officials, and journalists. Such actions, if proven true, would not only violate individual privacy rights but could also undermine democratic processes in the country.

The Mitsotakis government has faced scrutiny over its handling of surveillance issues, particularly following revelations about the use of spyware against political opponents and journalists. The allegations made by the Intellexa founder could further erode public trust in the government, which is already grappling with criticism regarding its transparency and accountability.

### The Political and Legal Ramifications

The potential involvement of the government in these hacking activities raises significant political and legal questions. If the allegations are substantiated, it could lead to calls for investigations into the government's practices and policies regarding surveillance. Additionally, it may prompt a broader discussion about the legal frameworks governing the use of spyware and the protection of citizens' rights in Greece.

Opposition parties have already expressed outrage over the allegations, demanding a thorough investigation into the claims. The political landscape in Greece could shift dramatically if evidence emerges linking the government to these unauthorized surveillance activities.

### Public Reaction and Future Implications

Public reaction to the allegations has been mixed, with many citizens expressing concern over the implications for civil liberties and democratic governance. The situation highlights the delicate balance between national security and individual privacy rights, a debate that is increasingly relevant in the digital age.

As the story unfolds, the Greek government will likely face mounting pressure to address these allegations transparently. The outcome of any investigations could have lasting implications for the political climate in Greece, as well as for the country's approach to surveillance and privacy in the future.

In conclusion, the allegations made by the convicted spyware chief present a significant challenge to the Mitsotakis government and raise critical questions about the intersection of technology, privacy, and governance in Greece. As the nation grapples with these issues, the need for clear policies and accountability in the realm of surveillance becomes ever more pressing.
