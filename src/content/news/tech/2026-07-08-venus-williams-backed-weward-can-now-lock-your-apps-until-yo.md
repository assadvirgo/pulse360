## Venus Williams Backs WeWard: A New Approach to Fitness Motivation

In an innovative move to encourage physical activity, the French app WeWard has announced a new feature that allows users to lock their applications until they reach a predetermined step count. This initiative, backed by tennis star and angel investor Venus Williams, aims to motivate individuals to increase their daily walking time, a goal that the app claims can be achieved by nearly 25% through its unique approach.

## The Concept Behind WeWard

WeWard is designed to promote a healthier lifestyle by rewarding users for their physical activity. The app tracks users' steps and converts them into points, which can then be redeemed for various rewards, including discounts and gift cards. The introduction of the app-locking feature is a strategic enhancement intended to further incentivize users to meet their walking goals. By restricting access to certain applications until users complete their step targets, WeWard aims to create a more focused and health-oriented digital environment.

## Impact of the New Feature

According to WeWard's data, the app has already demonstrated a significant impact on users' walking habits. With the new app-locking feature, the company anticipates an even greater increase in physical activity. The app's developers believe that by adding an element of urgency and commitment, users will be more likely to prioritize walking over sedentary activities, such as scrolling through social media or browsing the internet.

The concept of gamifying fitness is not new, but WeWard's approach of locking apps adds a layer of accountability that could resonate with users who struggle to find motivation. By leveraging the influence of Venus Williams, a prominent figure in sports and wellness, the app is positioned to reach a broader audience and encourage a cultural shift towards more active lifestyles.

## Venus Williams' Role

Venus Williams, a seven-time Grand Slam champion, has long been an advocate for health and wellness. Her involvement with WeWard underscores her commitment to promoting physical fitness and healthy living. Williams' investment not only provides financial backing but also brings significant visibility to the app, potentially attracting new users who admire her dedication to fitness.

In a statement, Williams expressed her enthusiasm for the app's mission, highlighting the importance of integrating physical activity into daily routines. "WeWard is not just about walking; it's about creating a community that supports each other in leading healthier lives," she stated.

## Future Prospects

As WeWard continues to evolve, the company is exploring additional features and partnerships that could enhance user engagement and reward systems. The app-locking feature is just one of many innovations aimed at fostering a culture of fitness and well-being. With the backing of high-profile investors like Williams, WeWard is poised to make a significant impact in the health and fitness app market.

In conclusion, WeWard's new app-locking feature represents a creative and practical solution to encourage more walking and reduce screen time. With the support of Venus Williams, the app is not only promoting physical activity but also redefining how technology can be used to foster healthier habits in a digital age. As users embrace this new functionality, the potential for increased daily activity and improved health outcomes could be substantial.
