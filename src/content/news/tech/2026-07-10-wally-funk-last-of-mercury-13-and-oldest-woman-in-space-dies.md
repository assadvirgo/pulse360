## Wally Funk, Pioneer of Space Exploration, Passes Away at 87

Wally Funk, a trailblazer in the field of aviation and space exploration, has passed away at the age of 87. Known for her tenacity and passion for flying, Funk was the last surviving member of the Mercury 13, a group of women who underwent the same rigorous training as their male counterparts in the early days of the American space program. Her contributions to aerospace and her lifelong dream of space travel have left an indelible mark on the industry.

### Early Life and Aviation Career

Born on February 1, 1939, in Taft, California, Funk developed an interest in aviation at a young age. She earned her private pilot's license at just 17 years old and went on to become a flight instructor. Her remarkable skills and determination led her to break barriers in a male-dominated field. In the early 1960s, Funk was selected as one of the 13 women who would undergo the same tests as the Mercury astronauts, though they were ultimately not allowed to fly.

### The Mercury 13 Program

The Mercury 13 program was a pioneering initiative that aimed to evaluate women for spaceflight. Despite passing rigorous physical and psychological tests, the women were not given the opportunity to fly. Funk, however, remained undeterred in her pursuit of space travel. Her commitment to aviation and her advocacy for women in STEM fields continued throughout her life, inspiring generations of female aviators and astronauts.

### A Dream Realized

In July 2021, Funk's lifelong dream of going to space was finally realized when she flew aboard Blue Origin's New Shepard spacecraft. At the age of 82, she became the oldest person to travel to space, fulfilling a dream she had held since childhood. During the flight, Funk expressed her excitement, stating, "I have been waiting a long time to finally get up there." Her journey was not only a personal triumph but also a significant moment in the history of space exploration, highlighting the importance of inclusivity in the field.

### Legacy and Impact

Funk's passing marks the end of an era for the Mercury 13 and serves as a reminder of the challenges women have faced in the pursuit of careers in aviation and space. Her legacy is one of perseverance, courage, and a relentless pursuit of dreams. Funk's story has been a source of inspiration for many, emphasizing the importance of breaking barriers and advocating for equality in all fields.

### Conclusion

Wally Funk's contributions to aviation and space exploration will not be forgotten. Her life exemplified the spirit of adventure and the belief that dreams can be realized, regardless of gender.
