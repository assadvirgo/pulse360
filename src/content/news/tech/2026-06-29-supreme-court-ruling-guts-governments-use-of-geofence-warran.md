## Supreme Court Ruling Limits Government Use of Geofence Warrants

In a significant ruling, the Supreme Court of the United States has addressed the contentious issue of geofence warrants, which allow law enforcement agencies to obtain location data from mobile devices within a specified geographic area. While the Court did not declare these warrants unconstitutional, its decision has imposed important limitations on their use, signaling a potential shift in the balance between law enforcement practices and individual privacy rights.

### Background on Geofence Warrants

Geofence warrants have become increasingly popular among law enforcement agencies as a tool for criminal investigations. These warrants enable authorities to request data from technology companies, such as Google, to identify and track individuals who were present in a specific location during a particular timeframe. This method has been utilized in various cases, ranging from theft to more serious crimes, as it allows investigators to gather evidence without directly surveilling individuals.

However, the use of geofence warrants has raised significant privacy concerns. Critics argue that these warrants can lead to mass surveillance, infringing upon Fourth Amendment rights against unreasonable searches and seizures. The potential for misuse and the broad scope of data collection have prompted calls for clearer regulations governing their application.
