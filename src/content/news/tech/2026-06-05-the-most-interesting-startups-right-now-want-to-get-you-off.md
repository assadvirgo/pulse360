## Startups Focus on Reducing Screen Time with Innovative Concepts

In an era where technology often dominates daily life, a new wave of startups is emerging with the mission of encouraging individuals to disconnect from their screens and engage in more tangible, real-world experiences. As artificial intelligence (AI) continues to attract significant investment and attention, these companies are taking a different approach, focusing on fostering human connection through in-person interactions and creative activities.

### Board: A New Approach to Social Gaming

One of the standout startups in this movement is Board, founded by Brynn Putnam, who has successfully raised funding to develop a platform centered around social games and experiences. The goal of Board is to bring people together in physical spaces, promoting social interaction in a world increasingly dominated by digital communication. By creating engaging, in-person games, Board aims to cultivate community and connection, countering the isolation that often accompanies excessive screen time.

Putnam's vision reflects a growing awareness of the need for balance in our digital lives. As more individuals report feelings of burnout and anxiety associated with constant connectivity, initiatives like Board offer a refreshing alternative, inviting users to step away from their devices and engage with one another in meaningful ways.

### Cyberdeck: The DIY Movement Encouraging Outdoor Exploration

Another innovative startup gaining traction is Cyberdeck, which has captured the imagination of tech enthusiasts with its whimsical DIY computers. These devices are designed not only to be functional but also to inspire users to "touch grass," a colloquial expression encouraging people to spend time outdoors. By crafting unique and playful technology, Cyberdeck promotes a hands-on approach to computing that emphasizes creativity and exploration beyond the confines of a screen.

The Cyberdeck movement resonates with a growing desire among individuals, particularly younger generations, to reconnect with their surroundings and engage in activities that foster creativity and physical interaction. As people increasingly seek to balance their digital lives with real-world experiences, startups like Cyberdeck are leading the charge in redefining how technology can be integrated into our lives.

### The Broader Trend: A Shift Towards Physical Engagement

The emergence of these startups highlights a broader trend in the tech industry: a counter-movement to the pervasive influence of digital devices. While AI and other technologies continue to advance and attract significant funding, there is a palpable shift towards valuing human interaction and physical experiences.

This trend is not merely a backlash against technology; rather, it reflects a nuanced understanding of how technology can be used to enhance, rather than replace, human connection. As more founders recognize the importance of fostering real-world engagement, the landscape of tech startups may evolve to prioritize experiences that bring people together.

### Conclusion

As we navigate an increasingly digital world, startups like Board and Cyberdeck are paving the way for a new paradigm that emphasizes the importance of stepping away from screens and embracing the richness of in-person interactions. Their innovative approaches not only address the challenges posed by excessive screen time but also offer a hopeful vision for the future of technology—one where human connection remains at the forefront.
