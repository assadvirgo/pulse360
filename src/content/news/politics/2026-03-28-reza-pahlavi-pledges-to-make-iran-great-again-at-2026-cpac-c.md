## Reza Pahlavi Advocates for a Stronger Iran at 2026 CPAC Conference

Reza Pahlavi, the Iranian opposition leader and son of the late Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi, made headlines during his recent address at the 2026 Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC). Pahlavi's speech resonated with themes of national pride and resilience, as he pledged to "make Iran great again," echoing a sentiment familiar to many political movements worldwide.

### A Call for Solidarity

During his address, Pahlavi emphasized the importance of continued support from the United States, particularly urging the Trump administration to "stay the course" in its stance against the Iranian regime. His remarks come at a time when tensions between Iran, the U.S., and Israel have escalated, with ongoing geopolitical conflicts drawing international attention.

Pahlavi's call for solidarity is not merely a plea for political backing; it reflects a broader desire among Iranian expatriates and supporters of democracy within Iran for a united front against the current government. He articulated a vision of a future Iran that embraces democratic values, human rights, and economic prosperity, contrasting sharply with the current regime's policies.

### The Context of His Message

The backdrop of Pahlavi's speech is significant. The Iranian regime has faced increasing criticism for its domestic policies and international behavior, including its nuclear ambitions and regional military engagements. Pahlavi's advocacy for a strong U.S. stance against Iran is rooted in a belief that external pressure can facilitate change within the country.

Furthermore, his remarks at CPAC highlight the intersection of Iranian politics with American conservatism, suggesting a potential alliance that could influence U.S. foreign policy in the region. By aligning himself with American conservative values, Pahlavi aims to garner support from a political base that has historically been skeptical of the Iranian government.

### The Future of Iranian Politics

Pahlavi's vision for Iran includes a commitment to restoring the nation's historical significance on the global stage. He envisions an Iran that not only thrives economically but also plays a constructive role in international affairs. His rhetoric suggests a desire to foster relationships with Western nations, particularly the U.S., while advocating for a democratic transition within Iran.

The implications of Pahlavi's speech extend beyond immediate political alliances; they also reflect a growing sentiment among Iranians both at home and abroad who yearn for change. His presence at CPAC signifies a strategic move to leverage American political dynamics in favor of his cause, potentially paving the way for increased activism and advocacy for Iranian democracy.

### Conclusion

As the geopolitical landscape continues to shift, the role of figures like Reza Pahlavi becomes increasingly relevant. His call to "make Iran great again" resonates with a generation that aspires for reform and a departure from authoritarian rule. The ongoing dialogue between Iranian opposition leaders and U.S. policymakers will likely shape the future of U.S.-Iran relations and the prospects for democratic change in Iran. As Pahlavi continues to advocate for his vision, the international community will be watching closely to see how these dynamics unfold in the coming years.
