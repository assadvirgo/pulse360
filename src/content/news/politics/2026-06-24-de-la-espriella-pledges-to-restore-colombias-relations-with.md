Under Gustavo Petro, Colombia's foreign policy underwent a notable transformation. The Petro administration emphasized a more independent approach, often distancing itself from traditional allies like the United States and Israel. This shift was characterized by a focus on human rights and a critique of U.S. foreign policy in Latin America, which Petro argued often undermined regional stability.

The previous administration's stance led to a cooling of relations with Israel, particularly regarding issues related to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. Petro's government expressed solidarity with the Palestinian cause, which was met with mixed reactions domestically and internationally.
