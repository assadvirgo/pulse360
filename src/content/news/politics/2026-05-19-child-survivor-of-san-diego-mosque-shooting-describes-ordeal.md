## Child Survivor of San Diego Mosque Shooting Shares Experience

In the aftermath of the tragic shooting at a mosque in San Diego, a young boy who survived the incident has come forward to share his harrowing experience. The shooting, which took place during a community gathering, resulted in the loss of three lives and left many others traumatized.

On a recent evening, the mosque was filled with families and community members when the shooting occurred. Eyewitness accounts describe a scene of chaos and fear as the gunfire erupted. The assailant, whose motives remain under investigation, targeted the gathering, prompting an immediate response from law enforcement.

### Survivor's Account

The child, who has not been publicly identified, spoke about the ordeal, expressing his confusion and fear. "I saw bad stuff," he recounted, reflecting the emotional toll the event has taken on him and others present. His description underscores the impact of such violence on young minds, as the memories of that night will likely linger for years to come.

Community leaders and mental health professionals have emphasized the importance of providing support to survivors, particularly children, who may struggle to process the trauma associated with such violent events. Vigils have been held to honor the lives lost, and local organizations are stepping up to provide counseling and resources for those in need. The mosque, a place of worship and community gathering, has become a focal point for healing and solidarity.

Local officials have also expressed their condolences and pledged to work towards ensuring the safety of all community members. The shooting has reignited discussions about gun control and public safety, with many advocating for stronger regulations to prevent similar tragedies.

### Moving Forward

As investigations continue, the community remains in mourning, grappling with the senseless loss of life.
