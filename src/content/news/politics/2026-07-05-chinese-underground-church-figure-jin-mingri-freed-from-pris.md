## Chinese Underground Church Leader Jin Mingri Released from Prison

Jin Mingri, the founder of the Zion Church in Beijing, has been released from prison, marking a significant development in the ongoing discourse surrounding religious freedom in China. His release has garnered international attention, particularly due to the involvement of former U.S. President Donald Trump, who reportedly made a direct appeal to Chinese President Xi Jinping on Jin's behalf.

## Background on Jin Mingri and the Zion Church

Jin Mingri has been a prominent figure in China's underground Christian community. The Zion Church, which he established, has been known for its non-registered status, operating outside the purview of the state-sanctioned religious organizations. This has often placed it at odds with the Chinese government, which maintains strict control over religious practices and organizations within its borders.

The church has faced significant pressure from authorities, leading to the detention of its leaders and members. Jin's imprisonment was part of a broader crackdown on unregistered religious groups, which the government views as a threat to its authority and social stability.

## The Role of International Advocacy

Jin's release is seen as a victory for advocates of religious freedom and human rights. ## Implications for Religious Freedom in China

Jin Mingri's release raises questions about the future of religious freedom in China. While it may signal a temporary easing of restrictions for the Zion Church and similar organizations, the broader landscape remains fraught with challenges. The Chinese government continues to prioritize control over religious practices, often leading to crackdowns on groups that do not conform to state-sanctioned norms.

The case of Jin Mingri serves as a reminder of the ongoing struggle for religious freedom in China, where underground churches and other religious minorities often face persecution.
