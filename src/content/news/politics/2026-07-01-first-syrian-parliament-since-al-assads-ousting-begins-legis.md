## First Syrian Parliament Since al-Assad’s Ousting Begins Legislative Duties

In a significant political development, Syria's newly formed 210-member parliament has commenced its legislative duties, marking a historic transition in the country's governance following decades of rule by the Assad family. This parliament, which is the first to be established since the ousting of former President Bashar al-Assad, represents a pivotal moment in Syria's ongoing journey toward political reform and stability.

### A New Era in Syrian Politics

The establishment of this parliament is seen as a crucial step in the reformation of Syria's political landscape, which has been dominated by the Assad regime for over four decades. The new legislative body is expected to play a vital role in addressing the myriad challenges facing the country, including economic recovery, social cohesion, and the restoration of public trust in government institutions.

The parliament's formation comes after a prolonged period of civil unrest and conflict that has ravaged Syria since 2011. The ousting of al-Assad was a result of widespread protests against his regime, which were met with violent repression. The subsequent civil war has led to a humanitarian crisis, displacing millions and creating a fragmented society.

### Composition and Responsibilities

The 210 members of the new parliament represent a diverse array of political factions and social groups, reflecting the complex tapestry of Syrian society. Their primary responsibilities will include drafting and enacting legislation, overseeing government actions, and addressing the needs and concerns of their constituents.

The parliament's inaugural session is expected to focus on urgent issues such as economic recovery plans, the rebuilding of infrastructure, and the establishment of a framework for national reconciliation. Lawmakers will also need to address the pressing needs of the population, which has been severely affected by years of conflict and instability.

### Challenges Ahead

Despite the optimism surrounding the establishment of the new parliament, significant challenges remain. The legacy of the Assad regime, characterized by authoritarianism and repression, continues to cast a long shadow over the political landscape. Many citizens remain skeptical about the parliament's ability to effect real change, given the entrenched interests that have historically dominated Syrian politics.

Moreover, the ongoing geopolitical complexities in the region, including foreign interventions and the presence of various militant groups, complicate the path toward a stable and democratic governance structure. The parliament will need to navigate these challenges carefully to gain legitimacy and support from the populace.

### Conclusion

The beginning of legislative duties by Syria's first parliament since the ousting of Bashar al-Assad marks a crucial juncture in the nation's political evolution. While the establishment of this body offers hope for a more representative and accountable government, the road ahead is fraught with challenges. The success of this new parliament will ultimately depend on its ability to address the pressing needs of the Syrian people and foster a climate of trust and cooperation in a country still healing from years of conflict.
