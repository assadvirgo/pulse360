## Tiger Woods' Arrest Report Reveals Details of Recent Incident

The recent arrest report concerning professional golfer Tiger Woods has been made public, shedding light on the circumstances surrounding his car crash last week. According to the report released by local law enforcement, Woods was found with hydrocodone pills in his possession and exhibited signs of impairment, including bloodshot eyes.

### Incident Overview

The incident occurred in the early hours of the morning when Woods was involved in a single-car accident. The report indicates that police arrived at the scene to find the athlete in a state that raised concerns about his sobriety. Witnesses reported that Woods' vehicle was found in a ditch, and there were no other cars involved in the crash.

### Findings at the Scene

Upon approaching Woods, officers noted that he appeared disoriented, with bloodshot eyes and slurred speech. The sheriff's report details that Woods was cooperative with law enforcement but was unable to perform field sobriety tests effectively. The presence of hydrocodone, a prescription pain medication, in his pocket further complicated the situation, leading to questions about his state at the time of the accident.

The findings in the arrest report raise significant legal and medical implications for Woods. Hydrocodone is classified as an opioid, and its use can impair motor skills and cognitive function. The report does not specify whether Woods had a valid prescription for the medication, which could impact any potential legal ramifications.
