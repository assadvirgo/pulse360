## Christians in Lebanon Observe Good Friday Amid Ongoing Israeli Attacks

On April 7, 2023, Christians throughout Lebanon marked Good Friday in a climate of tension and uncertainty, as ongoing Israeli airstrikes and evacuation orders cast a shadow over the traditional observances. The day, which commemorates the crucifixion of Jesus Christ, was filled with prayers for peace and calls for an end to violence.

## Context of the Observance

Good Friday is a significant day in the Christian liturgical calendar, observed with solemnity and reflection. In Lebanon, where a diverse array of Christian denominations coexist, the day typically involves church services, processions, and communal gatherings. However, this year’s observance was markedly different, as many communities faced the harsh realities of conflict.

The recent escalation of violence in the region has resulted in heightened tensions, particularly in southern Lebanon, where Israeli military operations have intensified. Reports indicate that airstrikes have targeted various locations, leading to widespread fear and displacement among residents. In this context, the Good Friday services took on an added layer of urgency, as congregants prayed not only for spiritual salvation but also for the safety and well-being of their families and communities.

## Prayers for Peace

During the services, church leaders emphasized the importance of peace and reconciliation. Many congregants expressed their hopes for an end to hostilities and a return to stability in the region. "We pray for peace, for an end to the suffering of our people, and for the protection of our land," said Father Michel, a priest in Beirut, during a sermon. The sentiment was echoed across various congregations, where prayers were offered for those affected by the violence.

In addition to prayers, some churches organized initiatives to support those displaced by the conflict, providing shelter and basic necessities to those in need. The spirit of community and solidarity was evident, as many Lebanese Christians sought to support one another during this challenging time.

## International Reactions

The ongoing conflict has drawn international attention, with various governments and organizations calling for restraint and dialogue. The United Nations has urged all parties involved to prioritize civilian safety and adhere to international humanitarian law. As the situation develops, the global community continues to monitor the region, advocating for a peaceful resolution to the ongoing crisis.

## Conclusion

As Christians in Lebanon observed Good Friday under the shadow of conflict, their prayers for peace resonate beyond the walls of their churches. The hope for a cessation of violence and a return to normalcy remains a central theme in their reflections and prayers. The resilience of the Lebanese people, particularly in the face of adversity, serves as a testament to their enduring faith and commitment to peace. As the situation evolves, the world watches closely, hoping for a resolution that will allow all communities in Lebanon to live in harmony.
