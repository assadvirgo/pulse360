## England's Quansah Banned for Two Matches Following World Cup Red Card

In a significant development for England's national football team, defender Jarell Quansah has been handed a two-match suspension after receiving a red card during the last-16 match of the FIFA World Cup. The incident occurred due to a sliding studs-up tackle, which led to his expulsion from the game.

### Incident Overview

The controversial tackle took place in the second half of the knockout match, which saw England facing off against their opponents in a high-stakes encounter. Quansah's challenge was deemed reckless by the match officials, resulting in an immediate red card. This decision not only affected the dynamics of the match but also has now placed the player's participation in the subsequent games in jeopardy.

### Impact on the Team

As a direct consequence of the suspension, Quansah will miss England's crucial quarterfinal match against Norway. This absence is particularly notable as the team prepares to advance further in the tournament. Quansah has been a key player for England, and his defensive skills will be sorely missed in this critical stage of the competition.
