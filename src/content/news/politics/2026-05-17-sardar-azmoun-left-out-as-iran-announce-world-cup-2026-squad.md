## Sardar Azmoun Omitted from Iran's World Cup 2026 Preliminary Squad

In a significant development for Iranian football, veteran striker Sardar Azmoun has been left out of the national team's preliminary 30-man squad for the upcoming 2026 FIFA World Cup. The announcement comes as Iran prepares for a rigorous selection process, with the final squad set to be trimmed to 26 players by June 1.

### Azmoun's Career Overview

Sardar Azmoun, often referred to as "the Persian Messi" due to his playing style and skill set, has been a pivotal figure in Iran's recent football history. His contributions to the national team have been substantial, with numerous goals during qualifying matches and international tournaments. Over the years, Azmoun has garnered attention not only for his performances in domestic leagues but also for his stints in top European clubs, including Zenit Saint Petersburg and Bayer Leverkusen.

The final selection process is crucial, as it will determine the players who will represent Iran on the global stage. As the deadline approaches for finalizing the squad, it remains to be seen whether Azmoun will be recalled or if the coaching staff will maintain their current selections.
