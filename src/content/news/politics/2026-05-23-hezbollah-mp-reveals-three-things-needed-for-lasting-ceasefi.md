## Hezbollah MP Outlines Conditions for Lasting Ceasefire with Israel

In the midst of ongoing tensions and hostilities between Israel and Hezbollah, a member of the Lebanese Parliament has articulated three essential conditions that he believes are necessary for establishing a sustainable ceasefire. This comes as the current US-brokered ceasefire, which has been in place for a month, continues to struggle in effectively halting the violence.

### Background of the Conflict

The conflict between Israel and Hezbollah has deep historical roots, often characterized by intermittent clashes and escalations. The latest round of fighting has seen the Israel Defence Forces (IDF) engaged in military operations against Hezbollah, an Iran-backed Shia Muslim armed group based in Lebanon. Despite international efforts, including mediation from the United States, the ceasefire has not succeeded in bringing about lasting peace.

### Conditions for a Sustainable Ceasefire

The Hezbollah MP, whose identity has not been disclosed in the reports, emphasized the need for three critical elements to pave the way for a more enduring cessation of hostilities. 

1. **Mutual Recognition of Sovereignty**: The MP stated that both parties must acknowledge each other's territorial rights and sovereignty. This recognition is seen as a fundamental step toward reducing hostilities and building trust between the two sides.

2. **International Guarantees**: The need for international oversight and guarantees was highlighted as crucial. The MP argued that a third-party entity, potentially the United Nations or another international body, should be involved to ensure compliance with the ceasefire terms and to monitor any violations.

3. **Dialogue and Diplomacy**: Finally, the MP called for a renewed commitment to dialogue. He stressed that sustained communication between the parties involved is vital for addressing underlying grievances and for the long-term resolution of the conflict.

### Challenges Ahead

While these conditions present a roadmap for peace, the path to achieving a lasting ceasefire remains fraught with challenges. The ongoing military engagements and mutual distrust complicate the prospect of dialogue. Furthermore, the geopolitical dynamics in the region, particularly the influence of Iran and the role of the United States, add layers of complexity to the situation.

### Conclusion

As the fighting continues, the call for a more comprehensive approach to ceasefire negotiations becomes increasingly urgent. The Hezbollah MP's outlined conditions may serve as a foundation for future discussions, but the realization of a lasting peace will require significant political will and cooperation from all parties involved. The international community's role in facilitating dialogue and ensuring adherence to ceasefire agreements will also be critical in the months ahead.
