## Amnesty International Raises Concerns Over Human Rights Risks at 2026 World Cup

As preparations ramp up for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, Amnesty International has issued a stark warning regarding potential human rights risks that could affect millions of fans attending the event in the United States. The organization emphasizes the need for robust measures to ensure the safety and rights of attendees, particularly in light of various social and political issues that could arise during the tournament.

### Context of the Warning

The 2026 World Cup is set to be a landmark event, as it will be jointly hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, marking the first time the tournament will feature an expanded format with 48 teams. With the anticipation of millions of visitors converging on the host cities, Amnesty International's concerns highlight the critical need for host nations to prioritize human rights protections.

In a statement released by Amnesty, the organization outlined specific areas of concern, including the treatment of marginalized communities, the potential for increased surveillance, and the risks associated with large-scale gatherings. These factors, they argue, could lead to violations of fundamental rights, including freedom of expression and assembly.

### Specific Risks Identified

Amnesty International's report points to several key issues that could pose risks during the World Cup:

1. **Surveillance and Security Measures**: The organization warns that heightened security protocols could lead to invasive surveillance practices. The potential for excessive policing and monitoring of fans, particularly those from minority backgrounds, raises concerns about privacy violations and discrimination.

2. **Treatment of Vulnerable Populations**: There is a significant risk that marginalized groups, including homeless individuals and undocumented immigrants, may face increased scrutiny and displacement during the event. Amnesty stresses the importance of ensuring that all individuals, regardless of their status, are treated with dignity and respect.

3. **Freedom of Expression**: The organization has called for assurances that fans will be able to express their views freely, particularly regarding social and political issues. Past sporting events have seen crackdowns on protests and dissent, and Amnesty is urging authorities to uphold the right to peaceful assembly and expression.

### Call to Action

Amnesty International is calling on the U.S. government, along with local authorities in host cities, to take proactive steps to mitigate these risks. This includes engaging with civil society organizations to develop comprehensive human rights policies that protect all attendees. The organization also advocates for transparency in security measures and the establishment of mechanisms for reporting and addressing human rights violations.

In light of the global attention that the World Cup attracts, Amnesty International believes that the event presents a unique opportunity for the United States to showcase its commitment to human rights. By prioritizing the safety and dignity of all participants, the U.S. can set a precedent for future international sporting events.

### Conclusion

As the countdown to the 2026 World Cup continues, the concerns raised by Amnesty International serve as a crucial reminder of the importance of safeguarding human rights in the context of large-scale events. With millions of fans expected to attend, ensuring their safety and rights must be a top priority for organizers and authorities alike. The world will be watching, and the actions taken in the lead-up to the tournament will have lasting implications for human rights in the host nation.
