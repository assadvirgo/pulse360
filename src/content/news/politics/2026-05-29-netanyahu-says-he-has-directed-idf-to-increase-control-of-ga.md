## Netanyahu Directs IDF to Increase Control of Gaza to 70%

In a significant escalation of military strategy, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced on Thursday that he has directed the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) to expand their control over the Gaza Strip to 70%. This directive marks a pivotal shift in Israel's approach to the ongoing conflict with Hamas.

### Context of the Directive

The announcement comes amid a backdrop of heightened tensions in the region. The Israeli government and Hamas had previously reached a ceasefire agreement in October 2025, which outlined specific terms for the cessation of hostilities. Netanyahu's latest decision raises questions about the future of this ceasefire and the potential for renewed violence.

### Implications for the Ceasefire Agreement

The proposed increase in military control is likely to contradict the terms of the ceasefire, which aimed to establish a framework for peace and stability in the region. Analysts suggest that this move could lead to further escalation, undermining the fragile peace that had been brokered between the two sides.

The ceasefire agreement was seen as a crucial step towards de-escalating the conflict that has plagued the region for years. By increasing military control, Netanyahu's administration may be signaling a departure from diplomatic efforts in favor of a more aggressive military stance.

### Reactions from Political Leaders

Political leaders within Israel and the international community are likely to respond to this announcement with concern. Critics of Netanyahu's government have previously warned that military escalation could lead to significant humanitarian crises in Gaza, further complicating the already dire situation for civilians caught in the crossfire.

International observers are keeping a close eye on the developments, as any significant military action could provoke reactions from neighboring countries and global powers. The potential for increased violence raises concerns about the stability of the region and the impact on ongoing peace negotiations.

### The Humanitarian Perspective

Humanitarian organizations have long expressed alarm over the conditions in Gaza, where a significant portion of the population relies on aid for survival. The expansion of military control could exacerbate the humanitarian situation, leading to increased suffering for civilians. 

As the IDF prepares to implement this directive, the international community is urged to advocate for the protection of civilians and the adherence to international humanitarian law. The ramifications of this decision could extend beyond the immediate conflict, affecting regional stability and international relations.

### Conclusion

Netanyahu's directive to increase IDF control over Gaza to 70% marks a critical juncture in the ongoing Israeli-Palestinian conflict. As the situation develops, the implications for both the ceasefire agreement and the humanitarian conditions on the ground will be closely monitored. The international community's response will play a crucial role in shaping the future of peace efforts in the region.
