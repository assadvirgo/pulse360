## Thousands Detained by RSF in Sudan's El-Fasher, Reports NGO

In a troubling development from Sudan, the Sudan Doctors Network has reported that thousands of individuals, including hundreds of women and children, have been detained by the paramilitary group known as the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) in the city of El-Fasher. This alarming situation has raised significant concerns regarding human rights and the safety of civilians amid ongoing conflicts in the region.

### Context of the Conflict

The RSF, which emerged from the Janjaweed militias that were involved in the Darfur conflict, has been a central figure in Sudan's complex political landscape. Following the ousting of former President Omar al-Bashir in 2019, the RSF has played a pivotal role in the power struggles that have ensued, often engaging in violent confrontations with various factions. The current situation in El-Fasher highlights the group's continued influence and the precarious security environment in Sudan.

### Details of the Detentions

According to the Sudan Doctors Network, the detentions in El-Fasher are part of a broader pattern of repression and violence perpetrated by the RSF. The NGO has emphasized that the majority of those detained are civilians, with a significant number being women and children. Eyewitness accounts suggest that the RSF has been conducting raids in various neighborhoods, leading to mass arrests and widespread fear among the local population.

The exact reasons for the detentions remain unclear, but there are indications that the RSF is targeting individuals suspected of opposing their authority or those affiliated with rival political groups. The situation has prompted calls for urgent international attention and intervention to safeguard the rights of those affected.

### Humanitarian Concerns

The ongoing detentions have exacerbated an already dire humanitarian situation in Sudan, where access to basic services and security has been severely compromised. Humanitarian organizations have reported that the conflict has led to widespread displacement and a critical shortage of food, water, and medical supplies. The detention of civilians further complicates efforts to provide aid and support to those in need.

Local health facilities are reportedly overwhelmed, with many unable to cope with the influx of patients, particularly vulnerable groups such as women and children. The Sudan Doctors Network has called for immediate action to protect civilians and ensure that those detained are treated humanely and in accordance with international law.

### International Response

The international community has been closely monitoring the situation in Sudan, with various human rights organizations urging governments and international bodies to take a stand against the actions of the RSF. The United Nations and African Union have been called upon to investigate the reported human rights abuses and to hold accountable those responsible for the detentions.

As the situation in El-Fasher continues to unfold, the need for a coordinated response to address the humanitarian crisis and protect the rights of civilians has become increasingly urgent. The international community's engagement will be crucial in determining the future stability of Sudan and the safety of its citizens.

### Conclusion

The reported detentions by the RSF in El-Fasher serve as a stark reminder of the ongoing challenges facing Sudan as it navigates a turbulent political landscape. With thousands of civilians caught in the crossfire, the need for immediate action and support from the international community is paramount to prevent further deterioration of the situation and to uphold human rights in the region.
