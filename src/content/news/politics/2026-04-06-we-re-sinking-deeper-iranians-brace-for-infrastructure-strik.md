## Iranians Brace for Potential Infrastructure Strikes Amid U.S. Threats

As tensions escalate between Iran and the United States, ordinary Iranians are expressing growing concern over the potential for military strikes targeting critical infrastructure. This anxiety is heightened by recent statements from U.S. President Donald Trump, who has threatened to destroy Iran's power plants and bridges unless the country agrees to open the strategically vital Strait of Hormuz.

### Context of the Threats

The Strait of Hormuz is a narrow passage that connects the Persian Gulf to the Arabian Sea, and it is a crucial route for global oil shipments. Approximately 20% of the world's oil passes through this strait, making it a focal point of international trade and energy security. The U.S. has long sought to maintain open navigation through this waterway, and any perceived threat to its security could prompt significant military responses.

President Trump's recent ultimatum has raised alarms within Iran, where citizens are already grappling with economic hardships exacerbated by U.S. sanctions. The prospect of strikes on key infrastructure—such as power plants and bridges—could further destabilize the nation, leading to widespread disruption in daily life and economic activity.

### Public Sentiment

In response to the escalating rhetoric, many Iranians are voicing their fears about the implications of such military actions. "We're sinking deeper," remarked one Tehran resident, highlighting the pervasive sense of vulnerability among the population. The prospect of losing essential services like electricity and transportation has intensified worries about the future.

Experts suggest that the Iranian government may be compelled to react assertively to any military threats, potentially leading to a cycle of escalation. The Iranian leadership has historically positioned itself as a defender of national sovereignty, and any attack on infrastructure could be framed as an act of aggression that warrants a robust response.

### International Reactions

The international community is closely monitoring the situation, with various nations urging both sides to de-escalate tensions. Diplomatic channels remain open, but the rhetoric from Washington has complicated efforts to find a peaceful resolution. Analysts believe that continued threats could push Iran into a corner, prompting it to take actions that may further inflame regional tensions.

### Conclusion

As the deadline set by President Trump approaches, the situation remains fluid and precarious. Ordinary Iranians are left to navigate an uncertain landscape filled with fear and anxiety over potential military strikes. The possibility of targeted attacks on critical infrastructure could have far-reaching consequences, not only for Iran but also for global stability. The coming days will be crucial in determining whether diplomacy can prevail over confrontation in this high-stakes geopolitical standoff.
