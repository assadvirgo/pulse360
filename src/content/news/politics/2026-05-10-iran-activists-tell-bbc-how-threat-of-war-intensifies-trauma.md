## Activists in Iran Express Psychological Toll Amid Threat of War

As tensions escalate in the Middle East, Iranian activists are voicing their concerns about the psychological impact of both internal repression and the looming threat of war. In a recent interview with the BBC, a dissident based in Tehran shared her experiences of feeling helpless and overwhelmed by immense psychological pressure.

## The Dual Burden of Repression and Conflict

Iran has long been a focal point of geopolitical tensions, particularly due to its nuclear program and its role in regional conflicts. The potential for military confrontation adds another layer of stress for those already living under a repressive regime. The dissident, who spoke on the condition of anonymity for her safety, described a pervasive sense of anxiety that permeates daily life in Iran. She articulated how the fear of war compounds the trauma experienced by individuals who are already facing government crackdowns on dissent.

Activists in Iran have faced increasing repression in recent years, with the government employing various tactics to silence opposition voices. This includes arbitrary arrests, censorship, and violent crackdowns on protests. The combination of these internal pressures and external threats creates a unique and challenging environment for those advocating for change.

## The Psychological Impact of Ongoing Repression

The dissident highlighted the psychological toll that living under such conditions can take on individuals and communities. Many Iranians are grappling with feelings of despair and helplessness as they navigate a landscape marked by both political oppression and the specter of conflict. The constant fear of government reprisals, coupled with the anxiety of potential military action, leads to a profound sense of instability.

Experts in psychology emphasize that prolonged exposure to such stressors can result in significant mental health issues, including anxiety disorders and depression. The dissident's account reflects a broader sentiment among activists who feel trapped in a cycle of fear and repression, exacerbated by the geopolitical climate.

## Calls for International Attention

Activists are urging the international community to pay closer attention to the situation in Iran. They argue that global awareness and pressure could help alleviate some of the internal repression and provide a platform for their voices to be heard. The dissident expressed hope that international solidarity could lead to meaningful change, although she remains realistic about the challenges ahead.

As the situation evolves, it remains crucial for the global community to recognize the interconnectedness of internal repression and external threats. The experiences of Iranian activists serve as a reminder of the human cost associated with political conflict and the importance of supporting those who strive for freedom and justice in their homeland.

## Conclusion

The psychological burden faced by Iranian dissidents is a poignant reminder of the complexities surrounding political repression and the threat of war. As tensions continue to rise, the voices of those living under such conditions must be amplified, highlighting the urgent need for both national and international efforts to address their plight. The path forward remains fraught with challenges, but the resilience of activists in Iran offers a glimmer of hope for a more just future.
