## Yemen’s Landmine Crisis Endures Despite Truce and De-mining Efforts

Yemen continues to grapple with a severe landmine crisis, which persists even in the face of recent ceasefire agreements and ongoing de-mining initiatives. The humanitarian implications of this situation remain dire, as civilians are disproportionately affected by the remnants of conflict that continue to plague the nation.

### Background of the Crisis

Since the onset of the Yemen conflict in 2015, landmines have become a significant threat to civilian safety. Various factions involved in the war have resorted to laying mines in strategic locations, leading to widespread contamination of land across the country. The International Campaign to Ban Landmines (ICBL) has highlighted Yemen as one of the most heavily mined countries in the world, with thousands of casualties reported since the conflict began.

### Impact on Civilians

Despite efforts to negotiate peace and implement ceasefires, the presence of landmines has resulted in ongoing fatalities and injuries among the civilian population. Reports indicate that children are particularly vulnerable, as they often unknowingly encounter these hidden dangers while playing or walking to school. The World Health Organization (WHO) has documented numerous cases of injuries, many of which require extensive medical treatment and rehabilitation.

### De-mining Initiatives

In response to the escalating crisis, several organizations, including the United Nations and various non-governmental organizations (NGOs), have launched de-mining operations aimed at clearing affected areas. These initiatives involve training local teams to identify and safely remove landmines, as well as raising awareness about the dangers of unexploded ordnance. However, the scale of the problem is daunting, and progress has been slow due to ongoing conflict and limited resources.

### Challenges to Progress

The de-mining efforts face significant challenges, including security concerns and logistical difficulties in accessing contaminated areas. The fluctuating nature of the conflict often hampers operations, as teams must navigate active combat zones to reach sites in need of clearance. Additionally, funding for de-mining projects remains inconsistent, further complicating efforts to address the landmine crisis comprehensively.

### International Response

The international community has expressed concern over the humanitarian impact of landmines in Yemen. Various countries and organizations have pledged support for de-mining initiatives, yet the urgency of the situation calls for a more coordinated and sustained response. Advocacy groups continue to pressure governments to prioritize funding and resources for de-mining efforts, emphasizing the need for a long-term strategy to ensure the safety of civilians.

### Conclusion

As Yemen strives for peace amidst a protracted conflict, the landmine crisis serves as a stark reminder of the lasting consequences of war. While ceasefire agreements and de-mining efforts offer a glimmer of hope, the reality remains that many Yemenis continue to live in fear of landmines. A concerted international effort is essential to address this pressing humanitarian issue and to pave the way for a safer future for the people of Yemen.
