## 'Ketamine Queen' Sentenced to 15 Years in Connection with Matthew Perry's Overdose Death

In a significant legal development, Jasveen Sangha, infamously dubbed the "Ketamine Queen," has been sentenced to 15 years in prison following her conviction for selling drugs that contributed to the overdose death of actor Matthew Perry. Perry, best known for his role as Chandler Bing on the iconic television series "Friends," had openly battled addiction for many years, making his untimely death a poignant reminder of the struggles faced by many individuals dealing with substance abuse.

### Background of the Case

Matthew Perry was found unresponsive in his Los Angeles home in October 2023, and subsequent investigations revealed that his death was linked to a combination of substances, including ketamine, which is known for its use as an anesthetic and, more recently, as a treatment for depression. The investigation quickly turned its focus on Sangha, who was accused of distributing the drug illegally.

Throughout the trial, prosecutors presented evidence that Sangha had been operating a drug distribution network that targeted vulnerable individuals struggling with addiction. Testimonies from several witnesses highlighted the destructive impact of her actions, emphasizing how her sales contributed to the rise in substance abuse and overdose incidents in the community.

### Legal Proceedings and Sentencing

Sangha's trial garnered significant media attention, not only due to Perry's celebrity status but also because it underscored the ongoing crisis of addiction in the United States. The jury ultimately found her guilty on multiple counts, including drug trafficking and manslaughter. During the sentencing, the judge remarked on the gravity of her actions, stating that they had not only led to Perry's death but had also perpetuated a cycle of addiction and despair among many others.

In addition to the 15-year prison sentence, the court mandated that Sangha undergo rehabilitation programs while incarcerated, aiming to address the underlying issues that contribute to drug trafficking and addiction.

### The Broader Context of Addiction

Perry's death has reignited discussions about the opioid epidemic and the broader challenges of addiction in American society. Many advocates are calling for increased awareness and resources for addiction treatment, as well as stricter regulations on the distribution of controlled substances. The case against Sangha serves as a stark reminder of the consequences of illegal drug sales and the need for comprehensive strategies to combat substance abuse.

### Conclusion

As the legal repercussions of this case unfold, it remains to be seen how it will influence public policy and perceptions surrounding addiction and drug trafficking. The tragic loss of Matthew Perry has not only left a void in the entertainment industry but has also highlighted the urgent need for a more compassionate and effective approach to addressing addiction in all its forms.
