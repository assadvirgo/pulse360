## Activist Keba Seba Arrested in South Africa Amid Controversy Over Benin Coup Support

In a significant development, Keba Seba, a prominent activist known for his vocal opposition to French influence in Africa, was arrested in South Africa. His detention comes in the wake of allegations regarding his support for a coup plot in Benin, a country that has been grappling with political instability in recent years.

### Background on Keba Seba

Keba Seba has emerged as a notable figure in African political discourse, particularly for his criticism of neo-colonialism and foreign intervention on the continent. His activism has often aligned him with military leaders in West Africa who have sought to assert greater autonomy from Western powers, particularly France. Seba's rhetoric has resonated with many who share concerns about the lingering impacts of colonialism and the influence of foreign nations in African affairs.

### The Allegations

The arrest of Seba is tied to his alleged involvement in supporting a coup plot in Benin, where political tensions have escalated. The West African nation has faced significant challenges, including accusations of authoritarian governance and crackdowns on dissent. The current government, led by President Patrice Talon, has been criticized for its handling of opposition voices and political freedoms.

While specific details regarding the charges against Seba remain unclear, his arrest has raised questions about the broader implications for political dissent in the region.
