## Iraq to Summon US and Iran Envoys Amid Rising Tensions

The Iraqi government has announced its intention to summon the ambassadors of the United States and Iran in response to a series of recent attacks that have raised concerns over regional stability. This decision comes as Iraq seeks to address the implications of the ongoing conflict between the US and Iran, particularly in light of the escalating violence linked to the US-Israeli operations against Iranian interests.

## Formal Notes of Protest

According to a statement from the Prime Minister's office, Iraq plans to deliver "formal notes of protest" to both the US and Iranian envoys. The Iraqi government expressed its deep concern regarding the impact of these strikes on its sovereignty and the safety of its citizens. The Prime Minister's office emphasized that Iraq does not wish to be a battleground for external conflicts and is committed to maintaining its neutrality in regional disputes.

## Context of the Attacks

The recent attacks have been attributed to the ongoing tensions stemming from the US-Israeli military operations targeting Iranian assets in the region. These operations have intensified in recent months, leading to retaliatory strikes and a cycle of violence that threatens to destabilize Iraq, which has already been grappling with its own internal challenges.

Iraq has historically been a focal point for various regional powers, and the current situation underscores the delicate balance the government must maintain in its foreign relations. The involvement of external actors in Iraqi affairs has often led to heightened tensions and violence, prompting calls for a more assertive stance from Baghdad.

## Regional Implications

The summoning of the US and Iranian ambassadors is indicative of Iraq's attempt to assert its sovereignty and protect its national interests. Analysts suggest that this move could serve as a diplomatic signal to both nations, urging them to reconsider their military strategies in the region. Iraq's leadership is aware that continued hostilities could exacerbate the existing security challenges, including the resurgence of extremist groups and the potential for civil unrest.

Furthermore, the Iraqi government is likely to face pressure from various domestic factions, some of which may support closer ties with Iran, while others advocate for a stronger relationship with the US. This internal division complicates Iraq's foreign policy decisions and highlights the challenges of navigating a path that satisfies all stakeholders.

## Conclusion

As Iraq prepares to engage diplomatically with both the US and Iran, the international community will be watching closely. The outcome of these discussions could have significant ramifications for the region's stability and Iraq's role within it. The Iraqi government's commitment to addressing these issues through formal diplomatic channels reflects its desire to maintain peace and security in a tumultuous geopolitical landscape.
