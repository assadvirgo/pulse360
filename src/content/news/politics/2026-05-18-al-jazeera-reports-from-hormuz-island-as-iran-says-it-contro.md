### Background on the Strait of Hormuz

The Strait of Hormuz is a narrow passage that connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman. It is one of the most critical maritime chokepoints globally, with approximately 20% of the world's oil trade passing through it. The strait has been a focal point of tension between Iran and Western nations, particularly the United States, due to its strategic importance and the potential for military confrontations.

### Iran's Assertion of Control

Iranian authorities have reiterated their stance on controlling the Strait of Hormuz, emphasizing their readiness to respond to any threats to their sovereignty. This assertion is part of a broader narrative that seeks to reinforce Iran's position in regional politics, particularly in light of ongoing tensions with the U.S. and its allies. The Iranian government has previously threatened to close the strait in response to perceived provocations, a move that could have dire implications for global oil markets.
