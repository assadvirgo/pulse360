## Iranian Official Declares End to Negotiation Policy Amid Rising Tensions

In a significant escalation of rhetoric, Mohsen Rezaee, a senior Iranian official, has announced that Iran's policy of engaging in negotiations while under military pressure is no longer viable. This statement comes in the context of ongoing tensions between Iran and the United States, particularly concerning military actions that have raised concerns about a potential conflict.

Rezaee, who serves as the secretary of Iran's Expediency Council, emphasized that continued U.S. bombings against Iranian interests would compel Tehran to respond with greater military force. "If the U.S. continues its aggression, we will escalate our response into a full-scale war," he warned, signaling a shift in Iran's approach to foreign policy amid what it perceives as existential threats.

### Context of the Statement

The remarks from Rezaee reflect a broader sentiment within the Iranian leadership that has increasingly viewed U.S. military actions as provocations rather than isolated incidents. The history of U.S.-Iran relations has been fraught with tension, particularly since the U.S. withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018, which was intended to limit Iran's nuclear program in exchange for sanctions relief. Since then, Iran has gradually reduced its compliance with the agreement, leading to heightened scrutiny and military posturing from both sides.

The current situation is further complicated by regional dynamics, including Iran's support for various militant groups in the Middle East and its contentious relationships with neighboring countries. The U.S. has consistently accused Iran of destabilizing activities, while Tehran maintains that it is acting in defense of its sovereignty.

### Implications for Regional Stability

Rezaee's statements raise concerns about the potential for escalation in an already volatile region. Analysts warn that a full-scale war between the U.S. and Iran could have devastating consequences not only for both nations but also for the broader Middle East. The implications of such a conflict could lead to widespread humanitarian crises, disruptions in global oil supplies, and increased military engagements involving other regional powers.

The international community is closely monitoring these developments, as diplomatic efforts to de-escalate tensions have so far yielded limited results. The Biden administration has expressed a desire to return to negotiations regarding Iran's nuclear program, but the recent statements from Iranian officials suggest that Tehran may be less inclined to engage in talks under the current circumstances.

### Conclusion

As the situation continues to evolve, the focus will remain on how both Iran and the U.S. navigate this critical juncture. Rezaee's declaration marks a pivotal moment in Iranian foreign policy, signaling a potential shift towards a more aggressive posture in response to perceived threats. The coming weeks and months will be crucial in determining whether diplomacy can prevail or if the region is on the brink of a larger conflict.
