## Millions in War-Ravaged Sudan Surviving on One Meal a Day, Say NGOs

As the conflict in Sudan continues to escalate, humanitarian organizations report that millions of people are facing dire food shortages, with many surviving on just one meal a day. The situation is particularly severe in the North Darfur and South Kordofan states, where residents are resorting to extreme measures to stave off hunger.

### Escalating Humanitarian Crisis

The ongoing violence and instability in Sudan have created a humanitarian crisis that has left millions in urgent need of assistance. According to various non-governmental organizations (NGOs) operating in the region, the conflict has disrupted agricultural activities, leading to significant food shortages. The United Nations has warned that the situation is deteriorating rapidly, with many families unable to access basic food supplies.

In North Darfur and South Kordofan, reports indicate that individuals are turning to unconventional and often unsafe sources of sustenance. Many are eating leaves and even animal feed in a desperate attempt to survive. This alarming trend underscores the severity of the food insecurity that has gripped these regions, where the effects of conflict have compounded existing vulnerabilities.

### The Impact of Conflict on Food Security

The conflict in Sudan, which has been ongoing for years, has had a devastating impact on the agricultural sector. Farmers have been displaced from their land, and those who remain often face violence and intimidation, making it nearly impossible to cultivate crops or tend to livestock. As a result, food production has plummeted, and prices for basic commodities have skyrocketed, further exacerbating the crisis.

The situation is compounded by economic instability and inflation, which have made it increasingly difficult for families to afford even the most basic food items. Many households are forced to choose between purchasing food and meeting other essential needs, such as healthcare and education.

### NGO Efforts and International Response

In response to the escalating crisis, NGOs are working tirelessly to provide emergency food assistance and support to affected communities. However, their efforts are hampered by ongoing violence and insecurity, which complicates the delivery of aid. Access to remote areas remains a significant challenge, and many organizations are calling for increased international support to address the urgent needs of the population.

The international community has been urged to take action to alleviate the suffering in Sudan. Humanitarian agencies are advocating for a coordinated response that includes increased funding for food assistance, as well as efforts to promote peace and stability in the region. Without immediate intervention, the situation is expected to worsen, with millions more at risk of starvation.

### Conclusion

The plight of millions of people in Sudan, particularly in North Darfur and South Kordofan, highlights the urgent need for a comprehensive response to the ongoing humanitarian crisis. As families struggle to survive on one meal a day and resort to eating leaves and animal feed, the international community must act swiftly to provide the necessary support and resources to alleviate their suffering. The future of countless lives hangs in the balance as the conflict continues to disrupt the basic human right to food and security.
