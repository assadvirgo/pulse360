## New EU Border System Expected to Triple Wait Times at Passport Control

The implementation of a new border control system across the European Union is anticipated to significantly increase the time travelers spend at passport control. According to airport officials, this change could lead to wait times tripling, a concern that has been echoed by airline operators, including Ryanair.

### Overview of the New Border System

The European Union is rolling out a new border management system aimed at enhancing security and streamlining the processing of travelers entering and exiting member states. This initiative, part of the EU’s broader strategy to modernize its border controls, involves the integration of advanced technology and data-sharing mechanisms. However, the transition to this new system is expected to create initial bottlenecks as airports adapt to the changes.

### Impact on Travelers

Airport executives have voiced their concerns regarding the anticipated delays. The head of a major airport expressed that the new system could lead to wait times at passport control increasing from the current average of around 30 minutes to as much as 90 minutes during peak travel periods. This projection is particularly alarming for travelers planning to visit Europe this summer, as it coincides with the busy holiday season.

Ryanair, one of Europe’s largest low-cost airlines, has also issued warnings to its passengers. The airline has advised travelers to prepare for extended waits at airports due to the new border system. In a statement, Ryanair emphasized the importance of arriving at the airport earlier than usual to accommodate the potential delays at passport control.

### Government and Airport Responses

In response to the growing concerns, EU officials have acknowledged the challenges posed by the new system. They have assured the public that measures are being put in place to mitigate the impact on travelers. This includes increasing staffing levels at passport control and enhancing communication with airlines to ensure that passengers are well-informed about the changes.

Airports are also investing in infrastructure improvements to facilitate smoother processing of travelers. This includes upgrading technology and optimizing the flow of passengers through security and immigration checkpoints.

### Conclusion

As the EU prepares to implement its new border management system, travelers should be aware of the potential for longer wait times at passport control. While the initiative aims to bolster security and improve efficiency in the long run, the immediate effects may pose challenges for holidaymakers and business travelers alike. It is advisable for passengers to plan ahead, allowing extra time for their journeys to avoid the stress of unexpected delays. 

The situation will continue to evolve as the EU and individual member states work to balance security needs with the convenience of travelers.
