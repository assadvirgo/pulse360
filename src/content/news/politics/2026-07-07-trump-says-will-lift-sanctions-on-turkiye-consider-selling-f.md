## Trump Proposes Lifting Sanctions on Turkiye and Considering F-35 Sales

In a significant development during a recent meeting in Ankara, former U.S. President Donald Trump announced his intention to lift sanctions imposed on Turkiye and expressed a willingness to consider the sale of F-35 fighter jets to the country. This announcement marks a potential shift in U.S. foreign policy towards Turkiye, which has been a focal point of tension in recent years.

### Background of U.S.-Turkiye Relations

The relationship between the United States and Turkiye has been complicated, particularly following Turkiye's acquisition of the Russian S-400 missile defense system. This decision led to the imposition of sanctions by the U.S. under the Countering America's Adversaries Through Sanctions Act (CAATSA) and resulted in Turkiye's removal from the F-35 program. The F-35 program, which is a joint initiative involving multiple NATO allies, has been a critical component of U.S. defense sales and international military cooperation.

### Trump’s Announcement

During his visit to Ankara, Trump emphasized the importance of strengthening ties with Turkiye, a NATO ally that has played a significant role in regional security. He stated that lifting the sanctions would foster a more cooperative relationship and enhance military collaboration between the two nations. Furthermore, Trump indicated that he would "consider" the possibility of resuming F-35 sales, a move that could significantly bolster Turkiye's air capabilities.

### Implications for U.S. Foreign Policy

Trump's statements have raised eyebrows among various stakeholders, particularly in Israel, which has expressed concerns over the potential sale of advanced military technology to Turkiye. The Israeli government has historically viewed Turkiye's military capabilities with caution, especially given the geopolitical dynamics in the region.

The proposal to lift sanctions and consider F-35 sales could signal a broader shift in U.S. foreign policy, moving away from punitive measures and towards engagement. This approach aligns with Trump's broader "America First" strategy, which emphasizes bilateral relationships and national interests.

### Reactions from the International Community

The announcement has elicited mixed reactions from the international community. Supporters argue that improved U.S.-Turkiye relations could lead to greater stability in the region, particularly in the context of ongoing conflicts in Syria and Iraq. Critics, however, warn that such a move could embolden Turkiye's more aggressive foreign policy actions, particularly in the Eastern Mediterranean.

### Conclusion

As the geopolitical landscape continues to evolve, the implications of Trump's announcement will be closely monitored. The potential lifting of sanctions and the consideration of F-35 sales to Turkiye represent a pivotal moment in U.S. foreign relations, with the potential to reshape alliances and influence regional security dynamics. Moving forward, it will be essential for policymakers to balance national interests with the broader implications for international stability.
