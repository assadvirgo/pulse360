## Greece to Implement Social Media Ban for Under-15s

In a significant move aimed at protecting the well-being of children, the Greek government has announced plans to ban individuals under the age of 15 from accessing social media platforms starting next year. This decision comes amid growing concerns regarding the impact of social media on the mental health and safety of young users.

### Rationale Behind the Ban

The Greek authorities have cited various studies linking social media usage among minors to issues such as anxiety, depression, and cyberbullying. The decision reflects a broader trend among governments worldwide to regulate online environments to better safeguard children. With social media becoming increasingly integral to daily life, the risks associated with its use have prompted many countries to reconsider their policies regarding minors.

The Greek Minister of Digital Governance, Kyriakos Pierrakakis, emphasized the need for protective measures, stating, "Our priority is to ensure that children grow up in a safe digital environment. This ban is a necessary step to shield our youth from the potential harms of social media."

### Implementation Details

The ban is set to take effect in January 2024, and it will apply to all major social media platforms. While specific enforcement mechanisms have yet to be detailed, the government has indicated that it will work closely with social media companies to ensure compliance. This may involve age verification processes to prevent underage users from creating accounts.

In addition to the age restriction, the government plans to launch educational campaigns aimed at both parents and children. These initiatives will focus on promoting digital literacy and encouraging safe online practices. The government believes that empowering families with knowledge about the digital landscape will complement the new regulations.

### Reactions from Stakeholders

The announcement has elicited a range of responses from various stakeholders. Child psychologists and educators have largely welcomed the decision, arguing that it is a necessary measure to protect vulnerable youth. They stress the importance of fostering healthy online habits and providing children with the tools to navigate digital spaces responsibly.

Conversely, some critics have raised concerns about the potential implications for freedom of expression and the ability of young people to communicate and connect with peers. Advocates for digital rights argue that outright bans may not be the most effective solution and suggest that education and parental guidance could be more beneficial in the long term.

### Comparative Context

Greece's move aligns with similar actions taken by other nations. Countries such as France and Italy have implemented regulations aimed at minimizing the risks associated with social media for minors. These measures often focus on age verification and parental consent, reflecting a growing recognition of the need to balance online engagement with safety.

### Conclusion

As Greece prepares to implement this groundbreaking ban on social media for individuals under 15, the implications of this policy will be closely monitored both domestically and internationally. The effectiveness of such measures in enhancing child safety online remains to be seen, but the initiative underscores a significant shift in how governments are approaching the challenges posed by digital platforms in the lives of young people.
