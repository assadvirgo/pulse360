The charges stem from his service in Afghanistan, where he is accused of committing unlawful killings during his deployment.

In a statement released to the media, he expressed his intention to vigorously contest the charges, asserting his innocence and commitment to defending his reputation. He emphasized that he has always acted in accordance with the rules of engagement and the laws of war during his military service.

The charges against Roberts-Smith have sparked significant public interest and debate in Australia, particularly given his status as a national hero. He is a recipient of the Victoria Cross for Australia, the country's highest military honor, awarded for his bravery in combat.
