## Who is Calling the Shots in Iran Amid the Deadly War with the US?

As tensions escalate between Iran and the United States, the role of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) has come under scrutiny. The IRGC, a branch of Iran's armed forces, has increasingly positioned itself as a dominant force in the country, particularly in strategic regions such as the Strait of Hormuz. This development raises critical questions about the balance of power within Iran and the implications for its foreign policy.

### The Role of the IRGC

The IRGC was established after the 1979 Iranian Revolution to protect the Islamic Republic and its values. Over the years, it has expanded its influence significantly, not only within the military sphere but also in politics and the economy. The recent escalation of conflict with the United States has further solidified the IRGC's role as a key player in Iran's strategic decision-making.

Reports indicate that the IRGC has taken a leading role in military operations and strategic planning, particularly concerning the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments. The IRGC's control over this region allows it to exert significant pressure on international shipping and energy markets, which is a vital leverage point in the ongoing conflict.

### Government Support

Despite the IRGC's growing authority, the Iranian government has publicly expressed its alignment with the military's actions. Officials have stated that they support the IRGC's initiatives, indicating a unified front in the face of external threats. This collaboration suggests that the Iranian government may be relying on the IRGC's military expertise to navigate the complexities of the current geopolitical landscape.

However, this partnership raises concerns about the extent to which civilian leadership can influence military decisions. The IRGC's increasing autonomy could lead to a scenario where military considerations overshadow diplomatic efforts, potentially escalating conflicts further.

### Implications for Foreign Relations

The IRGC's prominent role in Iran's military strategy has significant implications for its foreign relations, particularly with the United States and its allies. As the IRGC engages in aggressive posturing and military readiness, it may provoke a stronger response from the U.S., complicating any potential avenues for diplomacy.

Additionally, the IRGC's influence extends beyond Iran's borders, as it has been involved in supporting allied groups in the region, including militias in Iraq and Hezbollah in Lebanon. This network of alliances could further entrench Iran's position in regional conflicts, making it a more formidable opponent for the U.S. and its allies.

### Conclusion

As the situation in Iran continues to evolve, the balance of power between the IRGC and the Iranian government will be crucial in determining the country's response to external threats. The IRGC's leadership in military affairs, particularly in strategic areas like the Strait of Hormuz, underscores its significant role in shaping Iran's foreign policy. The alignment between the IRGC and the government may provide a unified approach to dealing with the U.S., but it also raises questions about the future of civilian oversight in military matters. As tensions rise, the international community will be closely monitoring developments in Iran, seeking to understand the implications for regional stability and global security.
