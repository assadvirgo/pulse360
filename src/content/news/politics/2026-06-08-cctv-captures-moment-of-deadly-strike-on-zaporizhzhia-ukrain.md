## Deadly Missile Strike in Zaporizhzhia Captured on CCTV

In a tragic incident that underscores the ongoing conflict in Ukraine, CCTV footage has emerged showing the moment a missile struck the city of Zaporizhzhia. The attack, which occurred in the early hours of the morning, resulted in the deaths of two individuals and left 23 others injured.

The missile strike took place in a residential area, causing significant damage to buildings and infrastructure. Eyewitness accounts describe a loud explosion that shook the ground and shattered windows in the vicinity. Emergency services were promptly dispatched to the scene, where they worked to rescue those trapped under debris and provide medical assistance to the injured.

Local authorities have confirmed that the two fatalities were civilians, and the injured include both adults and children. The severity of the injuries varies, with several individuals requiring immediate hospitalization. The attack has sparked outrage among residents, many of whom are already grappling with the ongoing repercussions of the conflict that has plagued Ukraine since 2014.

### Reactions from Officials

Ukrainian officials have condemned the missile strike, labeling it as a blatant act of aggression against civilians. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy expressed his condolences to the families of the victims and reiterated the government's commitment to ensuring the safety and security of its citizens. He called for international support to hold those responsible for such attacks accountable.

The Ukrainian military has stated that they are investigating the circumstances surrounding the strike, including the origin of the missile. While no group has claimed responsibility for the attack as of yet, the region has been a focal point of military activity, with both Ukrainian and Russian forces engaged in ongoing hostilities.

### The Broader Context

The missile strike in Zaporizhzhia is part of a larger pattern of violence that has escalated in various regions of Ukraine. Since the beginning of the full-scale invasion by Russia in February 2022, civilian areas have increasingly become targets, leading to widespread condemnation from the international community.
