## The Absence of Unscripted Press Conferences by Indian PM Modi

In recent years, Prime Minister Narendra Modi's approach to media interactions has drawn significant attention and debate. One of the most notable aspects of his communication strategy is the absence of unscripted press conferences, a practice that has become increasingly rare in his administration. This article explores the reasons behind this approach and its implications for transparency and accountability in Indian politics.

## The Controlled Narrative

Prime Minister Modi's administration has been characterized by a carefully curated media narrative. By limiting the opportunities for unscripted interactions, the government can control the messaging and avoid potential pitfalls that may arise from spontaneous questioning. This strategy is not unique to Modi; many political leaders worldwide adopt similar tactics to manage their public image and maintain a favorable narrative.

Critics argue that this controlled approach undermines the democratic process. Unscripted press conferences allow for a more interactive dialogue between leaders and the media, fostering transparency and accountability. By avoiding such engagements, Modi's government may be missing an opportunity to address pressing issues directly and engage with citizens' concerns.

## The Role of Social Media

Another factor contributing to the absence of unscripted press conferences is the rise of social media as a primary communication tool. Modi has effectively utilized platforms like Twitter and Facebook to reach the public directly, bypassing traditional media channels. This strategy allows him to present his views without the risk of misinterpretation or challenging questions from journalists.

While social media offers a direct line of communication, it also raises concerns about the lack of accountability. Without the scrutiny of a live press conference, statements made on these platforms can go unchallenged, leading to a potential disconnect between the government and the public.

## Political Strategy and Image Management

Political analysts suggest that Modi's approach is also a strategic decision aimed at preserving his image as a strong and decisive leader. Unscripted press conferences can expose leaders to criticism and scrutiny, which may not align with the carefully crafted image that Modi's administration seeks to project. By avoiding these interactions, Modi can maintain a sense of control over his public persona.

Moreover, the frequency of unscripted press conferences has diminished not only in India but globally, as leaders adapt to a changing media landscape. The focus has shifted toward more controlled environments, such as pre-arranged interviews or scripted addresses, where the risk of challenging questions is minimized.

## Implications for Democracy

The absence of unscripted press conferences raises important questions about the state of democracy in India. A vibrant democracy thrives on open dialogue and accountability, and the reluctance to engage with the media in an unscripted format can hinder this process. It is essential for leaders to be held accountable for their policies and decisions, and unscripted interactions with the press are a vital component of this accountability.

As India continues to navigate complex political challenges, the need for transparent communication becomes increasingly crucial. While Modi's media strategy may serve short-term political goals, the long-term implications for democratic engagement and public trust remain to be seen.

In conclusion, the absence of unscripted press conferences by Prime Minister Modi reflects a broader trend in political communication. While it may offer immediate benefits in terms of image management, it also poses significant challenges to transparency and accountability in governance. As the political landscape evolves, the importance of open dialogue between leaders and the media cannot be overstated.
