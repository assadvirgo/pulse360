## The Importance of the Strait of Hormuz

The Strait of Hormuz is a narrow stretch of water connecting the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman. It is one of the world's most crucial maritime chokepoints, with approximately 20% of the global oil supply passing through it. The strait is vital not only for oil exports from the Middle East but also for international shipping routes. Any disruption in this area can have significant implications for global energy markets and trade.
