## Men Disqualified from Two Oceans Marathon for Competing as Women

In a notable incident during the Two Oceans Marathon held in Cape Town, South Africa, two male runners were disqualified after competing in the women's category. The event, renowned for its challenging routes and scenic views, has drawn international attention not only for its athletic prowess but also for the integrity of its competition.

The Two Oceans Marathon, which took place recently, is one of the most prestigious running events in South Africa, attracting thousands of participants from around the globe. The marathon features various categories, including a full marathon, half marathon, and ultra-marathon, with specific divisions for men and women. 

This year, two individuals, who initially finished outside the top 10 in the women's race, were later discovered to have swapped bibs, allowing them to compete as female runners. The incident came to light when race officials conducted a routine check of the participants’ identities and bib numbers, revealing discrepancies that prompted further investigation.

The organizers of the Two Oceans Marathon have expressed their commitment to maintaining fairness and integrity in the competition.
