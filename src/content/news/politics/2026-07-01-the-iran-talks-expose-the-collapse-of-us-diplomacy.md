## The Iran Talks Expose the Collapse of US Diplomacy

The recent negotiations surrounding Iran's nuclear program have brought to light significant concerns regarding the current state of American diplomacy. Critics argue that the approach taken by the United States, particularly during the Trump administration, reflects a departure from traditional diplomatic practices, raising questions about the effectiveness and integrity of US foreign policy.

### A Shift in Diplomatic Leadership

Historically, US diplomacy has been characterized by the involvement of seasoned diplomats and foreign policy experts who navigate complex international relations. However, under the Trump administration, there has been a noticeable shift. The negotiation table has seen the presence of individuals with backgrounds in real estate and business rather than seasoned diplomats. This change has led to skepticism about the motivations and strategies employed in high-stakes negotiations, particularly those involving Iran.

### The Iran Nuclear Deal: A Case Study

The Iran nuclear deal, formally known as the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), was a landmark agreement reached in 2015 aimed at curbing Iran's nuclear ambitions in exchange for the lifting of economic sanctions. The Trump administration's decision to withdraw from the deal in 2018 marked a pivotal moment in US-Iran relations and set the stage for a series of escalating tensions.

Since the withdrawal, the Biden administration has sought to revive negotiations, yet the path forward remains fraught with challenges. The involvement of non-traditional negotiators has raised concerns about the United States' commitment to a diplomatic resolution. Critics argue that the absence of experienced diplomats has led to a lack of coherence and strategy in the ongoing talks.

### The Implications of Diplomatic Erosion

The erosion of traditional diplomatic norms has broader implications for US foreign policy. As the United States grapples with its role on the global stage, the reliance on individuals without extensive diplomatic experience could undermine its credibility and effectiveness in negotiations. This shift not only affects relations with Iran but also sends a message to other nations about the US's commitment to diplomacy as a primary tool for conflict resolution.

Moreover, the reliance on business-oriented approaches in diplomacy raises questions about the prioritization of national interests over global stability. Critics argue that such an approach may lead to short-term gains but could jeopardize long-term relationships and trust between nations.

### Looking Ahead: The Need for Experienced Diplomacy

As the United States continues to navigate the complexities of international relations, particularly in the context of Iran, there is a growing call for a return to experienced diplomacy. The need for skilled negotiators who understand the nuances of foreign policy is more pressing than ever. A diplomatic approach grounded in expertise and a commitment to multilateralism may be essential for restoring the United States' standing as a leader in global affairs.

In conclusion, the ongoing Iran talks serve as a critical reflection of the current state of US diplomacy. The departure from traditional diplomatic practices raises significant concerns about the future of American foreign policy and its ability to effectively engage with complex international issues. As the world watches, the United States faces a pivotal moment in redefining its diplomatic identity and approach.
