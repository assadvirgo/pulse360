## Driver Arrested After Chasing Down Child Cyclist in Cheney

In a concerning incident that unfolded in Cheney, Washington, a driver has been arrested following a reported chase of a young boy riding his bicycle on a neighborhood footpath. The event has raised significant alarm among local residents and authorities alike, highlighting issues of safety and community vigilance.

### Incident Overview

According to local law enforcement, the incident occurred on a residential street where the child, whose age has not been disclosed, was cycling. Witnesses reported seeing a vehicle closely following the boy as he rode, prompting fears for his safety. The driver allegedly pursued the child on foot after the boy attempted to evade the vehicle, leading to a tense situation that ultimately resulted in the driver’s arrest.

### Community Reaction

Residents of Cheney expressed shock and concern over the incident. Many took to social media to voice their apprehension about the safety of children in the area. "This is not something you expect to see in our neighborhood," one resident commented. "We always thought it was a safe place for our kids to play and ride their bikes."

Local community leaders have emphasized the importance of maintaining a safe environment for children. "We must remain vigilant and proactive in ensuring our neighborhoods are safe for all residents, particularly our youth," stated a spokesperson for the Cheney City Council.

### Law Enforcement Response

Cheney Police Department has confirmed that they are investigating the incident thoroughly. The driver, whose identity has not been released pending further investigation, faces multiple charges, including reckless endangerment and harassment. Authorities have urged anyone with additional information about the event to come forward.

In a statement, the police department reassured the community, emphasizing their commitment to public safety. "We take incidents like this very seriously. Our priority is to ensure the safety of all residents, especially children," said Police Chief Alex Johnson.
